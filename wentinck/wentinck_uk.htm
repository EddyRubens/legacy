MISSING

UPDATES!

The Wentinck detective agency has been called upon by Mr. and Mrs. van Klaveren to look for their daughter, the 24 year old Rosanne Renate van Klaveren from Zevenaar, Holland.
This woman has been missing since April 10, 1998. A neighbour has seen her walking on the gallery of her flat (Schumanstraat) that day, at about 5.30 PM. She was carrying heavy bags, probably some groceries she had just bought.

Later that day, at about 6.00 PM, Rosanne has left a message on the answering machine of her friend Jeaninne T. Nothing has been heard of her ever since.
Rosanne van Klaveren is a single mother of two sons, Samuel (4) and Robin (6). That Friday afternoon she brought her children to their grandparents in Boskoop, to stay for the weekend. Sunday afternoon her parents discovered that she was not home, as they were bringing the boys back to their mother.

There were no traces of any violence in her flat and the immediate vicinity. On the other hand, there were no traces of an intended departure either. There was a large amount of wet linnen in the washing machine, which was already smelling fouly. The milk and fresh vegetables indicated that she intended to stay home for the weekend.

The family members are puzzled and very worried. They think Rosanne van Klaveren has not disappeared out of her own free will. They fear she has been kidnapped or she has been the victim of a terrible accident. Mother Wilma van Klaveren: "I had the feeling that something wasn’t right. Rosanne usually calls to see if the boys are alright. She didn’t call this weekend, which got me worrying. When we were in Zevenaar to bring back the boys and she didn’t answer the door I got really scared. Did she have an accident, was she lying unconscious in her flat ? Those things do happen you know. We could open the door with the spare key from the housing corporation, but she was not home."

She had an appointment with her friend Jeaninne T. on Saturday, for their weekly evening watching television. Rosanne canceled the evening appointment with a message in the answering machine on Friday. This message is the last that has been heard of her: "Uuhm, hè Rosanne here, I can’t come tomorrow evening, to bad huh? Yes, humm. Well, you know, you’ll be hearing from me, alright? Bye."
Inspector P. van Veen discovered a gents slip in the washing machine. This to the amazement of the family and the friends, because they didn’t know that Rosanne had contact with the opposite sex.

It was a grey slip of medium size with a speckeled pane pattern.
 

To speed up the investigation, we want to ask the public the following questions:

  • Did you see Rosanne Renate van Klaveren after Friday April 10?
  • Do you know the whereabouts of Rosanne?
  • Did you notice anything special about Rosanne or her surroundings recently?
  • Do you know the possible owner of the grey slip found in her washing machine?
  • Do you know of any ‘friends’ in the live of Rosanne, that could be of danger to her?
