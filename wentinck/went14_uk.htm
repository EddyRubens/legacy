Bret Easton Ellis 

 

A lady, who prefers to remain anonymous, sent us this picture. In the accompanied letter she wrote that she has been following our search for Rosanne van Klaveren for several months already. That's why the man's tie struck her. The resemblance of the pattern to that of the gents slip found by inspector P. van Veen inside Rosanne's washing machine, is striking indeed! The only difference is the number of specks. Instead of the four specks inside the panes of the gents slip, we count only one on the tie.

The owner of this tie is the American writer Bret Easton Ellis. His last but one book, American Psycho, is full of horrible passages in which the main character Patrick Bateman tortures and murders people. Reactions to this book were very divergent. Maybe that is the reason why it took Ellis eight years to get his last book published. This book is called Glamorama and its events take place in the international fashion society. The main character gets offered a huge amount to search for a model missing in Londen.
In vieuw of the fact that Bret Easton Ellis has been known as a man with only three black suits and twenty white throusers, he must have only few ties as well. We wonder if this writer of atrocities has the same taste as the owner of the slip that has been found.
