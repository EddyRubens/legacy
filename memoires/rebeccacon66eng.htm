When I got back to my room, Carmen was already in the shower. I grabbed the phone and called the number Agent Andrews gave me if there was trouble. After I dialed the number I got a busy signal. 'Great,' I thought. 'If someone were trying to kill me I would be getting a busy signal.' I pulled out my laptop and emailed the CIA address where I had reached Agent Andrews before. I mentioned everything that has just happened and said it was urgent that he responded back as soon as possible. I wrote the 'subject title' as...Tachikawa on the Run! Hopefully Agent Andrews will get the message soon. I figured since I had my laptop out, I would write a quick email to the address Megan gave me to her family. If the Agents are not worried enough to help find her, I know her family will.

The letter read as follows:
Dear Mr. and Mrs. Isakova,

You are probably wondering who I am and why I am writing you. My name is Rebecca and I am a friend of your daughter, Megan. Megan, as well as myself, have gotten involved with a company which deals with memory recall. It involves a small implant in the brain allowing people to relive their own memories as well as experience the memories of others. It's all very complicated. The reason I am writing to you is because Megan is in serious danger. There is a very horrible doctor here who has been abusive to Megan. He has kept her in solitaire and is trying to make her reveal memories of her father. He is very interested in the KGB and wants to know many specifics, which Megan does not know. When I went to help her leave this place yesterday, she was gone. All of her belonging are gone as well. No one seems to know where she is and I am very worried about her. She gave me your address in secret and pleaded for me to contact you if anything went wrong. I am writing to you for your help because I don't know who else to turn to. Please respond to my email address at anytime.

Regards, Rebecca

Once I finished the email to Megan's parents, Carmen came out of the bathroom. She looked at me and asked what was wrong. I told her about Dr. Tachikawa leaving and how Dr. Hamilton seemed to be covering it all up. I also told her that I called the Agents, but got a busy signal, so I left them an email. "Well," She said. "There's nothing we can do now but wait."
