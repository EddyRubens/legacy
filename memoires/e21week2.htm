In between appointments I saw Davel close to the vending machines. He drank a can of coke and stepped aside so I could get a tonic. He laughed at me. He smiled in a pleasant way. “Are you alright?” I asked careless. “Perfect!” Davel answered. “Can’t be better. Although my head is still loaded with questions.” Davel always wants to ask and understand everything. When I take my time he tells me endless stories. But seldom something concrete. “Do you think you’ve almost reached the core of this big mystery?” I probably asked him this a bit too sarcastic. Davel opened and closed his mouth several times. He looked like a fish making bubbles. I felt sorry for him. Maybe I was too harsh with him. I wanted to pet him on his head but rubbed his shoulder when I found out his head was much taller than mine. Has he grown, not again? Such a bundle of muscles!  

My hand was resting on his shoulder a bit too long, it seemed so. Perhaps Davel thought it was on purpose. He held me tight. Less tight than last week but still enough not to escape from his hands. He kissed me. His soft lips opened and a wet kiss followed. I felt it itching in my stomach. Too nice to stop it. I shortly kissed back. I liked it but even though I pushed him away. It was not appropriate. 

“Tonight Dr. Tachikawa is coming back,” I said. This remark really didn’t make sense. For both of us. Why did I tell him that, at such a moment? Davel stared at the ground confused. Our eyes met for a second when he walked away. “Good for you,” I overheard just in time. What a fool I am! I really like Davel! He’s a nice boy, so why did I act so stupid? Was it a Freudian remark? Kodjiro’s absence didn’t strike me because of Davel. But this Japanese mystery still has a warm spot in my heart. That’s why there’s no destination for Davel. Not in this way.

I saw Dr. Tachikawa getting out of the taxi carrying two suitcases. I offered him a hand in the lobby. “No thanks,” he answered grumpy. He had returned showing his bad mood.
