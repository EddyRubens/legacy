Dr. Tachikawa had brought all sorts of equipment from Japan. This morning during the meeting he opened his brief-case. Two Japanese newspaper-cuttings about Braintec, M-Stores and a small instrument. Adrian, Steward and a few other members of the team who know all about techniques were getting impatient. ‘Coss they couldn’t wait any longer for they all wanted to admire the instrument at the same time. I couldn’t take my eyes off the M-Stores. They looked a bit different. Another new model? I wonder if Japanese memories are included?

Everybody was talking against each other. Dr. Tachikawa had to answer six questions at the same time. “If everybody is willing to sit down again, I will explain something,” he hushed up. When each one of them was seated again, Dr. Tachikawa explained that the instrument is able to scan brain-waves more secure than any other measuring instrument. It has been developed by a Japanese company of which I cannot remember the name right now. I think it sounds like Sjoes or Sju. Anyway, this company definitely has shown serious interest in Braintec and is suggesting cooperating. In order to support this way of cooperation they offered us this proto-type. Dr. Tachikawa looked proud and happy when he told us. He surely felt himself like a hero in need. The idea that I found him very attractive in this way, was constantly on my mind. He was enthusiastic in such a passionate way! He explained in a dazzling way how we could approach cooperation with this company. If he is right about his evaluations our know-how can redouble at once and we can realize our dreams within one year. Everybody was inspired by his enthusiastic explanation. He was a hero indeed.

Dr. Tachikawa packed his stuff again. When he stored the strange M-Stores without any explanation I couldn’t control my curiosity any longer. “What about that one, are those new M-Stores?” Dr. Tachikawa couldn’t help laughing hearing my questioning in such a greedy way. “No. It is the same technique in another form,” he declared. “Only different pictures on it. In Japan they just adore this.” 

Everybody was surprised when suddenly Dr. Hamilton stood up. “I think it is a wise idea when we have a talk in private,” he said with his trembling voice. Dr. Tachikawa nodded and suggested to go to Dr. Hamilton’s office. He walked round the table following the director and turned to me when he passed in front of me. He winked. Kodjiro winked at me! I’m sure of that; I saw it with my own eyes. Again my tummy loaded with thousands of butterflies. Why do I feel everything in such a sensitive way? 

Dr. Tachikawa and Dr. Hamilton retired during half an hour in this luxurious office. That morning I had nothing to do. Wished I could be with them, hearing what they had to tell us. I was walking up and down the hall, tied my shoelaces for the eighth time looking for other reasons to find an excuse to be here. Sometimes I could hear them talking. I couldn’t clearly catch up what they were saying but I understood their emotions right from the start. It sounded very serious.  Words changed into yelling. They got into a fight. I was afraid they would hit each other and I nervously was looking for an excuse to enter their room. Dr. Tachikawa left the room furiously. I walked into another direction like nothing had happened. When I peeped through the office-door I saw Dr. Hamilton standing in front of the window. The early sun of spring lighted his worried face.
