Because of the fight, the atmosphere hasn’t particularly improved. As long as Dr. Hamilton and Dr. Tachikawa aren’t together, things are not so bad. But when they have to work in the same space, there is some real hostility in the air.

Adelle proposed to go to town and buy a cake. That seemed like a good idea to me. Something as festive as a nice cake can produce wonders. We chose a gigantic one with loads of whipped cream and tried to make up a reason for getting it on the way back. Publication in the Scientific American magazine somehow didn’t seem like a good excuse. Adelle then asked me if there are some Dutch traditions that would work. A Thanksgiving celebration or some Dutch equivalent. If necessary we could make something up. But I thought some kind of jubilee might be a better reason for throwing this party. It’s been three months, or so and so days since whatever happened. Lot’s of possibilities. Once back at the Research Centre we got a calendar. The first test subject (Dr. Steward) was operated on exactly four months ago, but that seemed a more personal occasion that you wouldn’t celebrate with the entire group. But then we found out that it was 6 months and 4 days ago that the Braintec-team moved into the building. 6 months, sounds like a winner!

Dr. Hamilton saw us busy with the cake. “Who’s birthday is it?” he asked. “Braintec’s!” Adelle said, displaying a massive grin. Dr. Hamilton looked a bit surprised. "It is about half a year ago that Braintec was established here wasn’t it?” Adelle sounded a little uncertain. Had we gotten this all wrong? Dr. Hamilton thought for a second and then came to the conclusion that we weren’t far off. His worried face turned cheerful all of a sudden. “A celebration! What a welcome suggestion.” he said and left to get some champagne. Not long after that we were all sitting around the table enjoying cake and bubbles. Everyone seemed happy. Dr. Hamilton gave a short speech to specifically express his gratitude to all team-members. “And Tachikawa,” he continued, but then paused… everybody held their breath, “Without Tachikawa we would never have come this far.” Dr. Tachikawa nodded in confirmation and we were all relieved. We raised our glasses and started getting into a party mood. The only one who wasn’t in a festive mood was Steward. He was staring at his feet looking dazed.
