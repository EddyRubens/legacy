Well, I just returned from the tour. Quite amazing. I admit, now that I have seen the facility, samples of the MSI and MESI implants, how clean everything is, and how nice the staff are, I hope I do get chosen for an implant. I of course am hoping for the MESI. They were using percentages of how many can get which particular implant. I think that they said 80% can get the MESI. It would be so exciting to experience my own memories as well as someone else's! I met a few of the people who have the implants. Their names escape my mind right now but they were all very helpful and were eager to share their experiences. 

I was however a bit irritated when Rosanne made us all wait in the hall while she stared at a bouquet of flowers that was sent to her. She was talking to some woman with whom she was giggling and reading a card with. We waited out in the hall for a good 10 minutes. I know receiving flowers is exciting but it seemed a bit unprofessional to have us all standing around in the hallway.  

Dr. Hamilton was nice. He seemed to have a lot on his mind so he didn't chat for too long. He welcomed us all and excused himself to a meeting. I suppose being the director of such a facility is very time consuming. Not much time is left for small conversations. 

As we went along the tour I met Sheila. She is the psychologist. She seems very level headed. I over heard a few people talking about her. They said she was a bit unfriendly and hard to talk to. She seemed nice enough to me. I mean, I wouldn't go out of my way to start a conversation with her, but I wouldn't not talk to her if I needed answers. I wonder what this Jessica person they were talking about was like. 

Everyone else seemed nice, but I am not to sure about Mark Steward. I know I shouldn't pass judgment about someone I don't know, but this guy seemed creepy. He was staring at me and this other girl on the tour (I think her name was Megan) as though he could see right through our clothes. I wondered what kind of memories he has. I bet there are no cute puppy dogs and ice cream cones in any memory he has!
