I probably entered the common office at the wrong moment, ‘coss Steward suddenly stopped talking. He waited till I had picked up all the dossiers I needed and then continued talking in a subdued voice. His mysterious silence arouse my curiosity, even more so ‘coss he was only speaking to Dr. Tachikawa. I had to eavesdrop on this conversation through the closed door.

My curiosity controlled me totally. Steward almost whispered, and therefore I had to push my ear against the door till it turned red.  “Don’t you think it’s odd? I would feel passed over if I stood in your shoes. You are in to this together, aren’t you?” What was Steward talking about? For one moment I had this scary feeling that he was talking about Dr. Tachikawa and me. How did I pass him and what exactly are we into together? “If I were you, I would explain the ins and outs of the matter very clearly to him. He is nowhere without you.” I could hardly hear what Steward said after that. Something about his mind or being blind. I tried to hear more through the keyhole. That wasn’t such a good idea. The door flew wide open and hit me full on my temple. Dr. Tachikawa walked away angrily. He turned once, to see me, and I think I could read some wonder off his face beside the anger. Then he continued walking. The whole left side of my face hurt. But especially the part right next to my eye. It felt warm and thick and it pumped as if my hart had moved to there. Steward entered the corridor as well. He asked me in an annoyed way if I had listened in. When he saw how I leaned against the wall, he became milder. “What is wrong with you?” I murmured that I missed one dossier and that the door had hit my face. “What a prick.” Steward said grumpily. Did he mean me or Dr. Tachikawa?

Because the door had seriously hit the left side of my face - I almost had a black eye – Steward wanted to make an MRI-scan. So I had to enter that claustrophobic tube again. Fortunately everything was still intact. My face looks like I have been in a fight and I have already been asked several times what I had done with it. “Walked into a door.” is the classical answer. I don’t worry about this injury and now that he knows that the MESI isn’t damaged, Steward doesn’t either. What does go on in my mind, are the words that made Dr. Tachikawa so angry. What was Steward talking about? It wasn’t about Dr. Tachikawa and me, ‘coss he mentioned a he.
