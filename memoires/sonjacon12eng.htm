Still quivering with emotions from my visit at the monkey cellar beneath the bloodbank, I dialed the number I found in the keeper’s breast pocket when I was at the station. Yes, the telephone rung. It took a long time. Maybe it was a mobile phone and I would get a voicemail message in a while. No, someone answered. “Paul speaking," a voice said. I pretended to be a Japanese speaking English. "Who speaking please?" "Paul. Paul Bentley," the voice said. "Is it you Sonja?" Well, that was a surprise. "Correct, but who the hell are you?" I answered irritated. "Listen," Paul whispered in a hurry, "I am a friend. Your friend Rosanne isn’t well. She and some Adrian Young are heavily addicted. At this moment they lay in some kind of opium dive using uploads all the time – I’ll get them out soon, they are in danger. Adrian also has the monkey-uploads, don’t ask me how he’d got them, but they definitely have to stop using them. They need to go to the Detox as soon as possible, because they are going into cold turkey... They have Hans and Heather as well, two test subjects from Portland. It is all classified, I can’t tell you more. Tomorrow you must come to the old temple in the neighborhood east from the bloodbank, and then we will speak further.” Then he hung up.

I wished I was home. In Brussels. With Hugo. Even though he didn’t love me (anymore?). Then at least I would now what I’m up to. Tokyo seemed to be empty. The neon lights were reflected in the wet streets. The few people on the street seemed to move in slow-motion. They looked at me, while they passed through. I felt terribly alone. After I pulled myself together, I went further to eat something and then back to my guesthouse. It was as if I hadn’t been there for ages. When I closed the door behind me, I was overwhelmed by tiredness like I hadn’t had in years. I drunk a glass of water at the sink, took a shower and made preparations to go to sleep.

I switched on the small light at my bedside table, and heard something falling on the ground. It rolled underneath my bed. I bent down, and felt blindly in the dark on the floor. There it was. A small tube. By the lamp I could read the script. 'M-Store X564C203' it said.
