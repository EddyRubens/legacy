The nicest place to kill time is the Japanese garden. The vegetation is arranged well and there are enough benches to sit on. I can sit there for hours while watching the birds and making up life stories of unsuspecting passers by. On weekdays there are less people in the garden. Especially Mondays and Wednesdays, those are the quietest and thus nicest days.

Jessica came along with me to the park this afternoon. She’s a nice girl. She’s sincerely interested and her openness is inviting. This enables her to get much more information out of me than Sheila, another test psychologist who usually irritates me with her dopiness. Sheila is a dumb blonde. Jessica is good at what she does because she has such an open personality, even after working hours. For a moment I considered to talk to her about Dr. Tachikawa but I didn’t. I think she finds him boring.

Jessica stays in the research-center during the week because otherwise she will miss out on leisure time. She lives in San Francisco, where she claims to have a ‘house to die for’. She wouldn’t move for anything in the world, and with the distance being so great she will only go home during weekends. Last time she was mesmerizing about the ideal location and fabulous view, she told me that maybe I could come along some time. I’m thinking it’s not such an ideal location if she has to spend most of the week at Braintec. Still, she’s made peace with the situation and even appreciates being in Portland from time to time. Her rather than me, because I think Portland’s boring.

At first I was wildly enthusiastic about the city because it satisfied all the urban needs, something Greenland lacked. After more than a year of snow, ice, rocks and a population that knows no cities bigger than Nuuk’s, with its population of 12.657, every city is probably going to be fantastic in comparison. Now I see more narrow mindedness in Portland than I saw in the whole of Greenland altogether. The American Dream, how low can you go? Yes, all those shops come in handy when you need something. But if your needs are simple and you refuse to let the media talk you into needing more, those overcrowded streets are just plain irritating. Whenever I roam too long without any purpose, I feel like somebody who’s homeless. I don’t get that feeling in the Japanese garden; there you have to pay a small entry to get in. Nobody is looking for food in the trash cans there.
