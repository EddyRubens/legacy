The upload of my own dogslegde-trip was different than I expected. I thought it would be more clinical. It was the first time that I already knew what kind of memory I would recall and maybe that made the difference. Now I could imagine how it would be. The whole morning I was with all of my thoughts back on the ice in Greenland. I called all the dogs by name; Iluliaq and Immaqa, Kajortoq, Naasoq and Sukkulaati with the spots.

All of them hard workers. They ran their longs out of their bodies for the little bit of meat they would get at the end of the day. A speed of 60 kilometers an hour is common on flat plains. I always become a bit homesick when I think about the good things in Greenland. But this homesickness was never as strong as after the recall of the dogslegde ride. I was so totally back in Greenland that the tears sprung from my eyes. I couldn’t stop crying and felt ashamed. Dr. Hamilton and Steward paced up and down the room while Jessica gave me some tissues to clean my nose. In the meantime she softly massaged my neck. Dr. Tachikawa left the room expressionless. Why did I ever leave Greenland? I felt so much like home!
