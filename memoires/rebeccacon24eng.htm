Carmen just got back from her upload. She was sort of disappointed. She said that her upload was not exciting at all. I found that very hard to believe. The fact that we are experiencing someone else's memory is exciting enough for me! But I dared not tell her how I really felt. I just sympathized with her as she shared her upload with me. 

She said it started off with her in an old cabin. She said it was out in the county on a hillside. She said she felt it was a place where she was comfortable and had spent many afternoons alone. She described the two small rooms and the loft upstairs. It sounded marvelous! But, I just said to her, "How boring!"
She said after walking around 'the old fire hazard' (as she was calling it) she went outside into a field of wild flowers. She remembers picking many different kinds and putting them into a basket she had in her arm. She said the only real sensations she felt were, the cool breeze blowing and the aromatic smell of the wild flowers. 

I would have been thrilled to experience such peacefulness! But Carmen was clearly unhappy with it. She told me that she asked how come she didn't get the jets like I did. Dr. Hamilton supposedly told her that they randomly select the M-stores for the subjects. But in her case, they decided to choose something calm because of the complications she had during her surgery. Of course that irritated her! I told her, "Don't worry Carm... They'll see that you did fine with this one ... and I know that next time will be more exciting!" She gave me sort of a half smile and said, "Yeah, I guess so."

I told her to call Diego to tell him about it. He has this special ability to always get her smiling. I just hope he doesn't talk her into going home. He has been so on the edge since Carmen's surgery. He has been calling me all the time to ask how she is doing. I finally gave him my email address and told him it was easier if he left me mail. 

Please, please, please let Carmen come back smiling! I know she hasn't been too happy lately and I am worried that she will want to leave. I keep telling her it's all new and we just need to take time to get use to things. I don't think Carmen likes change to much. Plus, now that she has experienced what she was desperately waiting for, and it was not what she expected, I feel she might decide her time here is done.  Maybe I should call Diego and talk to him. I couldn't stand to be alone here! 

Dr. Hamilton left me a note to come see him.  I wonder what this could be about! I will go to his office and see. Maybe when I get back, all will be well with Carmen.
