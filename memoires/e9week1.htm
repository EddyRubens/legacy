A fantastic memory! This afternoon I’ve got one of Robberts’ memories uploaded. A memory of his childhood. It was an amusement park, probably Disneyland. Nowadays I wouldn’t go to one of those parks, but this way I had a wonderful time. So much authenticity is hard to find in a real experience. I felt enormous enthusiasm; the elation of a young child. I used to go to amusement parks when I was a child too, but I don’t remember it having such an impression on me. The pure happiness in a world where you can have anything you wish for. An ice-cream you can only dream of.

If I think about the images of the upload, I find them not so alluring. The emotions made it so powerful. I was over emotional during this session that I had a sore throat and I almost fell down through weakness. Even now, when the memory is still on my mind, I become emotional again. I can easily burst into tears if I want to. The memory gave me such a peaceful and happy feeling; I can’t remember ever feeling so comfortable. This emotion might be similar to happiness. I could feel this warm intensity from my fingertips to the deepest spot in my belly. Did Robbert feel the same when he was a child? Unfortunately for him he did not. Previous uploads of Robberts’ memory always gave me a good feeling, but this exceeded everything. And when Robbert got his own amusement-park-memory uploaded yesterday, his reaction wasn’t that special at all.

Jessica took much more time than usual for the interview after my upload. I am happy she did. This experience is so radical that I feel the urge to speak about it. My emotional response might tell us more about my current state of mind than about the memory. Maybe this upload put a finger upon a weak spot. It brings up the thing I miss the most: sense of security and trust.
