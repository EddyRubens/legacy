It has been two weeks since my trip to Germany and overhearing about Braintec. Since then I've made contact with Dr. Hamilton, the director of Braintec, and had a ticket to Portland mailed to me. I leave in three days. I never in my life have been spontaneous. I am the kind of person who need to sit and rationalize everything out, but this Braintec thing just seems to be calling to me.  

I have so much to take care of before I go. I have already bought this laptop to record my diaries in. Who knows what kind of computers they have at the Braintec facility. And who knows if they will let me use them. Journaling is important to me. I know I will like to look back on this experience in years to come. But then, if I get chosen for an implant maybe this will be a waste of time.  I might be able to just relive these memories!  

I will have to call my work tomorrow and tell them that I need to take some personal leave. That will just make it easier to explain. Then I'll try to explain all this to Josh and beg him to adopt Bruce while I am gone.
