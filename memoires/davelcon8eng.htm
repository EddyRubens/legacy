When that M-store was over, I was flush with the joy of being alive.  I was looking at all situations from four different angles.  Instead of seeing the doctor, I saw, the doctor's mother, how she must have encouraged him to read by rubbing his back when he was a child.  Everone was "someone reinforced or prohibited," and not just a mass of being, sitting in their modern world, trapped from meaning. 

Was the existentialist in me dying?  I wanted to make love to the cold earth.  I wanted to transmute with it, into an everchanging Walt Whitman of a creature.  I wanted to find myself under my own feet.  I was more than one.  I was a multiplicated human being.  I was plural, yet not at all alone and crazy.  I joined hands with myself, just by looking across the quad at four females playing soccer in their shorts and sweatshirts.  I was giving shrift to the almighty, simply by breathing. 

It is hard to explain all of this at once.  But that's the only way to explain it.

AT ONCE.  We are many AT ONCE.  WE ARE AT ONCE.  Future and finite, immediate and infinite.  And our death is our beginning and our beginning is our death.

And it already happened and is happening. 

Maybe I will ask Roseann to marry me.  Maybe this is all a fantasy come true, and that life tomorrow will be gone before we know it. 

Janis Joplin once said at a concert i attended in an M-Store, "Hey man, why so down?  I mean there is no tomorrow.  It's all the same fucking day!!!"   And then she rocked right out of this earth.
