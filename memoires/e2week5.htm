I’ve been laying here, bored stiff for the last three days. I still get wheeled around in the garden after tests, but not as much. I get to walk to the toilet, but that’s all the movement they will allow me. Hanging outside the bed is about the only exercise my legs will get today. Thankfully that will change tomorrow. Then I will begin the physical recuperation process by going onto the gangway. The mental tests will continue also. Math, stock of words training, association tests, you name it. If I hadn’t gotten along with the test psychologist so well, I would probably have refused to cooperate. Stupid business. Jessica is nice and also funny, I almost look forward to the tests because of her.

Another pleasant break from staring at the ceiling is Robbert, a fellow test subject. Too bad he prefers reading all day; otherwise I might have found a more regular chess opponent. Who knows, maybe I can share his passion for books sometime, as Robbert’s got a MESI implant, like me. He is the third man who has one implanted and I am the first women. That makes us an important couple. His implant was done precisely a week before mine. Seeing him do so well gives me renewed courage.  I’m sure things will be alright.
