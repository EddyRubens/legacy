I am not sure if it's refreshing or scary when I see new faces around here. On my way out to the Japanese Gardens, I saw Dr. Hamilton talking to a strange man. I say strange because I have never seen him around here before. But, I got the feeling that I knew him from somewhere. When I noticed Dr. Hamilton gaze up at me, I got so nervous that I quickly put it past me.

I was just grateful that Dr. Hamilton was momentarily preoccupied. I didn't want him to stop me and want to talk. Especially after last night. He obviously knew what was going to happen. But what was worst was...he let it happen. I quickly walked past the two of them and headed for the front door. I saw Rosanne walking to toward them as I took a look back. She shook the strangers hand and said she was "pleased that he made efforts to come and visit." Dr. Hamilton called out my name as Rosanne was talking to the stranger. I kept walking and pretended not to hear him. I know this must have angered him. I'll try to shed a tear for him later.

When I got to the Japanese Gardens, I headed straight for the Bonsai Tree. Megan was not there. I was afraid that someone might have found out about our plan and kept her locked up in the facility somewhere. As I was waiting, I couldn't help but think about the stranger at Braintec. Where had I seen him before? Then I remembered. He was the man sitting in front of me on the plane, when I was returning from my trip in Germany. How could I have forgotten? He was the one talking to Rosanne and asking the millions of questions. He must have decided to try it out for himself. I wondered if I should let him know what he was getting in to. But then again, I can't help but want to blame him for my being here. It was the conversation he had with Rosanne that lit my curiosity fuse. 

Megan arrived about 20 minutes after I got there. She kept saying how sorry she was for being late. She said she forgot the directions and made a wrong turn somewhere along the way and got lost. I told her there was nothing to apologize for. I was just glad she was able to make it. She told me that the doctors, especially Dr. Tachikawa, yell at her when she forgets things. I assured her that I was nothing like them and have made plenty of mistakes myself. Once I was able to calm her down, we sat and talked. I told her my stories and experiences here, then she shared hers.
