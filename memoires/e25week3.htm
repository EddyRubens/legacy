A splendid idea if I would find an internet-café. Through email I thought I could succeed. Strangely enough I couldn’t find any possibility getting online. Probably everybody here is constantly online, at home or through mobile phones. Public internet facilities are not common here, or I do not recognize the places as such.

While I was searching for the Tourist Information I met a man who was holding a large sign. He stood still and was gazing in front of him. For a moment I asked myself if he was real. I couldn’t read what was written on the sign but discovered a drawing showing a red heart accompanied by a black arrow pointing diagonally. Following the direction of the arrow, I saw more red hearts and red crosses on the windows of the top floor. As I had nothing to do right now, and I got aroused by the idea, I decided to have a look. A red heart interprets medical care. I hope they can help me out over there.

They happened to donate blood. A young doctor in a white shirt was winking at me and started talking in Japanese. Fortunately he also spoke English. He wanted to know my blood-group. With a copy of my passport, a quick test and after three tubes of blood I earned 3500 yen. The doctor advised me to keep seated for a moment so I would feel much better afterwards. He gave me a rich-flavored-shake filled with protein which reminded me of Davel. Would this substance give me the same bunch of muscles like him? The stuff was sweet as honey. A disgusting synthetic flavor of vanilla, ugh. But I did empty the glass to the bottom. It helped. At once I felt much more stable.

When the doctor paid me the yen, I investigated about Braintec. I asked him in simply four different ways and pronounced Kodjiro’s name several times, but the doctor kept shaking his head. But he did know where I could get online though. At the Virgin Megastore. Six metro-stops to the north, changing direction to the JRline to Ikebukuro and following the southern direction of the station. I had to ask some passers-by before I definitely found the place. They were right, some dozens of computers, but they were all occupied. They were queuing up. No wonder, when such facilities are hard to find. Online is even free of charge!

After much patience it was my turn. It took some time before I managed to produce normal letters from the keyboard. Finally I got started. A sweet email for Adrian, nothing else. Nobody knew I was here. I emailed all test subjects and kindly requested Dr. Tachikawa and Sonja to contact me. Then Virgin closed down. Tomorrow morning I will be the first one to sit behind these computers.
