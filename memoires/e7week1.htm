I was almost bursting with impatience to upload another memory after the Grand Canyon experience. It was my plan not to force anything and just let it come naturally. The recall was nice, but not as nice as the previous one.

I was in a garden. A manicured garden with a good smell and a lot of flowers. I felt convenient here. The grass has been mowed and smelled delicious. Some birds were picking the blades. I was very proud of this garden. Did men realize how hard I had worked on this? It is not that easy to get these plants so full of flowers. Now that the rhododendrons were in full bloom, I got a good run for my work. The flowers were shining gloriously and made me smile. I touched one of the flowers carefully. Its red was as soft as velvet and it lost some pollen. After I walked a bit across the path I started to search for the rake. It was time to rake the loose grass blades together.

After this recall I wished for one moment that I had my own garden as well. It should be nice to dig in the earth and to see what the seasons do. But my garden would never be so well organized and full of flowers. I prefer a more wildly world and I would probably neglect my garden quite soon. Let nature do its work, then I shall do mine. Writing a diary for example.
