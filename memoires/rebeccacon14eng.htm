Last night, when Carmen and I got back to the facility, we had a long talk about Davel, the implants, and things of that nature. I think she picked up on the fact that I was a bit nervous to have heard about the whole Davel-Pirate story. "I don't think they messed him up," she said, "I think he was just smokin or tokin, if you know what I mean!"  She sure knows how to lighten a situation! I felt better after our chat and again became excited about being here! 

This morning I was called into Sheila's office. Dr. Hamilton was there as well. "Good news!" Dr. Hamilton said, "We would like to welcome you to our Braintec family!" He seemed to be in a surprisingly good mood. I looked at him for a moment, deciding whether or not I should burst out with, 'Daddy!'  I decided against it, for who knows if it would have spoiled his good mood. "I'm assuming I passed all the tests?" I said instead. He shook his head in a positive motion while smiling and looking over what must have been my file. Sheila said "The staff is very excited to have you here because you are quite different from the other test subjects." "Yes," Dr. Hamilton interrupted, "you see, you are the first test subject we will have that exhibits any obvious fears!  (I cringed because somehow knew this was coming!) If you allow us to insert the MESI implant we will be able to extract a memory when you have had.......... (there was a pause while he looked down into the file)....... an 'attack,' as you call them. This will enable us to upload your 'attack' memory to a willing testee to see how they react. This might also allow us to better understand how you feel when these 'attacks' happen and maybe possibly find a way to rid them from your life!" The first thing I thought was "Why does he project the word 'attack' then leave a short pause before continuing his sentence?" Then I thought of ridding these fears from my life was too good to be true. However, I didn't know what to say. The MESI implant was the one I was hoping to receive, only not particularly under these circumstances. "I guess that's what being a test subject is all about, letting the doctors test their theories." I said. Dr. Hamilton looked at Sheila and then back at me. He was smiling from ear to ear! 

I went back to my room with a pass to the "Left Wing' in hand. I was told to pack up my stuff, because I was going to be moved to a bigger room away from the new arrivals. I assumed this was a good thing. When I told Dr. Hamilton that Carmen and I would still like to share a room he was more than happy to oblige. The good thing about Dr. Hamilton being so enthusiastic about this is he would like me to have the surgery in two days. Carmen had decided to take the MESI implant as well. She told me the date and time of her implant. Turns out her surgery is right before mine, on the same day. I joked with her saying they needed to do a 'rough draft' before they dare touch their new 'Star Subject' She laughed and threw her 'Carmen pillow' at me. 

Here come those butterflies again! I must let Josh know of the exciting news!
