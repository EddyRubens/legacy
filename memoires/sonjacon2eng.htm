I couldn’t write anything down. Even sitting behind the cheap white chip board desk in my room, where for weeks (have I been here that long?) paper and pen have waited, is a major effort. And when I’m sitting, I wrack my brain trying to find something significant, but I feel empty and everything looks ugly.

Rosanne, who is a fussy and rather zealous type who talks with her hands a lot (and likes a drink!), does her best to put me at ease. I can see she’s trying, and I like her, but it doesn’t register. I didn’t even want to know the results of the EEG, and the hypnotic sessions were a fiasco. The doctor thought I wasn’t open to suggestion enough although I’m really trying to cooperate. I probably won’t go to ‘The Leftwing’. I hear the others whispering about it as if it’s somewhere sacred, some type of paradise, the solution to all their problems or something like that.

I haven’t spoken to Hugo for two weeks now. Not since after he told me, crying on the phone and everything, that he is in love with his assistant. Sylvia works at his practice. She’s young, a natural beauty, full of life and energetic, into aerobics and she’s insanely bourgeois. I couldn’t say a word, didn’t even know what to say, so I slammed the phone down and sat staring at it the following hour. I arrived at the evaluation too late and told them I had fallen asleep.

The first few days I acted as if nothing was wrong and cooperated with the research just like the others. I even acted cheerful at a party. I did drink way too much, so when I got back I struggled my way through gallons of tears and snot. Full of self reproach and self pity of course. The next day I looked awful and Doctor Hamilton asked me if I needed to talk. I got out of it by telling him I‘m allergic to crustaceans and that there must have been a few in the fish soup. I assured him I was taking pills for it.

I’m writing this while sitting in the Japanese garden, which has become my favorite sanctuary on the Braintec grounds. I feel very lonely, and though I’m angry at Hugo (why did he ever want to marry me?), I do miss him terribly. We went through a lot together in a short period, especially stuff to do with his career. But why doesn’t he call me? Will I return to an empty house, has he already moved into that dancing girl’s place? Maybe he will have committed suicide, plagued with guilt and remorse… Not good, I’m becoming way too sentimental over this. I think I’ll call him tomorrow. I can see Alice the Malice coming this way so it’s time to stop writing.
