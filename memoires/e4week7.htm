This morning I was shown the vase that the other test person had seen. Nothing like my vase! My vase was even uglier and also bigger. This vase was like a bad copy of the vase I experienced yesterday. This vase, the real vase, disappointed me. Jessica said that was no tragedy. Everybody matches the memories to their own experiences, she told me.

Fortunately the doctors were not disappointed. I’m as big a hero as I was yesterday. Dr. Tachikawa even invited me to diner. I hope my acceptance didn’t sound too eager. I’m just so ready to get out of this place. If I were to go into town alone I’d probably end up at Wendy’s again. Now I’m going to a fancy restaurant, Japanese probably. I’ve never been good with the sticks, I’m sure Dr. Tachikawa will be amused by my clumsiness. He is smaller than me and often chuckles like a child. I can’t figure him out most of the time. Is his invitation to diner a date or does he want to observe his test subject in a natural environment?
