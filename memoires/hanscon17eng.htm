After breakfast we disguise ourselves as proper business partners and order a huge taxi. We handle business with Mitsuki. We bought some art from Yukio as well. We only carry our new bought suitcases with hand luggage in it. The rest must be on see by now. After a long drive through the city, industrial areas and some rice fields we arrive at the airport. The driver knows where to find the entrance for rich people. Heather and I are unnoticeable between the business people. Fortunately it all goes smooth at checking inn. Mitsuki arranged it perfectly. Now directly towards the lounge for business class. Through the window we overview the hall for ordinary people. We probably see some Brainteccers. But we are not sure. We settle down in the fluffy furniture and overlook the environment.

It is a melting pot of Japanese, white people and Arabians. Most of them are properly dressed like we are but there is a terribly dress American couple as well and someone to whom all colors mismatch. It’s even a Dutch guy. I ignore him. A couple of children throws the respectability overboard and jumps at the elegant benches. Nobody stops them. Heather started a conversation with the parents. They are American and lived in Japan for two years. Now they fly back to the States. They have to arrange some things in the nick of time. Heather offers them to take the children to the plane. This way she’s also disguised as a parent. Her plane leaves two hours before mine. With two jumping kids at her side we say goodbye. It isn’t easy. We had such a nice time together. All those troubles couldn’t disturb that.

With a toddler at each side Heather walks to the gate in her suit. I feel lonely immediately and take my laptop out of the suitcase. This is the end of my diary. Within a few moments I will start a conversation with an economical topic with a business man. Then we can walk without being noticed towards the plane. When I’m back home I’ll have something else on my mind than a diary. Then I’ll write to Heather.
