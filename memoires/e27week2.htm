Never ever saw Dr. Tachikawa so irritated. It’s obvious, seeing me here in this building was not what he wanted. A few seconds later we joined the table with three men I’ve never seen before. Jessica entered the room with a tray loaded with tea and small snacks. Obviously this was the Japanese delegation of Braintec. If Kodjiro didn’t lie to me about the fact that there weren’t more than ten people in the whole of Japan knowing what Braintec was really about, then this must be the hard core. Thrilling.

A grumpy looking man stood up and said something in Japanese while he wasn’t an Asian himself. Everybody was nodding and I did the same. Then he switched over into English, fortunately. He told us about a couple of test subjects arriving to Tokyo yesterday, coming from Portland. He meant Heather and Hans, an American woman and a Dutch man. According to the network they would be here for a special upload, that’s what he said. But according to our information the ins and outs of the matter were completely different. He looked thoroughly at the man to the right. I hadn’t met him before either. “It’s a trick,” this man answered in an amazing high voice. “He’s been framed. It must be the NPI and the FBI dealing with this. We should operate very accurately.” There was a buzz. The man, who spoke before, apparently the leader of this group, hit the table with his fist. “We shouldn’t let them know that we got it, no way!” he yelled. He scared me a bit. “Let us take the bait and then pull them towards us.” Again everybody was misunderstanding each other. The man in charge didn’t mind this time. He stood up, bent forward and walked out of the door. 

Didn’t know what I should feel or think. Hans and Heather could have a real problem. But I had too little information to find out in what way they are in terribly danger. I wanted to talk to Jessica. She might give me a clear picture. I looked around me, but I couldn’t find Jessica anywhere. No idea where she had gone so quickly. Dr. Tachikawa grabbed me by the arm and almost dragged me outside. He looked at me seriously in the elevator. “From the beginning I have tried to protect you and kept you out of it,” he said snappy. “Now you are on your own.”
