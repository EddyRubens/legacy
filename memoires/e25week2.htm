There I am, in Tokyo. After arrival I was roaming about the airport all morning. I was exhausted. I hardly slept on the plane. I have been watching two lousy movies and a documentary about Inca’s. I couldn’t relax at all. I’m rather restless. It feels like I’m on the run. They are most probably looking for me now. At the same time I wish to be found, by the people who work for Kodjiro. How on earth am I going to find them?

Due to the time difference I had the feeling I arrived in Japan in the afternoon, while the sun was rising again here. I had not the slightest idea where I had to go and tried to sleep for a while. How inconvenient those hours. End of the morning I exchanged my last dollars for yen. Not much, I’m afraid. I spent half of it on a meal of noodles, which they call ‘ramen’ over here, and a train ticket to the centre of Tokyo. A hard job. They don’t speak English as much as I thought they would. So many Japanese words were going through my head before I understood I had to buy a ticket first from the slot machine, before they could serve ‘ramen’. It would be much easier if they showed photos of the meals attached to the slot machine instead of those strange letter indications. I had no idea which meal I had ordered. I chose the cheapest one. The food was a bit dull but eatable.

Buying my train ticket was a great adventure as well. Also this dispenser was confusing. The only thing I understood was the huge red button mentioning ‘help’. I pressed this button a few times. Suddenly a piece of metal beside the dispenser disappeared and a man’s head with glasses was showing. “Can I help you?” the head asked. His English was bad but I managed to explain which train I had to take. My ticket was also valid in the metro. As long as I didn’t pass the small gates outside, I could travel throughout the whole city. So I did. The metro is traveling above the ground quit often so I was able to see a lot of the town. Tokyo seems to have more than twenty centers. After every trip by metro you reach another central spot full of flashing neon-lights. Almost everybody sleeps in the metro. Whether they sit down or stand up. On every briefcase and mobile phone small dolls are attached. Also businessmen wearing black suits and shining shoes are decorated with Pikachu or Hamtaro.

Being in the metro for a few hours I got out at Kokkaigijidomae. I liked that name. I put my luggage into a locker. For the price of 850 yen! Spending so much money again in one day I will not have much left. Don’t think of it now. Do they have Salvation Army here? I hope I will find Kodjiro, Sonja or Jessica as soon as possible. I’m afraid I’m looking for a needle in a haystack.
