Hmmm. I had a bit too much wine. I feel quite light headed. The keyboard seems to have lost some of its stability; some of the keys are pulsating and feel weird to the touch. This was the first day since the operation that I was allowed to drink a considerable amount of alcohol. Alcohol is my greatest enemy but tonight I decided that I couldn’t pass on this opportunity to have some drinks. I can’t remember when wine tasted so good. Maybe it was the combination of good wine and refined food or the nice atmosphere. Or maybe it was the implant in my lobe. It was sweet of Dr. Tachikawa to not order a third bottle of wine, even though the waiter asked if we wanted more. “You’ve had enough for the next three weeks” he spoke fatherly. He was right, I guess.

For some reason I was having a hard time to keep some moderation. After three glasses my natural reservations have almost completely dissolved. The only thing left was too try and quench my unquenchable thirst. The more I had, the thirstier I became. My cheerful drinking came with some really strange impulses.

Dr. Tachikawa had to apologize for me when I tried to turn the salad leaves, which were covered with a most exquisite dressing by the way, into a hair-net. “Salad on your test person” I kept repeating. How was he to know the typical Dutch word play (in Dutch, salad means salad but also to hit)I was clumsily trying to translate into English? Nobody thought it was funny. This evening I was abnormally exuberant, as if I was going on my first school trip. I was so nervous it was irritating, which made me giggle a lot. I was really quite annoyed. Happily Dr. Tachikawa didn’t seem too bothered. Japanese women are also very giggly, so he must be used to it. If he had ordered that third bottle he would have had me in the palm of his hand. I wonder if he realized that. Maybe he didn’t want anything. Possibly he was only concerned with the work. After all, that third bottle would have cost a few more brain cells that are needed for the research. Yes, his research. He hardly speaks of anything else. Will anything else ever be more important than his research?
