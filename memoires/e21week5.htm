I think I made a mistake. The man sitting next to me in the plane a couple of weeks ago appeared to be a public health inspector! Today he paid the Research Center a visit to check on our permits and to inspect how things are going nowadays.

I saw him talking to Dr. Hamilton and I walked towards him. I had forgotten his name, but I can recognize his troubled face out of a thousand people. I shook hands with him and told him I was pleased that he made efforts to come and visit us to see how things are developing here. How could I know that this man was not, as I was hoping it would be, a new test subject to be? How could I know he came by to investigate the whole business? Dr. Hamilton watched me with a nasty look and ordered me to come to his office later that morning.

Dr. Hamilton yelled at me. “How could you have in mind sending the inspector’s visit to all of us? Are you going mad? If you do not like it here, you better get the hell out of here!” I hadn’t the slightest idea why Dr. Hamilton accused me in that way. It was obvious that there were big problems caused by me. Although I didn’t know anything about the inspector’s visitors! I was flabbergasted. I suddenly began to cry and couldn’t stop anymore. “I know, I know!” I sniffed through my tears. “I cannot do anything good! I am a shitty bag, a total failure. Always have been!” Dr. Hamilton’s anger exchanged into irritation. He muttered something about giving me the benefit of the doubt but he declared roughly that I will have to go next time I’m causing any trouble. Still sniffing I reached my room. On my way I met Adrian, who asked me what was going on, very worried indeed. I told him all about it and he was searching for some comforting words. When he couldn’t find any, I started crying again. Just one more stupidity and I can pack my bags.

In my room I comforted myself. Maybe it was wrong to tell that man, the other day, openly and honestly about my epileptic attacks which bothered me during the first weeks of my stay at Braintec. He asked me all about it and at that moment I didn’t realize that he was part of the inspector’s visit. I thought I could assure him to become a test subject as well!

Later Steward told me that the inspector had examined all the test subjects. In general everybody was very positive. I didn’t have to answer questions. I already did in the plane. I heard that Dr. Hamilton described me as an unpredictable, unstable personality, being capable spreading around rumors. A typical Borderline case. I don’t care at all. Fortunately Kathy had just left for home. As far as Kathy is concerned we can be grateful to her we didn’t get into more problems.
