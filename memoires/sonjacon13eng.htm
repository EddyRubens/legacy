Just as I was looking at the inscription on the M-Store the telephone rang. Which was strange because nobody knew I was here. "Hi, it's Paul again. Paul Bentley," said a warm, friendly voice. He said the plans had changed and that I was expected at the station in Osaka, tomorrow afternoon at 12.30 pm. His voice was reassuring, and though I didn’t really know who he was and what he wanted from me, I trusted him instinctively. "Have a nice rest, Sonja. You’ve earned it. And bring the empty M-Store, we’ll need it.." We hung up, and soon I fell into a deep sleep.

The landscape shot past with a raging speed, but my thoughts were elsewhere. What would be waiting for me this time? Being suspicious, I’d taken an earlier train so I could get there an hour earlier, to see who was coming to pick me up -and to check if I really wanted to go for it. But I was also curious about Paul and his enchanting voice.

A slightly older, but not bad looking man walked towards me as I got off the train. It was him. He shook my hand, squeezing it softly to encourage me and he held on a bit longer than would be normal. I was happy to see him, and I knew it was OK. He put a hand on my shoulder and gently guided me past the busy station restaurant to a door. "Don’t worry, I have a good plan," he said, as he opened the door.

"Are you Sonja? I’m Adrian Young." We shook hands as if we had known each other for a long time. Paul excused himself. Adrian and I went to get a coffee and drank it by the coffee machine, while chatting about our adventures in Portland and in Tokyo. And how I played the part of double agent for Kodjiro and the Canadian, and how I was supposed to kill Rosanne. I had no idea what to make of it and neither did Adrian. Adrian also spoke of a Hans and Heather, who supposedly returned to Portland, but I didn’t know them.

Paul came back, bringing a skinny little woman with a white face. Then I recognized her: it was Rosanne! My, she had changed, and she looked very tired. We embraced, and I was glad that Paul brought her here as well. Thankfully Rosanne found it funny when I told her she was looking at me as if I was about to kill her... But Paul was in a hurry, and didn’t hesitate to unfold his plan. Rosanne and Adrian were going to jump off a building wearing parachutes and, once down, get an injection to go into a sort of clinical death, while an extractor was going to record everything to the empty M-Store I had brought, and another one of Paul’s. "And let’s do it right now," he added.
