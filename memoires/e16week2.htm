Steward’s damaged memory which Steve experienced as the first one created a commotion for everybody. Since this morning a gossip is heard that the M-Store in which this memory is filed has been used again. Steward’s dull boat-trip was said to be recorded over a delicate adventure. I do not know exactly who came up with this idea but everybody was flabbergasted. Whose delicate memory and how he got this idea about that M-Store? Are they experiencing with other things apart from the program that I do not know of? According to Sally’s claims, it is Dr. Tachikawa who is experiencing all sorts of strange ideas. She is sure, for a 98%, that traces of that former upload are heading his direction. Steward, however, denies it. During al that time the M-Stores have been under his supervision and impossibly have been used for other purposes. He surely would have noticed that.

During the day gossips were getting more and more extreme. That’s why a meeting was held urgently. I was allowed to attend it because after that I had to inform the other test subjects. It was the weirdest meeting I ever attended. Dr. Tachikawa was sitting opposite me. I could not help watching him. He must have noticed that. Nevertheless he neglected me completely. I could see on his face that he does not sleep too well. Although he looked ten years older he seemed to be happy. Has he fallen under the spell of all those developments he had made or is he preoccupied by Jessica? 

Dr. Hamilton opened the meeting. “Time is over and done with it. All this is of great danger to the belief and the continuation of our project!” I have never seen such a serious director. He severely looked at us one by one and continued. “Each one of you knows exactly what I mean.” Dr. Tachikawa played with his tea. Did Dr. Hamilton really look at him longer than the rest of us? Or did it seem so? “The gossips going about of the corrupted M-Store,” Dr. Hamilton continued, “...are absolutely unfair and not based on facts. As a matter of fact an M-Store is not a floppy disk. You cannot allow yourself to add and delete memories on it as you please. That is completely nonsense. It is quite clear to me that something is wrong with this M-Store. Probably it happened during the extraction.” Steward suddenly sat straight like he was struck by lightening and stared at Dr. Hamilton with big eyes. “But there is no proof of that.” Dr.Hamilton gave a signal to Steward he should wait in order giving any reaction. “Such gossips are very threatening to our company and should be eliminated immediately. And also we have to make sure that in future there will be no reasons any more for such imaginations. Today the M-Store concerned will be destroyed and tomorrow morning we delete Steward’s implant.”

The members of the board were talking around the subject. It was hard to follow all conversations at the same time but most of them agreed upon Dr. Hamilton. Except Steward. “With all respect…” he started with a loud voice to be heard between all the loud talking. “I do not see any reason for removing my implant!” Steward looked like a child being kept under house arrest for no reason. “Dear Mark,” Dr. Hamilton replied in a friendly but authoritative way, “...don’t you think that your implant is in your head way too long? Your MESI is one of the first proto-types and you know for sure that it is very unhealthy to keep it in your head for such a long time. Whether you agree with me or not: Tomorrow morning we will take it out.” Steward, broken into his spirits, simply gazed in front of him. Dr. Hamilton’s words struck me as well. ‘Coss just like Steward I have a first type of MESI. Could it be dangerous? Does my implant also need to be removed? I do not hope so!
