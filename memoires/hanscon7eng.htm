From the top of the hill there is a beautiful view to all directions. A lot of bushes, softwoods against the hills and broad-leaved forest beneath. If a glider approaches, we dive in some bushes. We overview where we’ve walked. With some effort we trace the track from the past days. We see movements at the spot where we’ve slept. With sharp vision we see red and white figures. Our sleeping spot has been discovered! How is that possible? The only possibility seems to be the laptop. So we search for programs that might be into it. Now the laptop seems to registrate all my body functions and movements. Braintec could follow me till Heather pulled the wires out of my body. Now it is completely disorganized. After fifteen minutes we get the functions back online. We arrange it this way that we seem to move to the east. Yes, the misguiding seems to work. The red and white figures really go eastbound. We shaked them off for a while.

How to continue? In the other directions is nothing else than forests. In the distance we see a small plane departing. There has to be an airport. Is that a way to escape or is Braintec there as well? We just try it. With a compass (I always take that and a knife with me when traveling) we enter the woods. No path, so that goes slow. After two hours plodding we find a sand path heading almost the right direction. After another hour we hear the sound of an engine behind us and we dive in the bushes. It is a Landrover with a trailer for a glider.  We are on the right track! The sand path is long. When the evening comes we still can’t see the airport. There is still enough to eat for dinner and we make it comfortable this evening together.

At five o’clock in the morning we wake up because everything is getting moistly. we took a necessary walk at dawn. The breakfast is our last food. After a four hours walk we hear a small planet flying right above us. The airport can’t be far now. After another hour we suddenly see it through the birches and beeches. Our enthusiasm disappears quickly when we see a red and white car between the small planes. Braintec is too quick for us. We craw towards a hangar and put on some pilot’s clothes we find hanging there, above our own clothes.

From here we can hear what the Brainteccers are saying. They offer the pilots some money to look for us. One pilot says to a colleague that he finds it too dangerous because he will fly alone and he needs all his concentration for flying. We walk towards him and promise him to search if we may fly with him. He finds that an excellent idea.

After thirty minutes we depart. Heather could buy some provisions in the airport shop. We soon say that we see ‘ourselves’ walking down there. We give the exact locations where we must be according to the laptop. East from the sleeping spot. The pilot passed it on to Braintec through his radio. The response there is enthusiastic because it compares so well to their other data. The pilot has his reward and set course to his destination.
