I was back at school and we were talking about the Second World War. We were asked to bring things connected to this war, which us. Most of the children brought coupons for food, some brought an article from a newspaper and Joep had a real Star of David, belonging to his uncle. We all looked amazed at this yellow star. I brought the little spoon from my grandmother with me. This wasn’t a regular spoon. Granny gave me this spoon last week, when we spoke about the war. She told me, that this spoon had saved her from starvation. When there was nothing left to eat, she swapped this spoon for a bag full of. It was a silver spoon, definitely worth a lot of money. After the war, she was able to get her spoon back, in exchange of the same amount of potatoes.  

I understood how important this little spoon was and I told everyone about it at school. They laughed at me and didn’t believe my story. Back home, I suddenly couldn’t find the spoon. I searched for it everywhere. When I told grandma, she wasn’t angry at me. It was all just a story, she told me. That spoon hadn’t saved her at all. In reality, she swapped all her silver cutlery for a small bag of potatoes and she had never seen it again after the war. But she did find this particularly small spoon behind the stove. I could clearly see how the loss of this spoon affected her. But I couldn’t understand it. Why had grandma lied to me? My mother explained me later, that it had been very hard for granny. She needed stories to maintain. Even when the war was over. Otherwise she couldn’t deal with reality. Wouldn’t it be perfect, if she had met a farmer like that? I have never fully understood. You aren’t supposed to lie, are you?

I have never found the last spoon and I have always felt guilty for it. Even the fact that grandma lied about it. I know for sure some children stole it out of my bag.
