Back in Tokyo, Paul parked the car in a backstreet. Two bold Japanese (yes, those exist) came out of a house to threw Rosanne and Adrian easily over their shoulders and bring them back in. Paul and I followed them. I waited patiently in a room with rice papered walls, while Paul prepared the M-Stores. A smiling and bowing woman brought me some tea. It took long before Paul returned. “Yes sorry,” he said, “but I had to arrange some things for you before you can go to the bloodbank." He comforted me by explaining extensively how the FBI would deliver the Canadian into the hands of the Japanese intelligence. And Kodjiro, I wondered. According to Paul he wouldn’t be of any danger: he would have plunged himself into deep debts at the jakuza, and the FBI is satisfied when he loose his honour and one finger...

We went out for some food, and Paul told me endlessly about his ranch, and how much he missed it. His stories were so colourful and detailed that I started to miss that ranch as well. Now and then he looked at me tenderly. I didn’t know how to respond, and broke the tensions with jokes. But from the inside I was glowing from desire.

The bloodbank was a piece of cake. The Canadian was there, but Kodjiro wasn’t. He took Rosanne and Adrian’s M-Stores a bit amazed but very delighted, and mentioned that I would go far in "the organization". After which I walked out of the bloodbank, to the car where Paul was waiting for me. While we drove away I saw about 8 or 10 police cars arriving at the entrance with shrieking brakes and dozens of policemen with guns and bulletproof jackets rushed into the building.

"Are you going with me to Portland?" Paul asked in the car. I was shocked by his vigour – he wouldn’t let the grass grow under his feet. "To Braintec?" I asked superfluously. Paul had to do some business with dr. Hamilton. Before I was aware I said yes. Paul carefully took my hand and kissed it, after which he gave me this piercing look and said: "I’m glad you do." We drove to my guesthouse to pick up my suitcase, which was hardly unpacked, and drove to the airport. We had to wait a couple of hours for the next flight. Paul was reading a newspaper, and I laid my head on his shoulder and fell asleep immediately. We didn’t say much during the trip to Portland. There was a relaxed silence and we enjoyed each others company.
