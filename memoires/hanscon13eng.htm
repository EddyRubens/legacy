Nothing worth mentioning happens the days after that. That doesn’t mean it wasn’t fun. On the contrary; Heather and I had a wonderful time. We visited some tourist attractions inside Tokyo and short trips to the countryside. Spring in Japan is marvelous. There’s even more in bloom than in Holland. At the countryside, tradition and modern civilization go hand in hand often. Just beside a mechanized arable farm is an old monastery.  After giving a donation they would predict our future. We both got some kind of cylinder with dozens of sticks in it. We had to shake it that hard that one stick fell out. That stick made our prophecy. Heather could handle it quit soon. “You will marry within a short period of time”, was her prophecy. “That definitely not what I had planned” Heather responded. Then it was my turn. It wasn’t easy. Either all sticks went back inside the cylinder or several sticks came out at the same time. At the end I could manage. “You will conquer all your difficulties soon” the stick said. Should Braintec bring us even more problems?

We went to the theater or music events almost every night. Both Japanese traditions and the more western kinds of arts are present in Tokyo. You get used at the Japanese appearances quit soon. They love to help after you greet them politely. 

There wasn’t much to refuse. Dude to their standoffish we were seldom invited to enter buildings. Nobody forced to get in touch with us. We couldn’t stay for ever. My boss expected me and Heather had to continue her study. We made reservations for our flight home. Two days before the journey to home we finally received a message, at a place where we didn’t expect it. In a small town in the countryside where we were having lunch, a woman carrying a red and white Braintec file headed towards us. She asked us if she could take us to a small Braintec office in a small town nearby. We refused that. Heather and I have discussed before what to with such a request. We came up with the idea to meat each other the following day at the terrace of a teahouse near a temple in Tokyo. That is a neutral spot in the presence of a lot of other people. And we know the area there. The Japanese sustained after huge protest. That night we invite the Japanese artist to enter our room. We bought a lot of nice food and tried to make a traditional Japanese meal. The guest don’t recognize it as Japanese but the like it. It is very cozy. We hear a lot about things going on inside the Japanese art world.
