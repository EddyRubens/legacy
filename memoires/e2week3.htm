It seems like somebody’s put a schedule together for visiting hours. Everyday a few people come by to inquire how I’m doing. Some only stay for a short while, like Dr. Hamilton, others stay longer. Most of my time I spend with Jessica. When she’s done taking the daily tests, she takes me for a quick walk in the garden in a wheelchair. This morning we saw a squirrel there. We chat a lot and it’s like we’ve known each other much longer. It’s nice to have a friend while I’m here. It makes my discomfort a lot more bearable.

Dr. Tachikawa came to play chess with me three times. Completely unfair of course, because someone recovering from brainsurgery is bound to loose miserably. During our third game of chess he let me glue one of my electrodes on his shoulder, to monitor his tentions.

“Only when you are able to win, you will be fully recorverd.” he says everytime I use the damage in my lobes as an excuse for a stupid move. Who knows, that might happen real soon because he is a lousy player. Unless he is doing it on purpose to give me a chance.
