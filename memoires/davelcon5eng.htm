Stayed up until three a.m. talking to Roseanne, smoking cigarettes, which I have never smoked.  I told myself never to stop,but the stress has been classified as "smoke alert" in my book, so I've taken to them.  And the fact that Roseanne is one of the most amazing conversationalists and thinkers I've come across is not a small part in my reasoning to stay up late and ask question after question about her long and experimental journey at Braintec.

I'm not sure what all she said, but I do remember some very interesting tidbits.  Like, she was found on the ferry from Vancouver, and that's partially the reason that she stayed at Braintec.  It started out as a friendly invitation, but then she soon realized the scientific importance, and, like a soldier or a missionary answering the call to a higher duty, she enveloped herself in the process. 

It's amazing that a woman of such beauty and intelligence, and so young, would fight the passions that she must have for other things in life, and still dedicate herself to such an ambiguous, but exciting project.  I feel safer knowing that I've made the right decision.

We talked most of thenight about these "hallucinations" that I've been having, and we came to a startling conclusion.  It must result from my failure to accept the process.  The memory cache that I am supposed to be building is fragmented, simply because I am too shocked and unwilling to "give myself up" to the process of memoric organic growth.  I think that's how it is written in the pamphlets and white papers of the science foundation. 

My dual memory and "othered literary narrative process" must come to me, whether I want it to or not, when it comes.  When it does come, I am supposed to be recieving it as an act of concentration and release, but up until now I have been trying to run away from the differing perceptions of the world, as received through the mind chip.

With that conversation I walked home, taking the long way, walking all the way up the Broad street to the Milly Tavern, seeing it was closed, walking a little further, and cutting across the Mayor's lawn to get home to my apartment off campus.  I asked for the apartment off campus, because I am a recluse and afraid of people.
