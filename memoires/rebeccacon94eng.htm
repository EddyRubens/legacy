When I returned to my room, everything that belonged to Carmen was gone. I couldn't believe how fast they did things here. When they wanted someone to disappear, they did it and they did it well. As I stood staring at her empty half of the room, I felt a hollowness in the bottom of my stomach. Even though I knew that she was OK, this was like a final notice they acted upon in order to let me know that I wouldn't be seeing her anymore. That's only what they thought!

I only had a short time before someone would come looking for me since I was due for an upload. I went into the bathroom to hide the 'Left Wing' pass and the photo copies I took of the baby files. I pulled out the Band-Aid box from inside the medicine cabinet. I pulled out some of the Band-Aids and shoved in the pass and the folded up photo copies. Then, I put all the Band-Aids back in the box. I was proud of the hiding spot I found. I cleaned off the make-up from my face with cotton and astringent, and flushed it down the toilet when I was done. I brushed my hair into a ponytail and walked out of the bathroom.

I was shocked and startled to see Sheila sitting on Carmen's barren bed. Good thing I flushed the toilet. No telling how long she was in my room. If I were to just walk out, she might start to wonder what I was doing in there that whole time. If she was here for the pass or the copies, I wouldn't want her to get any ideas as to where my hiding place was. I could tell she knew something was going on, and she was here to confront me. "Rebecca..." She said. She paused for a minute and looked down at her feet, as though she was trying to find a specific way to say something. "I understand that you probably have a lot of questions that need to be answered...so I spoke with Dr. Hamilton....He agreed that this would be a good time for you and I to sit down and discuss all your...well,...present issues."

Because she paused so many time in so few sentences, I wasn't sure if that was the end of her opening speech. So, I waited for a minute without saying anything. It wasn't until she raised her eyebrows at me with that knew she was waiting for me to respond. "I'm not quite sure what your getting at Sheila." I said to her. I could tell by the look on her face that she was disappointed that I didn't sit down and spill the proverbial pot of beans. Now she had to come up with a way of asking for the information she wanted. "Well," She began. "I suppose I thought you might be wondering what happen to your friend Carmen, for one. You have noticed all her belongings were gone, haven't you?"
"Yes, I noticed." I replied. "Did you take them?"
"Oh, heavens no." Sheila quickly answered. She continued with some crazy story about Carmen. Her explanation for Carmen's recent absence was supposedly due to a 'ruptured ovarian cyst.' She said since Carmen and I were both found in the wrecked car with Carmen in the driver seat, it was assumed that her 'abnormally large cyst' must have ruptured while she was driving. "The pain must have been so horribly excruating that she lost control of the car." She explained. "You must have blacked out...then blocked all memory of it."

I stood there looking at her. "Um...yea..." was all I managed to mutter. Poor Sheila. She was a horrible actress. So dramatic. Too dramatic. "Well," She added, "Carmen was rushed to another emergency care facility that Braintec is affiliated with. She is recovering well from the minor surgery that she had there. Dr. Hamilton informed me that she has called him and requested for her MESI to be removed. She will have the procedure tonight and will be going home when she has fully recovered."

That was it. That was the best excuse they could come up with. I told Sheila That I needed some time to absorb all the information she had just shared. She acted concerned. I decided to play her little game and told her that I would find her if I needed to talk. I went over to the door and opened it. It was my polite way of asking her to leave. She got the hint and stood up and headed for the door. Before she left she turned to me and asked, "Is there anything else bothering you?...Or...Is there anything you might,...I don't know... want to get off your chest?
I looked to the right for a second, frowned, and acted like I was thinking. Then I said, "No, nothing I can think of."
"OK," She said, "You know where to find me."

Once she left and headed down the hall a bit, I stuck my head out the door and called her name. Sheila turned around and faced me saying, "Yes?"
"Carmen and I are best friends..." I said. "Yes,....." she said again, only in a different tone. "Just seem funny...you know...that she never called me!" I said. Sheila got a real funny look on her face and I went back into my room. I couldn't let her have the last word on this one! I only hope I didn't screw things up for Carmen. I need to get hold of Kaleb and see when that other Agent will be coming for us.
