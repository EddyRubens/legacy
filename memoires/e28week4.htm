At nine thirty I was already waiting on this small bench. The plan I got yesterday, was leading to a park which many young Japanese usually visit. More than half of the visitors had bleached their hair. I was having great fun, while I was watching these teenagers. They also were looking for a way to be themselves.

At five minutes past ten an old woman came to me. She didn’t speak, but she motioned me to follow her. So I did. Three streets further down she went into a building. I was following her rather insecure. It was an office building and together we took the elevator. On the third floor she got out and pointed at a door left open. Then she returned taking the elevator down. I got scared when I pushed the door carefully. I had this inauspicious feeling. The room behind the door was pretty dark. I couldn’t see before one’s face. That’s why I couldn’t see the approaching arms suddenly grabbing me tight. Somebody pushed something filthy into my face and soon afterwards I collapsed. 

I woke up in a train compartment. There was this friendly looking man in front of me. I think he was an American. The rest of the compartment was empty. “I’m from the secret service,” the man said. “The FBI.” He showed me his pass. The photo was matching but I forgot to look at his name. Outside fields of rice were passing by. For heaven’s sake where are we going to? “You are in great danger, Rosanne,” the American continued. “They want to murder you.” Suddenly he caught my attention. Murder? Why me? “You cannot go back to your Japanese friend. He is not your friend. Each day you stay longer unless strictly necessary, could be your last day. From now on you’ll have to sleep somewhere else every night. These are the keys of the apartment where you will be tonight. You have been there just a while ago so you know how to find it. After that you’ll have to take care of different sleeping places yourself. You can pay for everything with this credit card.”  He gave me two keys attached to each other with a small ring and also put a credit card on the small table. I put them in my pocket, somehow insecure. “Don’t trust anybody. Not even your own instinct.”

“How is that possible?” I asked. “How can I take decisions?” For a moment my questions seemed to knock down the American. “Something is wrong with your head, dear Rosanne.” I don’t like it when people call me dear of beloved when I don’t even know them. “Even we don’t know whether you could be trusted.” This man was irritating me. I was fed up by the whole situation. “Oh, and what about me? How will I know whether you can be trusted?” “You cannot,” the American answered quietly. “You can only wish for it.” 

I was sighing deeply and looked outside, where, apart from rice-fields, I could see a building now and again. I was tired. Wished I could fall asleep and wake up in another reality. For instance at home, in Holland. Maybe it was time to go there. “I advise you not to take any uploads anymore. Sooner or later you’ll get one which is fatal.” The American was talking continuously while I was staring outside. “Try to stay in Japan anyway. As soon as you try and leave the country they will definitely make an end to you. They think you are a spy.” The American gave me some suggestions I could hardly remember. I had this bursting headache and the only thing I wanted was sleep. After that he stood up. He got out of the train but I could be seated for just a short while. At about half an hour the train would arrive in Tokyo again. A spy? I don’t see the point. This whole meeting was terribly confusing to me.
