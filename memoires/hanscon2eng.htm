Problems. It seemed to go so fine in the beginning. Everything was perfectly organized. They even added a train ticket Ajrnhem-Schiphol to the documents. The journey started Sunday evening but it wasn’t spectacular. The plane was half empty. A lot of people seemed to prefer not the fly during the war in Iraq. I assume it is even safer now with the extra security. I have asked if there are more passengers for Braintec but there aren’t. The convenience of an almost empty airplane is the opportunity to sleep stretched out using four seats.

Changing at Chicago. Couldn’t see much of the airport because it was dark. The second plane wasn’t full either. So I could sleep a bit more and arrived at Portland in good shape. A man with a red-white Braintec-sign waited for me at the exit. We left inside a red-white van. Strange, even now no other passengers. I asked the man several things concerning Braintec but I didn’t get much answers. He was only the driver, he said. I couldn’t see much from Portland. We drove through long stretched industrial parks and some suburbs with a lot of bungalows and every now and then a huge supermarket. Suddenly the Braintec-building showed up in such a business park.

The welcoming was neat. The whole building was decorated in red-white. Even the drinks! Milk and tomato juice at lunch, red and white wine at night. There wasn’t a division of the chambers, I’ve got a four persons room for me alone, kind of a three-star-hotel. Organizational structures were discussed. Everything is organized in this building. During off-days I can get money out of an ATM which will be compensated with my expenses. The guided tour wasn’t much. It is a hotel with operation chamber, surrounded by a garden. I haven’t seen other test subjects yet, neither during my walk in the garden after the tour. The garden has some nice red and white flowers. High fences separate the terrain from the rest of the business park. The porter avoided my question about the reason for these security manners. During diner I met the first other test subjects. No Dutch people, three American women and a Japanese couple. Non of them had surgery yet. So they can’t tell me their experiences.
