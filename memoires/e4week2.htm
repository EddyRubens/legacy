Dr. Steward is of the opinion that I am now a cyborg. Because my body, without free will as an intermediary, can be directly linked to binary codes or something. Strange man, that Steward. I don’t like his face and the way he comes across. He’s a boring man who seems to live only for his work. Fully committed to the research. There’s not much else that appears to interest him and he has absolutely no sense of humor. Only when he talks about the future of humanity, he seems somewhat radiant. In his world we have already evolved past the human stage and we’ll be looking for alternative living environments in space soon. He talks with so much passion about this that I hardly dare to talk back. That would be like forcing reality on a child that believes its teddy bear is alive. Even grown-ups have the right to dream. Especially grown-ups; they probably need it more. Sometimes I doubt Steward’s intelligence, although his knowledge and education prove that he’s way more clever than I am of course. But he comes across a little too simple sometimes. I find him boring. A real jerk.

I don’t know which of the things Dr. Steward claims are true and which aren’t. But it’s a fun idea. The word cyborg however has a bit of a strange ring to me. Like I was some sort of robot. For Dr. Steward these things are way more romantic. He’s proud to be part of these developments. I guess I might as well be proud myself.
