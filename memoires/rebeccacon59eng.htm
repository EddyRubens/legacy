On the way to Huber's, Carmen commented on how she thought Adrian was flirting with me. I told her he was just making conversation. "Rebecca, you can't even see it! He was hinting for you to invite him to dinner." She said. "Then," she continued, "you left an open ended invitation." When I asked her what that meant, she said I made it seem like I was interested in him. "I thought I was just being nice." I said. She just laughed and said "Yea Becka, a little too nice!" I laughed. I could see she was feeling better than earlier. I didn't want to spoil her mood but felt it was only fair to tell her about Kaleb and Megan. I gave her a quick run down of what had happened before I had woke her up. "Where could they be?" She asked. "I have no idea!" I said. "All I know is, we better never leave each other sight. It's too weird and it's too scary."

We drove the rest of the way in silence. I think we were both pondering what the future holds for us. I could see Carmen rubbing her pregnant belly. I broke the silence by saying, " I know everything will be OK after tonight!" Carmen just looked at me. I could tell she trying hard to believe it. When we reached the parking lot at Huber's, we both sat quietly for a minute. This was it. The moment of truth. We were either going to go in there and find out that it's all a scam, masterminded by Dr. Tachikawa, or we were really going to get some serious help. "You ready?" I asked Carmen. "Well,.........." she started to say. "Yea, me too." I finished.

We got out of the car and went into the restaurant. It was busy and crowded. Carmen and I were both wearing jeans and a sweater.  I looked over at Carmen and told her that I felt a little underdressed to be here. "Yea..." She said. Then some older woman in a horrible pant suit walked in the door behind us. She looked us up and down then huffed and shook her head at us. Carmen smiled and said something to her in Spanish. "Espero tú sufocar sobre el hueso de pavo." The woman looked at me then at Carmen and smiled. She acted as though Carmen was complimenting her. She ran her hand down the front of her ugly brown flowered shirt, then went over to the Bar. I asked Carmen what she said to the rude woman. "All I said to her was that I hope she choked on a Turkey bone." We both started to laugh.

The hostess came over to us and asked if we were lost. "No," I said. "We are meeting some people here, the reservation should be under 'The Justice Party." She went and looked in her reservation book and then signaled for us to follow her. On the way to our seats, she commented that we were the last of the Party to arrive and the others were already waiting for us. She made it sound like more than the two agents were there. When we got to the large booth, I realized why the hostess said 'they were all waiting.' There were two extra, unexpected, members to the party. When I saw the others, I looked at Carmen. I could see by the look on her face, she was just as shocked as I was.
