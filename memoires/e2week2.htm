When I woke up there was a gray rabbit on my bed. The animal looked at me with glassy eyes. Is this real, this rabbit? I wondered. Is it dead?

From experience I know that excessive morphine intake causes hallucinations. Carefully I tried to stroke the animal. It didn’t disappear! I wouldn’t have been surprised if my hand would have gone right through the rabbit. So it was really there and it wasn’t even afraid of me. A nurse entered the room. Her unexpected entrance startled me. I sat up with a start and the rabbit was gone. “I saw you were awake,” the nurse said. “I came right away to see how you’re doing.” How can I trust that this nurse is real? I asked myself. She might disappear as suddenly as the rabbit did.

“So, how are we doing?” She gave me such a stern look I thought I’d better answer her promptly. Even if I was talking to thin air. “Alright” I answered with great difficulty. My mouth was so dry! The nurse gave me some water that I drank through a straw. Could she read my thoughts? My mouth sucked the water up like cardboard and then I could speak more easily. But I had nothing to say. The nurse did; she began speaking animatedly while she inserted a thermometer in my mouth. “Everyone is so excited about you. You are the pearl of Braintec.” Before swine? I thought immediately after she said it. “Even Dr. Tachikawa, I have never seen him so enthusiastic! His eyes were sparkling.” The nurse rattled on and on and I found it hard to concentrate. “When he heard yesterday afternoon that you were getting a MESI he flew over immediately. He was in Tokyo, you know. Or Osaka, some meeting I believe. Well, isn’t that fantastic? When someone flies from one side of the globe to the other just for you? He came to see you before speaking to Dr. Hamilton. But you were sleeping. Oh, there’s the rabbit he brought you. It’s attentive, isn’t it? And still there are people who say he’s so cold. I don’t understand that at all. OK, he might be a little distant in relationships, but he’s terribly kind. You should count yourself lucky with all this attention.”

She probably rattled on for minutes after that but I stopped listening from the moment she put that rabbit back on my lap. The gray animal took up all my attention. So it was there but it was a toy. Apparently it was mine; I got it from Dr. Tachikawa. I’ve seen some pictures of him, on the internet and so on, but I don’t know him. He, apparently, is the man who invented the implants and their use. Must be a clever man. And polite because he is Japanese. He flew to Portland just for me. Well mostly for his MESI of course. 

But why a rabbit? Must be a Japanese custom. It’s a cuddly rabbit. By now I can tell she isn’t real, but it looks a lot like the real thing though. Her fur is really soft and she’s got a cute mug. I pressed her softly against my body while falling back to sleep.
