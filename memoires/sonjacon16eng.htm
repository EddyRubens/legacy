Seeing back all the test subjects was a fancy happening. Or should I say: partners in misfortune. We all had some kind of adventures, and we all had a lot to tell. But the biggest adventure was most probably the one of Hans and Heather: they were going to marry! Everyone was busy preparing the ceremony. It was so beautiful, the flowers, Heather in her white dress, Hans overwhelmed by happiness, and dr. Hamilton’s nieces as bridesmaids! Also nice to see Rosanne this way: I have known her as a spicy, tough lady, but during the ceremony she was sniffing as a sick seal...

It became a huge party, and we all became more or less drunk. Paul surprised me: he turned out to be a well-trained tango dancer and he whirled me across the dance floor as if I weighted nothing. "Do you have Latin blood or so?" I mumbled tipsy, while I was almost doing the splits. "No," said Paul, "but I do have red blood!" on which I was brought to pause at the side of the dance floor with a double Axel.

When it became light again and the birds started to sing we were the only ones left on the dance floor. As in a dream we danced on the sweet-voiced but spend voice of Sinatra. "When I saw you for the first time, I started to believe in faith.” Paul whispered softly in my ear. “I like you too,” I said. And we stood still in a deep hug. There was nothing and nobody else in this world than us. I wished this could go on for ever.
