In the City Center I saw a boy and a girl on a bench, kissing each other passionately. I never liked stickiness in public and usually I pretend not to see it. Today I couldn’t look away. The French kiss kept burning my retina; I had not enough power to stop it. I wished I was one of those teenagers. This thought surprised me. How could I wish to take part in something I dislike seeing? Something that irritates me so much? But it was true. I would love to become the boy or the girl who were lapping up the kisses. For today I would like to have a good kiss in the middle of the Van Woustreet, please, where everyone can see me. It happened to me in bygone days. After a school party, at the swimming pool or just somewhere in the City Center like these teenagers. I never cared about the frowning faces of bystanders. Even if my buddies’ hands moved under my T-shirt, I didn’t mind the presence of pedestrians.

Since I work as a test subject for Braintec, several trifles made me think about the past. Even after the sessions I constantly think about my memories. How were things in the good old days and do I wish to experience them again? Sometimes this center becomes a sort of psychiatric hospital where I’m supposed to rethink my life through therapeutic self analyses.

Do I wish to be young again? No, that’s bullshit. Though, even if you’ve passed the age of forty you can still be a passionate kisser lacking good manners. Or if you’ve passed fifty or sixty. I can imagine myself kissing as if my life depends on it when I’m over eighty. Or is it the daily job that resists me? Maybe I haven’t got the right character for a job with people above me. I want to rebel. Most probably it’s just the tension, the rashness that I desire. Not the French kiss.
