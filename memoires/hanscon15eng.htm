Don’t join the fight, says Heather. The information on your laptop seemed to be far too valuable. After sixty seconds Heather carefully peeps behind the stone. Nothing to see. Where is everyone? Well, then we can come out, I say. No, you don’t, answers Heather. But it all seems to be safe, I continue. See how you look, you really can’t show yourself this way. Only then I see I’m bleeding. Breaking free made to much pressure to the pods at my skin. Out of every pod flow little streams of blood. I look like one of the monsters shown at the temple walls. We try to see it as a symbol to get rid of Braintec’s bad spirits. The bandage Heather is carrying are just sufficient enough to cover all wounds. Heather cleans the wounds first with the water of the holy spring. That shall prevent me for infections, I say. With all we’ve done here we certainly enter the Nirvana, Heather thinks.

Now I can be seen but still my clothes can’t. The blood made some nasty spots. So we sacrifice my shirt to the holy stone. There is a temple shop nearby where we saw some textile hanging. We walk fast towards it. My naked chest isn’t that weird because the Japanese drummers have that as well. Inside the textile collection is very limited. It is a temple shop and they only have traditional Japanese ceremony clothes. Heather loves it and searches for a richly decorated bridegroom outfit. It suits you perfectly, she says. As I want to walk outside again, Heater pulls at the huge collar of my suit. Look there, she warns me. On top of a roof we see people with guns. They want to shoot us. The information we carry with us must be very important.

What to do next? First we wait. At the shop is a restaurant and we eat a lovely Japanese honeymoon meal that fits perfectly to my outfit. After this dinner the shutters are still present. There is someone with a gun hidden on top of another building as well. Isn’t that Paul? Isn’t he to be trusted either? It is better only to trust ourselves. We need a new plan. Fortunately that comes easily. The long honeymoon ceremony inside the temple has finished and the whole bunch of people slowly heads for our direction. We won’t be seen if we join this procession. I am already dressed for it. Now Heather needs some new cloths. I make her happy with a beautiful wedding dress I saw before. As a real bride and bridegroom we join the group at the moment they pass the shop. It turns out to be three couples to be married at the same time. That makes four including us. We are the most beautiful pair, we think. We have to act Japanese not to be conspicuous. We can handle that. We feel rather home here. Do we really have to leave Japan tomorrow? Outside the temple area are a lot of cars waiting to transport the whole group. We hold a taxi that looks like the wedding cars.

The driver understands how he has to join the group. The Japanese families don’t react at the fact we sneak into their procession. During this trip we see a car driving by with the people we saw with guns on the temple roof. Two minutes later another car drives by with Paul in it. We don’t know what he wants from us, so try not to pull his attention. The procession stops at a rich hotel. We ask the taxi driver to wait. Inside the hotel are shops, happily. We buy clothes and attaché suitcases where we store our wedding clothes. As hansom business people we walk out again. The driver is surprised by our transformation but doesn’t mention it.
