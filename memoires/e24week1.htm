I received a postcard with a special message from Dr. Tachikawa! A plane ticket to Tokyo! Reading on the postcard: “You’re welcome to join us whenever you wish, reg. K. Tachikawa”.

While I was walking through the building indifferently, Steve contacted me. “There’s a letter for you. Registered, so you’ll have to sign for delivery.” I walked with him, as fast as I could, to the postman waiting for me patiently. I like receiving mail. Especially when I have to sign for it. In that case it is something special. “And?” Steve asked. “Something special?” When I saw the envelop and the plane ticket I couldn’t imagine anything more exciting. Kodjiro loves me, I thought for a moment, but then immediately I skipped the idea about that. Instinctively I was hiding the ticket for Steve. “It’s a postcard,” I said. “A good friend of mine.” I directly ran to my room to examine the contents of the card. Actually the card is a bit weird. Tourist stuff. But the words on it are all that matters. No date written on the ticket. I found a telephone number requesting contact within three months to confirm a definite booking. Fantastic! This mail is surely something special.

I was thrilled. Nobody ever has sent me a plane ticket just like that. Okay, Braintec obviously, paid for my flight from Greenland to Portland, but that’s for business. If Kodjiro sends me something expensive like this here, I’m positive he likes me very much. Anyhow he wants to see me! Makes me feel so happy!

I was excited all day long. A few times somebody asked me why I looked so happy. I decided not to tell anyone about it. Dr. Tachikawa probably has another good reason why he prefers to have me around. This reason will only turn into a fight when they find out. Pronouncing his name already has created a taboo. I don’t mind sharing the great news with somebody. In the past I have said something casually. I did to Sally and much later also to Adelle. She exactly knows when something important is happening to me and then she questions me too much. I haven’t told them a bit. I’d rather keep it for myself for a while as long as I haven’t decided what to do with this ticket.
