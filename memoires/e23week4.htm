Lucas thought it a great idea to hypnotize me. He overheard the gossips about my misbehavior concerning the small spoons in the coffee-room. He suggested recalling memories of my youth. ‘Coss Dr. Hamilton had this feeling something else was going on. He talked about a child-trauma which could be the cause of the schizoid psychosis. That could explain my delusions. When I told him that I never had bad experiences during my childhood, Dr. Hamilton doubted my words. It was obvious to him that most likely I had hidden away some unpleasant times.

Lucas was hoping to get things clear through hypnosis. It was alright for me. Now that I won’t get any uploads, I have nothing to do. He suggested doing this in my room. Nobody would disturb us there and there’s no place where I feel more comfortable. To calm down completely he switched off the main light, so we only saw the lamp on my desk. Relaxation was no problem at all. In no-time I sunk down into a relaxing fantasy, playing at the lake on a sunny afternoon. Together we went down the long stairs and at every step I went further back in time. Soon we entered the museum of my memories. Piles of boxes and drawers, filled with small and bigger things of my childhood. Heaped up against the walls. All information mixed through each other. A cacophony of voices. A collection like a kaleidoscope of visions, feelings and sounds. I wonder if Lucas understood the whole thing. Unbelievable for me. If my unconscious looks like that, I can imagine I would rather prefer to get rid of that.

Despite the chaos Lucas managed to guide me to the girl of six years old. Nothing was wrong with her. The anxiety for desertion and the traumas Dr. Hamilton was mentioning just a moment before, were not there at all. A happy girl fond of fantasies. So, nothing special. We also met my imaginary friends. I talked about my cuddly toys, my friend from Morocco I was going to marry one day and about my dog Bas. Bas always joined me on a holiday. During the trip I imagined that I was a dog myself and I was married to Bas. Together we would raise five puppies. They were born on the camping site. Instead of playing with other children, I was busy picking and collecting blades of grass while imagining living a dog’s life. I never walked with Bas so often as I did during that particular holiday.

Lucas was satisfied. Nothing wrong with this six-years-old girl. No alarming child-traumas threatening my health. Happy for that. Good to know that no unpleasant memories would show up which I cannot remember at all. Half an hour later I was scared to death. We never had a dog and I have never ever been to France with my parents! And definitely not on a camping site!

Quickly I searched for Lucas. He told me about synthetic memories. How those are often used to replace the real facts. Sometimes to fulfill desires and sometimes to explain things when the truth is too hard to understand. We decided to do another session tomorrow. More a free session this time. Just to walk around in my childhood, as he called it.
