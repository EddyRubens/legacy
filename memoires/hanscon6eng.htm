We walk relaxed as patient and guide beside the road. After a couple of minutes we hear noises behind us. They seem to have discovered our escape. The Brainteccers were effing and blinding because their car can’t pass the gate closed by the electric failure. Time to get off road. Hikers are too conspicuous. There is a path between two companies that ends at a little bush. Arriving there we hear children’s voices. We walk to there and reach a schoolyard. This seems to be the best place to get away, because there isn’t normal public transport. We say that we have a breakdown and ask if we can go with the school bus to find a garage. There isn’t a school bus, but in a while parents and nannies will arrive to get up the children. We ask a nanny if we may join her. The Philippine girl doesn’t speak much English but she takes us with her. I have unbound myself. The ever registering laptop is still at my back. That isn’t easy at the car seat. After thirty minutes driving we are dropped at a garage in a rich villa neighborhood in Portland. Heather wants to rent a car. The man from the car rental has the local radio on. The news about two escaped criminals. The description resembles us perfectly. We aren’t safe yet. In the distance I see a red-white van heading this way. Getting out of here. At a company building behind the garage a driver is having troubles getting his cargo into his wagon. We help him and ask for possibilities to get away from there. He confirms that renting a car is the only possibility, but we can drive with him to a truckers bar in another suburb of Portland.

That turns out to be far away. It is a two hour drive and at the border of cultivation. At that place are busses and taxi’s driving through. The TV is on at the bar. Two pictures are shown that are very familiar to us. They are taken from the Braintec website! So we head the other way. Through the backdoor into nature. No map, no compass but the sun is shining and we can find directions by sunlight. Away from the buildings and we will see where we strand. We could get us some fat American snacks in the road house to get rid of our hunger at night. Slept outside underneath our luggage. Slept close to Heather to fight the chill. I can imagine worse. This morning quit fit again. Heather is removing the laptop from my body. It is too unhandy to walk with it. It is attached to my body with wires. We improvise band-aid to stop the bleeding. It is all painful but it works. Another Big-ofeverything to eat and on our way. We are going up a hill. At the top we rest to overview the area. I can write this on the laptop.
