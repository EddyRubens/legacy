Ireland

I am early awake on this chilly winter day. The sun is just rising above the sea and the tops of the Wicklow Mountains are coloured red. I decide to have a lazy, long morning with a huge breakfast, then a bit of reading and after that a training loop. My culinary memory is searching for the correct spoiling of my taste buds. I have almost all the ingredients for a pure Irish breakfast in the fridge. Bread in the toaster or I’ll make a french toast. Worries for later, first I’ll sneak under my blanket again.  

Banging on the door. I don’t have a doorbell, a cottage in the field doesn’t need one. The several small scraggy dogs seem to wait for my movements before they loudly show the early visitor their displeasure. Great, I shall feed you again one day... very brave are you. It aren’t my dogs. But all the compartments and corners in my garden seem to give them enough shelter for the night, and my ginger heart for food in the morning. A lovely life for an irish dog, believe me.

When I reach the door the barking has grown to a hysterical concert and I am afraid that whoever it might be, that person is already on the run towards his car and already half way at the mountain... back to the village. But in spite of what I expected I found a young fellow. back against the wall, big cape a bit protecting ... cap diagonal on his head and in spite of the cold, lightly sweating.

Dr Lucas??? Yes, that's me. i answer while I push away those hyperventilating creatures with my foot...  

Delivery for you, Urgent. Sign here please and the thumb-marked clipboard with crumpled paper is pushed beneath my nose. The crowd has replaced it’s attention to the open door and while I sign the paper a brave one comes in the house. But when the young man leaves all these pekinese-like run as japanese torpedoes after him.  

I shut the door and look at the cover. Overnight delivery URGENT from the USA. well that seems to be important... first some tea.

While the kettle is sizzling I open the cover... a plane ticket, and a short description how to find your way in ... Portland. Please be at the gates at 11:30. I look at the clock. 8:15 Jeeh. Those fellows are weird. How could they manage that, in such a short time, in Ireland.

Okay take it easy. What should I do with this? No time to call my secretary, she’ll wrangle me because she has probably been out al night as usual. And when I call her right now she might think it’s a proposal, or at least a suggestion...

My girlfriend is also still asleep, but she will follow me if I dare to leave without a serious farewell. So I hastily pack some things in my small backpack. I put on some warm clothes and start my motor. There is sufficient time I assure myself. It is only a one and a halve hour’s ride, the road is dry so that makes it easier. Passport, money, credit card. A book for on the plane. Tea, I want some tea, but the electric kettle has shut itself off ages ago and I decide that it would take too long to heat it again. I have to do without it. As quick as I can I put on my leather clothes and leave towards the other site of town. Susan is still half asleep and wants me to keep her company. No, I can’t, plane, 2 hours before departure, I’ll call you when I’m at the airport. Love you. Kiss

And I rush away. The road is empty. Saturday morning and with the speed of light I can make it in 1 hour and 20 minutes. Not bad for this old man. There is nobody at the desk so I can check in with only hand luggage without problems. Shall I do some tax-free shopping. Nah, all rubbish, but I do find a nice book. Men in Dirty T-shirts. intriguing title so i take it.

Time flies and I have hardly had a cup of tea and a cigarette when I hear the boarding call. How long for Portland, actually? I will notice and I do some exercises to cross the non-smoking time. It always works. Few people on the 747-400 so I can make it comfortable. An empty chair on both sides. This is going to be fun.
