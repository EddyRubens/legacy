It had all gotten too much for me. We had left the uploader in the dark apartment and we were without upload almost twenty hours. The withdrawal symptoms were a disaster. Everything felt empty and cold. And my own feeling, my identity, seemed to be out of reach. I desperately even tried biting an M-Store into pieces with my teeth. As if that would be of any help.

At least Adrian was in the same bad condition. I’ve never ever seen him so miserable. He was walking up and down and once in a while he was grumbling something. “Maybe the idea of bungee-jumping wasn’t such a bad idea after all,” I tried to cheer him up. He looked up for an instant. “In any case something should happen,” I continued. “We cannot stay in this room forever.”

Finally Adrian was able to discuss the matter. He had the same headache and was having problems on concentration too. It wasn’t easy. After talking nonsense he suggested to visit Kodjiro. He owed him a lot of money and seemed to find it difficult spending money from my credit-card. While I had gotten the plastic thing from Mr. Paul and wasn’t paying myself. Out of all people, from the FBI. They discouraged me not to stay close to Dr. Tachikawa. So now what? I couldn’t help thinking about the simple idea that the road to Kodjiro was the road to a new upload. And maybe I missed Kodjiro. Adrian is really very kind; he is like a brother to me, I never had. But I’ve never felt such feelings I have for Kodjiro for him. Again I was asking myself whether Kodjiro could be trusted, although due to my temptation for uploads I finally agreed. Together with Adrian it should be safe.  

Kodjiro was totally happy to see us. Shortly afterwards he asked me strictly where I had been during all that time. “To pick up Adrian,” I lied simplistically. “He can sleep here too, can he?” He agreed a bit grouchy. At least my sleeping-mattress which was left in my rucksack for weeks could be of use now.
