I have been intensively trained in the guidance of new test subjects. It is, above all, very important to give faith and to be trusted. Besides that I give explanations where necessary and I give people the feeling that they are welcome. I understand the importance of these goals, it all seems logical. But I really don’t see how to realize this. How can I reassure confused or worried test subjects when I am also confused every now and then? I haven’t forgotten the epileptic seizure and such. I can still become frightened when I think that it might be possible that the colors disappear again one of these days. And I definitely don’t want to lie about this. How could they ever foist this off on me?

In spite of that, it still attracts me, to guide new test subjects. Before I decided to become an artist, I considered getting a Diploma in Education. Teaching seems to be fun and is maybe not so different from guiding. This afternoon the first group of new test subjects will arrive. It is a group of five; three men and two women. Their planes won’t land at the same time, so I’ll get them, one by one, under my wing. I’ve read their dossiers well. One woman, Sonja Boutoille, is from Belgium. Her name sounds Walloon, but even then she still might speak Dutch. I’ve communicated in Dutch regularly on the Internet and by e-mail but I haven’t spoken it since ages. Except those few times I phoned home. If I make a quick calculation, I easily get one and a half year if I count my time in Greenland added to those ten weeks here at Braintec!  I really look forward to talk to Sonja in Dutch, if she speaks that language.
