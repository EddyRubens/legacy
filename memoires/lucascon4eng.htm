State site

The flight was fast and with good service. The landing in Portland, with a mellow temperature of 12 degrees nice, and still so early in the morning. actually I don’t have any address of Braintec but I suspect someone will take me up. If not, well then I will take a hotel. I have done some traveling in the past, and believe me, if you survived Afghanistan in 1980 they have to come up with more serious trouble to get you stressed. 

But no worries. I see some familiar glasses, well the black is very contrasting with the white skin. I guess the long observations, the arctic area and the winters in Holland has to be the cause for that.

So you are Rosanne? Nice to meet you. I see several reactions in her at the same time and I decide to stop my observations for the sake of politeness. It is not polite to look so deep into people, and still there is the NLP training that diagnoses her as a strongly kinetic person. fast and skilful hands... A bit stressed, and so am I.

I had everything well organized, before I entered the lions cave, first to overview the situation. But I had thought it would be Jessica or some other responsible person to get me up.  

Fortunately I have the excuse of smoking so we could search for a place at a table. Coffee please I say and while she walks away gallantly I roll a cigarette. Aah, that tastes well after a 9 hour flight and fortunately a nice nap. I smell the Folgers, long time since and I see her waiting. We chat for some minutes about the weather, the flight.  

Tell me a memory you have recalled through the module please I ask her, I want to know how it works without asking directly. And she keeps on telling me. In detail, just how I wanted to hear it. Not only a short note as a flat postcard.  

She’s somewhere else while we walk to her car. So much different than I thought she would be. No chatterer. No over-enthusiastic pep-talk. Is she tired, diverted or so. Too little information to come to a conclusion. i look around me while we drive the I-205 to the south. I didn’t know we were so close to Vancouver. At the radio some cheerful weather report, high 40-ies, low 50-ies.

In the leaflet I read the following, probably most important facts about Portland 

  • Portland has the highest per capita consumption of gourmet coffee bean in the US according to the New York Times. 
  • Portland has more microbreweries per capita than any other city in world.
  • 462 vineyards in state – 47 wineries within 100 miles of Portland.

Well that is the kind of information I really needed. and in the meantime i speak to Rosanne, who opens up and responses for the first time. I tell her that I have lived in the Netherlands for 4 years, in Dordrecht and that draws her horns out. We exchange some unimportant details and opinions of Holland, the tax department and so on... and before I know it we drive at Braintec’s park lane. Nicely maintained. The sun is shining and I feel cheerful and lively. No tiredness from the flight.

We are heading the entrance, the glass reflexes both our figures. How different will it be within two weeks. I know I will understand what’s at the other site by then, experiences, maybe friends. Nothing will be the same again. Every stone we turn on takes us a secret, the fantasy loses and do we truly get wiser?

Enough wanders, I cross the threshold......
