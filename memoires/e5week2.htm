With mixed feelings I underwent my second re-experience today. The first one was really impressive, but seeing the real vase was such a disappointment. Dr. Tachikawa guaranteed me that this would not be the case today. It’s not possible to tell what kind of memory it is before it is uploaded because that will spoil the end results. What has been made clear is that it won’t be a painful, unpleasant or awkward memory. During my first series of sessions the memories to be relived will only be the most banal and trivial experiences anyway. Still, it’s exciting!

The team welcomed me enthusiastically and it seemed like they were all looking forward to it. I sat in the chair again, remembering the routine. The procedure was carefully explained again and I may have nodded in agreement a bit too much. It’s not as if I’d forgotten how it was done. The elaborate explaining is probably obligatory, like the gestures stewardesses make when the safety regulations are explain in an airplane. In a one to one situation you can’t really stare through the window looking bored though. Hence my polite nodding. After the relaxation exercises the reliving came faster than I expected. I don’t know if it took longer before the upload started yesterday but today it took me a little by surprise. Suddenly it got real bright. A burning heat on my skin, but not unpleasant. Colors formed before my eyes as I kept them firmly shut. I tried to see images in the colors before me but I couldn’t make anything out of it. But I did experience contentment, a restful feeling that was quite pleasant.

Time seemed to be standing still while it lasted not more than a second. Then suddenly it was over. “I don’t know,” was the first thing I said. “Take it easy, give it some time.” I was encouraged to remain seated for a while. Then the questions came. I explained what it had been like for me and that I hadn’t seen any pictures, like the vase. Big smiles appeared on the team member’s faces. Despite my feeling of failure I was on target. Not all memories come as images, they explained to me. This was a re-experience of relaxing in the sun. My coin dropped at this explanation. Of course! I had been looking for the memory of an object or an occurrence. You can’t manipulate the upload then.

So simple yet so complex. Maybe sitting in the sun, relaxing in real time is just as complex as this memory, but I’ve never realized that before. It was nice. Sitting in the sun is lovely of course, but the memory was actually better. I think I felt happy, a type of contentment that borders on completeness. I felt complete; nothing else was needed to make me feel good. Memories like that they can upload with no limit, as far as I’m concerned.
