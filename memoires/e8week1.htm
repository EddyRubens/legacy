My first extraction has to be delayed because my menstruation wouldn’t stop. For some reason Dr. Hamilton thinks it’s not wise to extract or upload while I’m having my period. It doesn’t make a difference to me. I was really bursting with impatience to have my first upload, but not for this extraction. There is some curiosity of course and I do make the most divided impressions about the course of such an operation. But I’m not looking forward to it. Everything is going so fast; we hardly had the first uploads and now it’s time for the extractions. I wouldn’t mind if it goes a bit slower.

To be honest; I have been cheating today. The little bit of red fluid that I’ve lost this morning can hardly be called menstruation. It was so easy to create some spare time that I couldn’t neglect the opportunity. So it was a little white lie that brought me a day off for myself. I guess I’m not ready for this emotionally. I find the idea that they shall extract something out of my brain far more scary than when they put something in it. My memories will only be copied so I won’t lose them. But still. You never know if they accidentally remove something permanently or if there’s something else going wrong. And what will the other test subjects get to know about me if they recall my memories? Dr. Steward says that I can decide for myself how much memories I allow to extract, as in a normal hypnosis session. But I don’t believe that. I remember the look on Dave’s face very well, when I recalled his trip in the Grand Canyon National Park. I could tell the team members more about his trip than he cared for.

This day-off went on without any specialties. I have been lazy reading my book and have been to the park. My menstruation flannel won’t work tomorrow so than my first extraction will definitely go on. That is fine with me; I guess I will only get bored if I’ve got another free day. And Jessica is finally being nice to me again. I’m afraid we will never be as close as we used to be. That’s a pity. At this moment I am quite happy that she behaves normally.
