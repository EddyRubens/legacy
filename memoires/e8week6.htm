Where is my prince? That man who loves me unconditionally and without limits? The one who translates all my youthful indiscretions and clumsy attempts to find structure in my life, into charm? Who smiles at all my disgusting habits and who pushes me against the door to love me passionately? Why isn’t he here?

I immediately believed my mother when she told me that Santa Claus doesn’t exist. It was too unpredictable that someone could become so old. A man of that age could no longer foster my fantasies. Even though I was very shocked through her explanations and felt uncertain for a while. But when I mentioned that the amount of Christmas presents did not increase, I did no longer mourn over his sudden death.  

But no matter how often I’ve been told that the prince on his white horse doesn’t exist; I have never believed it. Of course I pretend to know better. I have learned through the years how to pretend that I shake off my daydreams and claim to know better with a smile. But I always keep my fingers crossed. Once the fairy will come for me and she will show me the way to my prince with her magic wand. Then the long and lonely days comes to an end. All my misfortune will melt as snow and my happiness will be of pure gold. My prince will get me out of here. Away of this cold research center. Far away from all those white coats. Better today than tomorrow.
