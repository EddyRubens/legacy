It's almost 5pm right now. I just returned from my first day testing. Sheila came by about 7:30 this morning to pick me up. Luckily, I took a shower last night and set my alarm to wake up early. I wonder how many days I will have to go through tests before they decide if I am an acceptable test subject.  

Today I went to this room with Sheila. I am guessing it was her office. She asked me a bunch of questions about why I wanted to do this and what I hoped to get out of this experience. Then she asked me a whole bunch of personal questions. She made me feel a bit uncomfortable. I really didn't see how any of it was relevant. I suppose there is a reason to all those particular types of questions.  

She was extremely interested in the fact that I had some phobias. Four to be exact. I have a fear of bees, (Apiphobia) fear of wasps, (Spheksophobia) and a fear of being stung by them. (Cnidophobia) These three are all related in a way. People thing I am joking or lying about it. But, my fear of them is so great that if a bee or wasp is anywhere near, I basically have a panic attack. It's uncontrollable. I have a feeling these phobias lead back to something that happened at childhood. I get all shaky and short winded just thinking about it. 

The forth phobia I have, I am a little embarrassed to admit to. It's called Automatonophobia. This one is the fear of ventriloquist's dummies, animatronic creatures, wax statues and anything that falsely represents a sentient being. How this came about, I am totally clueless. About five years ago, Carrie and I went to this party. It was sort of boring and one of the guys that lived in the house said he had this really cool collection, and asked if I wanted to see it. He wouldn't tell me what the collection was, but when I went into his room, I had one of my panic attacks. I completely shut down and this guy had to carry me back out into the living room. I was so embarrassed. His collection was all these little puppets and marionettes. I mentioned this fear to Sheila during our testing session. She kept saying: "I see...and how did you feel about that?"  I was getting a bit frazzled by her repetitive comments. I felt like telling her that I was starting to get annoyed. 

She gave me an hour break at 11:30am for lunch. I don't remember seeing a cafeteria here, so I hit up some vending machines and went back to my room.  I ate the small bag of Doritos, the giant, chewy, oatmeal cookie, (yummy!) and drank my Diet Coke. That took about 15 minutes so I decided to take a 30 minute nap then head back to Sheila's office. 

I spent the rest of the day with Sheila answering questions. She told me tomorrow I would be doing some physical testing and some different kind of mental testing. Finally something different.  

Carmen mentioned something about getting take-out Chinese food for dinner. While she is calling the restaurant, I am going to write Josh and see if I have any email from him. Then Carmen and I will sit near the front door to wait for our dinner to be delivered!
