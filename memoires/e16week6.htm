I was just starting my accompanying tour along the test subjects when Sonja called me to the small kitchen. Her voice sounded enthusiastic which made me curious. “Those test subjects can still wait for a while.” I thought and rushed towards her. On the table there was a huge flower bouquet. “From whom did you get……?” I continued but she shook her head and rapidly gave me this card into my hand. “For me?” My heart began to beat faster. Who was the one who spent so much money for me? Such a bouquet must have cost a fortune! It was my intention to open the cover very carefully but my fingers could not possibly do their work fast and at the same time carefully. So I had torn it into pieces. A card with much red, no doubt it was for Valentine and in this card was a handwritten poem:

 

I have somewhere surely
Lived a life of joy with you,
All is recall'd as we flit by each other,
Fluid, affectionate, chaste, matured,

It comes to me as a dream
I am not to speak to you,
I am to think of you
when I sit alone or wake at night

But matters not of dreams or real
It is the way I truly feel
And deep inside I know of love
And in the sky and up above

The web of our mind, like azure blue sky
Upon the water now caught by the eye.
And stately trees of emerald green
Echoing their past of things once seen.

 

 No sender. I turned the card again. Again no sender. Also I could not find a written clue on the pieces left who could have written such a sweet poem. Could it be….? I was afraid to think so but I desperately wished the card came from Kodjiro. “And?” Sonja asked who had been waiting patiently. “A very sweet poem, but no sender.” I reached to her the card. “Very beautiful.” she sighed. “Who do you think it could be?” Sonja raised her eyebrows. How would she know? Her eyes were glittering. “I think you got an admirer, Rosan, how thrilling!” Unfortunately I could not share her excitement. No need for an admirer, I want Kodjiro! I could not imagine that this gift came from him and also Sonja skipped his name. First of all he is much too busy, and secondly I never believe that he cares for those typical American traditions and thirdly… Again I felt that pain in my tummy. Thirdly he would have sent it to Jessica. I put the flower bouquet in the relaxation room. It’s up to them what to do with it.
