Finally 6:00 arrived. The day felt like and eternity. I have to admit that the walk through the garden really put my mind at ease. It is so beautiful there! I will surely go again!

I never thought I would be so happy to see Dr. Hamilton. He smiled at me when I entered his office and said, "Looks like you had a rough day, Kiddo!" I felt like saying, "No Shit, Sherlock!" But I just said, "Yea! You can say that again!" So, of course he did! I laughed in hope that it would make him stop!

He asked if I was ready for my upload? I was so excited I almost mentioned how much I love the ocean, but I stopped in time! Close call!

He walked me down to the room where I received my first upload. Steward was already there getting things ready. I was so happy, then I saw Sheila. She walked over and asked if everything was all right. I said I was just a little tired and would be fine. She said she was going to make an appointment for me to see her. I told her to just let me know when. I basically was just trying to get her to leave me alone!

When everything was set in place, the last thing I remember Dr. Hamilton saying was, "Prepare to enjoy!" Then I was there! It was an Ocean on the West coast. A place called Dillion's Beach. (From what the sign read.) It started out where I was walking down this paved road with a bunch of peaceful ocean front cabins on both sides. I think I must have came from one of them. I walked down the road a bit further when it went down in a hill-like fashion. When I got to the bottom I walked through the small parking area then right onto the sandy beach. It was so cold and windy. I could smell the salty water and hear the loud seagulls gawking and flying above me. I walked along the beach a for a while, watching people throw sticks to dogs and watching little kids play in the water. I remember thinking how cold it must be in that water. The little kids didn't seem to notice at all. That made me smile. I walked as far as I could until there was a large rock in the way. No problem I thought, I would climb it. I reached the top. It felt like I was on top of the world. It was marvelous! That was it.

When it was done, I wanted to do it all over again. Dr. Hamilton asked which upload I enjoyed better, The Jets or the Ocean? I told him there was no way I could compare the two. I wanted to leave so bad so that I could find Carmen. I was in much better spirits and had a feeling she found the gift and was too. Except, I had to sit and share every detail I experienced for my files. Dr. Hamilton mentioned that the experience I had was completely different from the other two people who received it. Apparently, the other two test subjects felt sadness and despair, and when they climbed the rock, it was because they were going to jump off it to end it all.

That was the last thing I would have thought while on top of that rock. And, come to think of it, why would Dr. Hamilton purposely give me what he thought was a depressing upload? I thought he liked me. I did laugh at his stupid jokes! O'well, I got something good out of it!

I am waiting in Dr. Hamilton's office. He said he wants me to see something, then he will let me go back to my room. I wonder what he wants to show me?
