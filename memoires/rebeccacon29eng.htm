It feels like every hour doubles while waiting for 6:00 to arrive. I did the rounds of the halls, got some juicy gossip, and I am still bored out of my mind!

I saw Carmen in the hall earlier this afternoon. I smiled at her and said, 'Hi!' She just stared at me. I'm not sure if she is still mad. I can't read her like I use to be able to. You know how you can tell if someone is happy or sad or scared or mad? Well, she wasn't showing anything. I decided to just leave her alone. I have been making the effort to stay out of her way. When she is ready for conversation she will come to me.

I did try to think of a way to make her happy. I took a short trip to town and found a small gift shop kind of store. I told the older, white haired and very round sales lady that I wanted to cheer up a friend but didn't know what to do for her. She suggested a card and a little set of chocolate monkeys. There were seven of them in a set. One for every day of the week. It said on the package:

Monkey's are the funniest animals around, and Chocolate is the cure for sadness. If you eat one Monkey day, It would surely bring you gladness!

I thought it was a little cheesy but I thought Carmen would get a kick out of it. The woman gift wrapped it for me for free and I headed back.
