Today some rich lady from California came for a visit. She got an extensive tour through the complex and afterwards went for lunch with Dr. Hamilton and Dr. Tachikawa. Jessica told the woman wants to have her personal memories recorded. She is willing to pay a lot for it. Now that she’s almost seventy, she is really afraid to lose her memory. I thought she was no more than fifty but a closer look at her hands and neck confirmed it. Those can’t be smoothed out so easily. If besides her memories her youth could be re-implanted as well I’m sure she wouldn’t mind to pay even more.

I’m curious as to whether she’ll get what she wants. I may not be an expert in judging who is suitable as a subject or not, but this woman strikes me as particularly unfit. Not only is she a walking wreck, she stressed that she hasn’t been feeling very good lately. Maybe she feels the end is near and is only trying to stretch things a little with the help of Braintec.

If I should ever have as much money as that I will definitely not spend it on something so crazy. I’d spend it on something fresh, one of those tourist trips through space for instance. I think it would be more enjoyable to have new experiences than to obsessively relive old ones. But, who knows, maybe she wants to have her memories recorded for posterity. Grandma’s special photo album.

I dislike women like her, really. Her personality even outdoes the penetrating smell of her perfume. From the moment she set foot in the building she acted as if she owned the place. Hovering through the halls like a queen. What I found just as deranged was the team member’s attitudes. They acted as if she was royalty too. If she would have asked them to paint the place purple they probably would have done it. I understand that the research that’s going on at Braintec is costing a lot, but how far can they go? I hope the team has enough backbone to refuse this woman. The other day I heard Dr. Hamilton moan about there not being much money left and the need for more sponsors. If she is accepted, it might say more about Braintec’s present state than about her.
