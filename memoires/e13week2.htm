It was a difficult journey, with two changes. I arrived at Schiphol totally exhausted. I was almost numbed with tiredness. The change-over in London was the worst. I had to wait one and a half hour. It was the afternoon there but it actually felt like very early morning.

To my complete surprise a large group of people were waiting for me at the Dutch airport. I had called my parents that I would be coming and my mother suggested she’d come and pick me up. I didn’t expect her to bring so many people. My dad, my sisters and their partners, my friend Jeanine, Karin, auntie Connie and uncle Joep. Just fabulous! I was dead tired but this warm welcome brought me back to reality. Jeanine had brought a Dutch flag and as we embraced she put it on me like a coat. Tears were running down my cheeks. It was such an emotional reception.

Once back at my parents’ house I soon had had enough of it though. It was nice to see everyone again and I really appreciated their coming, but I was incredibly tired. I just wanted to lie down. I hadn’t been able to get much sleep on the plane. I had been much too exuberant. After watching three crap movies I became tired enough to sleep, but then I needed to stay awake for the change-over. Coming home I was so tired that nothing meant anything to me anymore. And they were all going on about my health and to make things worse, even more members of my family came by to say hello. My mum had started organized a big welcoming party right after I called. Well meant of course but not what I wanted at this point in time. Defeated by lack of sleep I just sat staring into the distance. My ears ringing. Eventually I stopped caring and just crawled into the bed in the guestroom. As far as I was concerned they were welcome to party without me.
