It is oh so quiet without Kodjiro. He had gone away more often, but never so definitely. Any hope for him to come back seems impossible. It’s just as if he is dead. His name is fiction and is pronounced only by whispering. I really miss him. The idea of never ever seeing him again makes me go mad. And I thought I had forgotten all about him.

Davel knocked on my door. I happened to hug my rabbit which I got from Kodjiro after my operation and pinched the soft animal in such a way that it changed into strange shapes. Reluctantly Davel entered and sat beside me on my bed. “So Tachikawa has gone,” he said softly. I nodded my head. Then he started to talk nineteen to the dozen. He argued about a girl-friend who left him one day, just like that. About a teacher from school who did not show up the next day and the day after. And about faithful fathers who clean the faces of their children spread all over with mashed potatoes and then later never come back when they go and buy a package of cigarettes. I was fascinated by Davel’s happy face. I had to laugh because of him chatting all the time and I pressed his side. Directly afterwards I pulled his ear. I had to jump forward for he tried to grab my side. A minute later we started to fool around and fell onto my bed laughing like hell. We were tickling each other on every spot we could reach. Tears were coming from my eyes. I can’t remember if it was showing because of laughter or because of sorrow. Suddenly we fell on the floor. We stayed there for a while. My tummy was sore of laughing. Davel caressed my hair and looked at me in a sweet way. So what’s next? Are we going to make love after all?    

Davel asked me what I thought of his body. He hadn’t gained weight. I think it was good he stopped with that protein-diet. When I told him so, he was shocked. “Do you think I’m too fat?” he asked frightened. Never thought men could be so insecure about their figure as well. Davel stood in front of my mirror and looked at himself seriously. “I do not recognize myself anymore. How come?” I tried to comfort Davel. “If you have gained pounds so fast, it must be easy to loose them again. A bit of muscles is what you want, right?” Davel tried out a few poses. “As a matter of fact I don’t know what I really want,” he said. “Is it clear to you?” Again we started laughing. Strange feelings. We hardly know who we are and even more so what we actually want.
