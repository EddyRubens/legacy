The ride home from the airport was nice.  I told Josh all about Carrie, Bernie and the baby. Then I told him about all the sites I saw. I don't know how interested he really was, but he pretended to be anyway. I wanted to get all the small talk out of the way so he wouldn't have an excuse to want to come inside and chat when I got home. 

When we pulled into the drive way I thanked Josh for everything he had done while I was gone, and said goodnight for the evening. I closed the door and waited until he had driven out of site until I turned on the computer. I looked up the Braintec site and was amazed to see pictures of people who actually take part in a surgical implant in their brain and then experience other peoples' memories. There was mention of a MSI implant, the smaller of the two, and the MESI implant, which was a bit bigger but offers better memory experiences. I saw one girl who looked a bit familiar then realized she was the woman from the plane. Her name was Rosanne. I read about her experiences so far and some from a guy named Adrian. Wow!  

The website mentioned they were looking for people to come and be test subjects. A particular amount of money and a free trip to Portland, Oregon for testing was offered. I instantly knew this was something I wanted to be involved with. It's not the money or the free trip, it's something more. To experience something so personal and so real at the same time. I can't even begin to explain!
