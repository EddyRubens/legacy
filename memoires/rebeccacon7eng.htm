When I reached the front of the airport there was a man standing there with a sign in his hand. He was holding it down at his side as though he was tired of holding it in the air.  When I looked at my watch I realized I was about twenty-five minutes late.   

After I finished eating, I was distracted by a woman who decided to have an argument in the middle of the airport.  "How dare you walk out on me...and with her of all people!" she shouted "I'll take everything you've got!"  I was amused at the vulgar remarks she screamed across the airport as her husband (I'm assuming) walked away with some woman. Don't get me wrong I don't take pleasure in the misfortune of others, I just take pleasure in the fact that they would choose to humiliate themselves in front of hundreds of complete strangers. 

Anyhow, when I approached the man with the sign I tried to apologize for my tardiness. He just said "MmmHmm" and took my suitcases.  I followed him out to the car and filled him in on the commotion that took place over by the Wendy's. He didn't seem to amused so I decide to just drop the subject. 

The ride to the facility was nice. I enjoyed the scenery along the way. I didn't talk to the driver much. I don't think he liked me to well. 

When we arrived to the building I started to get butterflies in my stomach.  There were a few other people sitting inside this little room with all there luggage beside them. I assumed they were new here too. I said "Hi" to a few people but nobody was really in the mood for conversation. I couldn't blame them, they were probably nervous too. 

A woman came in the room and introduced herself as Rosanne (She was the girl from the airplane and the web site). She said she would take us to our rooms and let us get settled. Then in a few hours we would receive a tour and meet the staff. When I was shown my room, I was told I would meet my roommate later. She apparently went in for her first tests today.  

That leaves me to right now. I am sitting on a single bed looking across at my new roommates stuff. She has a huge diamond studded red pillow on her bed which reads Carmen across it. I hope we will get along as well as Carrie and I did in college. I guess I'll soon find out!
