Suddenly fun was over. I didn’t see him entering the room; suddenly he was there and was shaking me all over. It took some time before I recognized him. It was the man of the FBI who had given me the key of this obscure cave. We came to the conclusion we had been in this dark cave for three days and nights!

“And this, what’s this?” he shouted while he was throwing a hand full of M-Stores across the room. I couldn’t imagine he had never seen them before. Fortunately he managed to calm down. He explained we should leave immediately. They were looking for us. Adrian looked at him frightened. It felt as if he could attack him any moment. The man let go off me right away and showed Adrian his pass. “Bentley, Paul Bentley,” he said. “I presume you are Adrian Young?” Adrian nodded like a sheep. He seemed to be back in reality. “Pack the stuff you need as fast as you can,” Paul ordered. “They can be here any time.”  I grabbed my stuff as fast as I could. The M-Stores were lying all over the place on dirty mattresses. “Let go of it!” Paul growled. “You must be crazy risking your life for a few second-hand memories. You already had way too many. If you continue like that, I’m sure at least one of you has no chance of surviving.”

Adrenaline had taken control of me. Panic! We were in life’s danger and were surrounded by walls! I didn’t know which way I had to run. “Go outside!” Paul ordered and he was pushing us towards the direction of the door. “And try to make up something different apart from those stupid M-Stores to kill your brain. Try bungee-jumping or something like that.”

Confused we looked at all the rush in the street. This was not what we wanted. I managed to find the park instinctively, where the old woman picked me up last week. We sat in the bushes all afternoon. In the evening we felt normal again. The uploads had almost finished. We were hungry. I was longing to stretch my legs and suggested having a meal. And besides I didn’t really fancy myself staying in the bushes the whole night. We had to find a new place to sleep. Adrian needed just a little bit more time before he felt human again to go down town. He had hardly seen anything of Tokyo yet.

We were having a meal in a small restaurant and were making up our minds where we should spend the night. We made our choice fast. I still had the credit-card Paul gave to me in the train. Hadn’t used it yet. We walked into the first hotel we saw and checked in under a false name. What a relief sleeping in a normal bed and using a Western toilet. Slowly I became completely human again. We now longer had to pee into a hole in the ground.
