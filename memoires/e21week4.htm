Some friends arrived to tell us all about their holiday in Texas and later that night we were watching the stars. I was happy.

I suspect that their relation was perfect at this moment. For a long time they were not in love anymore. It had been exchanged for something much deeper. Mutual confidence and a serious solidarity. It was good. An enjoyable memory with a good, peaceful feeling. Crickets were heard making loud sounds. The heat was pleasant. I felt good, protected and in good spirits. This is my home.

Still enjoying the good result which this memory had on me, I visited Adelle. I was glad I did. I found her in her room where she was laying a cold wet towel on her eyes. Her eyes were completely red and it looked as if she had been hit. “Frank,” she muttered, “I really miss Frank so very much!” She pressed the towel even closer to her eyes. I took away the tissue and caressed her hair. “You are allowed to cry,” I said. “I have cried already too often!” And immediately her eyes were filled with tears again. I grabbed a box of tissues from her desk and put it beside her.
