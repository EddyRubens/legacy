As I wrote in the beginning, I study psychology. That’s where I would like to write about. Because I have never experienced something like this. I read a lot about all kinds of experiments during my study. For example, the research program where subjects thought they were hurting other subjects. This is how that went: the subjects had to test other subjects, who turned out to be role-playing scientists. They thought they had to investigate how people would perform under the stress of a physical treat. Every time this pretending subject failed, they had to give him a pulse. At the beginning they gave only a low voltage, but later on, when they were getting more faulty answers, they had to give a higher pulse. They scientist who were playing for subjects and who didn’t receive any pulses at all, screamed out loud that it was hurting them so much and that they were worried for heart failure. A professor encouraged the real subjects to continue giving pulses and most of them did. That was this research about, to find out how far people will go on a direct order. Only a few subjects refused.

Sometimes I have the feeling that the Braintec program is about something else as well. That it’s not about finding a cure for Alzheimer’s but about us. It wouldn’t surprise me if it’s about our response to the memories. What else could be the reason for so many questions and tests about our experiences? I often think that they monitor everything I’m doing. They truly do, of course, but not as intensive as I think. I think there are hidden cameras all over the place and the real scientists are writing down all our movements. They made a good story around it, I almost believed it.

I am not getting paranoia but I keep an eye on everything. Conversations and such are seen in several ways. If someone asks me for my opinion on the Tachikawa case, I make up several answers. Then I decide what the real scientist would like to hear and I give them the opposite answer. I don’t know what I wish to gain but it’s getting obsessive. I’m hardly spontaneous and they find it more and more difficult to get a grip on me. That’s fine with me. I am more a complex personality than they might think.
