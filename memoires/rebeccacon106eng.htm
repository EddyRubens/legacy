There was no doubt in my mind, the other shadow had to be Jacobs. My thoughts seemed to be floating and I was struggling to grab them. It seemed the one I was able to grasp was the deliberation as to where Thomas was. Even though Thomas was pretty damn strong and could handle himself in almost any situation, Jacobs was a lot bigger than him. And, even if they were having a fair match up, Andrews most likely got involved, out numbering Thomas, and helped Jacobs bring him down. I wanted to rush to his aide as he did for me, but there was no way out past Andrews and Jacobs.

"OK princess," Andrews said. "If that's how you want to play...just read the note we're giving you.... and..... make sure you make the right choice. (A piece of folded paper slid under the door).....Cause next time you and you new little boyfriend won't be so lucky. You'll both end up like that jerk-off agent's twin brother. Get my drift?....and Uh,....I know, you know, what I'm talking about!" After that, I heard them walking away. But Andrews managed to yell back to me, "I'll be sure to tell Josh about your new boyfriend. I'm sure he'd like to hear all about what's going on"....then laughing.

When I thought they were gone, I turned on the light. I put the dumbbell down and grabbed the (newly noticed) baseball bat behind the door. What was I thinking with that dumbbell anyway? I could never have lifted that thing to swing at someone! I grabbed the folded paper, and shoved it into my pocket with the Band-Aid box, Then slowly opened the door. I inched the door, little by little until I was positive no one was there. I ran back down the hall towards my room, baseball bat in hand, praying to find Thomas in one piece. When I got there, I found him beaten and tied to the safety grip handle in the shower. "Oh Thomas!" I said as I started to cry. "No time for tears girl," he replied. "Hurry and untie me, we gotta find Jerry!"
