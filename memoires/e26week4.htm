Although I admire Kodjiro a lot and although I’m madly in love with him, we did get into a fight. I suppose he expects me to take everything for granted and keep my mouth shut. Well, that’s not the way I am.

We walked over to his apartment, after we spent another evening dining out. This afternoon I understood from an e-mail that Hans and Heather, two new test subjects, are in real trouble. Just before or after their operation, actually I do not know exactly how the story goes, they got away with equipment of Braintec. It is not my responsibility anymore assisting test subjects. However, this time I couldn’t forget the incident. What the hell is going on in Portland? On the other hand, and I’m not pleased at all, I have no news from Sonja for such a long time. And Jessica, I’m dying to hear from her. Kodjiro knew more about it, I was sure about that.

So we were walking in the street and I asked him to inform me about it. Kodjiro was acting as if he didn’t understand my question. “Please stop pretending you do not know anything!” I shouted all of a sudden. “Why don’t you take me serious?” Kodjiro pushed me against the wall and squeezed my throat. Why on earth did I ask him this in this small desolated alley and not in the middle of a busy crossing loaded with people preventing him from murdering me?!

Fortunately Kodjiro took away his hands. I was coughing for more than five minutes before I was able to breathe normally. “You can trust me,” I coughed, “But can I trust you?” Kodjiro was acting nervously and apologized for his impulsive behavior. Afterwards he emphasized we shouldn’t have such conversations on the open. We walked to his apartment without saying a word.
