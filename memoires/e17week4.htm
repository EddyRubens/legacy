Hugo called me today. Or actually he called to Dr. Hamilton and then he asked me to take over the conversation. “You know better than I do how Sonja is getting along,” he said while he gave me the horn. It was clear that Hugo worried about Sonja. “Sonja sounded so much differently when I called her last,” he told me. “Not himself.” “That I can imagine!” I answered. “How would you feel if you discover that your lover would rather prefer to get rid of you in order to sleep in somebody else’s bed?” Silence was heard on the other end of the line. After a while Hugo broke silence by coughing. “Talking about the program Sonja’s watching over there with you. Is it okay?” Why Sonja’s husband became so much worried about her all of a sudden? It seemed to me very hypocritical. “The only thing that bothers her is you!” I shouted to my big surprise. “You men have not the slightest idea what you are doing. It is really time to make things happen!” Hugo became quiet immediately. “I just wanted to know how Sonja was doing,” he said confused. “Can you please give my regards to her and tell her that I love her?” This man really irritated me immensely. What did he have in mind? That he could fix things together with slime?  “Why don’t you do it yourself!” I answered abrupt and smashed the horn. Dr. Hamilton looked at me very surprised. “What is going on?” he asked. “Some men are driving me crazy!” I explained. I’m glad the director could not understand Dutch.

When my anger dropped a bit I looked for Sonja. She was staring out of the window in the coffee room. I told her about the telephone conversation and dark clouds over her head seemed to clear the sky. Slowly a smile was visible on her face. “Thanks, Rosanne!” she said and turned her head again towards the window. Again she was looking into the distance. With this never ending view.
