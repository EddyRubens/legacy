The situation in which Sheila found me this morning had reached a decisive factor. For a while she had been walking around with a strange suspect and now it was time to talk about it with me. Sheila thinks I have a schizoid psychosis. Something between schizophrenia and a psychosis if I’m not mistaken. It sounds that way. We have been dealing with it almost all day.

I had an appointment with her, but I didn’t show up. Sheila visited me in my room and she found me there. I can vaguely remember something in that direction. I wanted to be left alone. Sheila asked me what was wrong and I didn’t feel like answering. She kept asking me questions, very boring. When she finally left the room, I hid myself into the cupboard. I knew she would be back soon. First she searched the bathroom and a few minutes later she found me in the cupboard. I was squeezed between my rucksack and some clothes hanging. “Are we going to play hide-and-seek?” Sheila asked. Actually I should have kicked her into her face. Who the hell she thinks I was, an elderly woman?

In one way or another Sheila always manages to get me under control by letting me think in a logical way. Anyway, she pursued me to swallow a huge red pill. I was swallowing this mega-smartie while I was still in the cupboard. It didn’t make sense staying in there any longer. Sheila told me we had to discuss a few items and suggested to follow her to her office. There she told me what she suspected all the time.

The syndrome scared me a lot. I cannot help thinking about schizophrenia without imagining most tragic cases in mental hospitals. Creatures totally strangers in this world and who can never play an accepted role in society again. I got down-hearted on the spot. Sheila trivialized her diagnosis and reminded me at the fact that she is not a psychiatrist. Probably I have dealt with so much emotions and information which had become all too much for me. According to her, receiving memories of somebody else cannot be without risks in the long run. If symptoms prove the causes of uploads, it would be a good idea to stop them temporarily in order to recover. The frightful idea I get when I deal with schizophrenia and psychosis calmed me down a bit realizing I’m not able to get my weekly uploads for the time being.

Right now, reflecting the whole situation in my bed, I think differently about it. I do not see any reason why I shouldn’t get new uploads any more. Sheila gave me medicine to suppress the psychosis. Neuroleptics. Those pills are pretty good. Haven’t heard any voices since and I seem to be closer to myself more than ever. Although I suffer from a headache and a sore throat. Why can’t I get any uploads now that this medicine helps me out? And why Dr. Hamilton, who is a real psychiatrist, has no time for me?
