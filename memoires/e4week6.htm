I saw a vase. A red vase with a ribbed base, and a handle. Quite an ugly vase, really. If someone would bring flowers as a gift, putting them in such a vase would be an insult, ghastly! This vase is the first memory that’s been implanted in me. I didn’t only see the vase, I felt it as well. The material felt rough and cold in my hands. I couldn’t smell anything; maybe it was too clean to be smelly. It’s a strange experience, this vase. Like dreaming with my eyes open. An image formed on my retina, fingers feeling matter and arm muscles making a mild effort to carry the imaginary object. A vase, no doubt. The doctors were wildly enthusiastic when I told them it was a vase. Even before we walked out of the lab they were already holding glasses of champagne. The team’s exuberance, their combined enthusiasm was contagious. The image of that hideous vase gained a sort of merry charm. I was given a glass of champagne as well. It was as if I had passed an important exam. My implant works!
