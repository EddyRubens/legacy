Again another upload. I was desperately longing for another upload but I was too upset heading towards laboratory. Imagine what could happen if I saw Dr. Tachikawa and Jessica? Already one of them made it impossible for me to keep paying attention to the upload.

Luckily both of them were not there. No need to explain Dr. Tachikawa’s absence. Normally he works on those M-Stores ready for use during day and night. However, I could not find a good excuse for Jessica’s absence. “Jessica not here today?” I asked with an open mind. Reactions of the members of the board were striking. Steward could not hear a thing and fully neglected my question. Sheila also looked into the other direction as often as possible. Something is going on! Finally Dr. Hamilton answered my question. “Jessica made advantage of all her days off,” he said while he looked at me trying to understand me. “She was longing for a break just like you.” “Just like me?” In my tired head suddenly a brightness showed up which misapprehended the whole matter. “It was Jessica who wanted me to leave from here!” I suddenly shouted and now in return my colleagues had problems looking into the other direction. “You bitch!” Again I was in need of throwing things at everybody. Fortunately I could control myself. “And Dr. Tachikawa? Is Dr. Tachikawa really that busy so that I haven’t seen a glimpse of him yet, or you are just telling me that on purpose? I’m sure he is in bed with Jessica at this moment!” Dr. Hamilton made clear I should sit down for a while and reached to me a glass of water. I calmed down again.

A few minutes later I received the upload. It was a strange one. One moment I was on a sort of sightseeing boat and in the next women in feathers were suddenly dancing in front of my eyes. “I’m getting mad!” was the first thing I said when the upload was over. “Or is something wrong with this memory?” Dr. Hamilton acknowledged that the last conclusion would probably be the case. Also Steve and Adrian had seen strange figures. They told everybody in details and that’s why Dr. Hamilton was happy he could test the memory on me. I was the only one who did not know beforehand what kinds of figures were mixed through it.

Sheila wanted to discuss the matter a bit longer after the usual interview. I suggested we should do so next time for my headache was giving me such a hard time that every thought was painful. She gave me some tranquillizers and told me I was welcome any time if I felt like talking about it. No way, no more test psychologists for me anymore. I jumped into bed and I planned for myself to stay there for good.
