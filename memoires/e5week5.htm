These last few days I have relived all sorts of banal memories; the vase, relaxing in the sun, a balloon, being on a swing and yesterday it was eating sushi. In theory the sushi was as banal as the rest, but it had an unexpected and confusing aftertaste.

The test subject from whose memory I recalled clearly loved raw fish. Besides the structure, the smell and of course the flavor of the food, I also experienced a sort of satisfaction. The enjoyment you experience while having your favorite meal. It was mouth watering! That’s saying something for someone who doesn’t like raw fish! This has confused me so much though that I refused to do another session today. Dr. Steward thought that I had more than deserved a break, so I got some free time to saunter through town today. I stopped suddenly at a little Japanese shop. Sushi. I think I had sushi for the first (and last!) time about six years ago and it’s not a good memory. It was really hard to swallow and I fed most of it to the cat. Even in Greenland I managed to avoid raw fish dishes. Now I suddenly felt an impulse to buy sushi. I guess the satisfaction I got from the imaginary sushi yesterday hasn’t been forgotten. My curiosity was stronger than my confusion and I took a dare. Some rice spilled into my blouse as I tried to eat the sushi as casually as I could. I didn’t get far. After two bites I was gagging and I fed the rest of it to the birds. No more sushi for me, whatever happens. I could have known.
