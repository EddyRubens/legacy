Sonja’s meeting left its mark on me. Because I don’t know whom I should trust and whom I don’t, I called Adrian. I can always trust him. Luckily I still had his mobile number with me. Meanwhile I was afraid it was in the middle of the night in Portland. He sounded a bit sleepy.

It was great to hear his voice. I told him all I knew. About Jessica, about the strange extraction and the way he tried to strangle me and that that’s my reason for not knowing whether I should trust Kodjiro any longer. About that strange meeting and about Hans and Heather. And of course also about Sonja. Adrian was listening attentively. He also finds it hard to take part in it. Then it was his turn. He told me that he had dinner with Dr. Hamilton. And that, meanwhile, he possesses a great number of M-Stores. Part of them had been sent to him by mail, together with an air-ticket to Tokyo. “Did you also?” I asked. “Are you coming over here? I would love to see you again.” Silence on the other end of the line. He didn’t make up his mind yet. I think he preferred that I would return to Portland. But I really don’t fancy that at all.

“What do you think I should do, Adrian?” I really sounded desperate. Am I really in need that much?  “Usually I ask myself that question a couple of times a day,” he answered. “I really don’t know. Maybe I want to return to Elgin.” I absolutely didn’t fancy that option. At that moment Adrian seemed to be my only link to reality, somebody who is not influenced by unexplained behavior or through my suspicion. If he goes home now, we are on a sinking ship! “No, you’d better come over here!” I said. “You didn’t get that ticket for nothing?” Adrian seemed to hesitate a lot. He couldn’t make up his mind yet. We were talking about a few beautiful memories we had experienced together and emphasized our relationship between brother and sister. Then suddenly my phone-expenses expired.

Shall Adrian come to Tokyo as well? Or will he keep waiting in Portland until I return? Now that Dr. Hamilton no longer threatens to remove my implant, I think I might be safer over there. Then I shouldn’t be afraid for that grumpy man Sonja was warning me about. That collection of M-Stores Adrian was talking about, also made me curious. What could be on it? If I return to Portland, I’ll probably never find out what’s the real situation concerning Hans and Heather. Only if I stay here, I can mean something important to them.
