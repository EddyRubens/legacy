Today an urgent meeting was held which lasted almost the whole day. For this morning Dr. Hamilton has had a brief but clear discussion with the National Institute of Mental Health, our main sponsor. Through the media they were informed about the improved techniques. The interpretation of the press was absolutely not tolerated by them. The Institute refuses to invest money in public entertainment and underlined the conditions in which finance is supporting this project. If we cannot deal with that they will stop the flow of money as from today. Before the end of this week they expect us to give them an explanation with respect to these improvements.  

It was not that easy. Meetings going on the whole day. Without the financial support of this institution we can shut down the whole matter. Then we won’t be able to pay the rent of this building. So we have to meet with their demands whether we like it or not. Dr. Hamilton never intended to change ideas on the original plans. But Dr. Tachikawa is facing him now right at this moment. “Future can be found in entertainment, not in medical complaint!” he shouted when state of mind was getting out of hand. And I truly believed that Japanese never showed any of their feelings. If you would ask me, I think Kodjiro has become the same hothead as I. 

I never saw Dr. Hamilton that furious and especially now he admitted the whole matter much better, but all the same he managed to temper his anger very well. I felt sorry for him. If Braintec had to realize somebody’s dream, it would be his one. He would rather prefer to develop a new medical method for Alzheimer’s disease or dementia. Not to become famous or just for the sake of money, but the merciful side of the medical world: to recover the sick. Dr. Hamilton is a born starry-eyed idealist. And now he sees with his own eyes that Dr. Tachikawa is heading a different direction. Just the one and only direction in front of him. That is really hard on him. And even more difficult to find a good solution for it. Dr. Tachikawa is definitely not interested in changing his intentions. He also has a dream to come true.

“But without the financial support of the National Institute you also cannot develop your own inventions!” Dr. Hamilton suggested in a diplomatic way. “Or were your intentions to approach Sony just like that?” Also Dr. Tachikawa was not touched by this remark and it kept on going like this. This meeting was such a very exhausted one. Nobody got any wiser. That explanation for the institution will definitely break through. On paper Dr. Tachikawa’s ideas will be not as interesting and even eliminated. But in reality his brightness will never go away. Inspirations all over the place. Nobody can stop Dr. Tachikawa anymore.
