We have just had some violence here. Dr. Hamilton and Dr. Tachikawa had a fight. I don’t know how this happened exactly, but their argument was about that article in the Scientific American. Dr. Tachikawa blamed Dr. Hamilton for not mentioning his name, while it was his invention. And he seemed to have gathered a lot of irritations during the last months, which he all poured out. It must have bothered him a lot, ‘coss he started to throw with everything. The quarrel became heated.

I haven’t seen this fight myself, but I have heard it. It was an enormous shouting and throwing match. I had just left my room, where I put some cream on my sleep. When I heard the noise, I rushed quickly towards it. They were in the coffee room, in front of a dozen test subjects and several colleagues. Everyone who wasn’t already there came running in as quickly as possible, to find out what was happening. At the time I arrived it was almost over. “If this thing continuous, it might perhaps be better if I start for myself!” Dr. Tachikawa screamed. He gestured that we had to get out of his way and walked away furiously. Dr. Hamilton stayed behind flabbergasted. He suddenly seemed to be ten years older. Dr. Tachikawa was almost around the corner when I got the impulse to follow him. “Kodjiro!” I shouted and rushed towards him. “Kodjiro, wait!” This was the first time that I called him by his surname. That was probably the reason why he halted abruptly. He took a deep breath, turned around and took my face in his hands. “Maybe we should leave this place together.” he whispered and he kissed me on my forehead. He softly caressed my temple and muttered some excuse. Then he continued walking, with his head down. Again, I felt the impulse to follow him, but I didn’t do it this time. It might be better to leave him alone for a while.

I lie down on my bed to rethink what had happened today. Dr. Tachikawa’s response was way out of proportion, I thought. Maybe Dr. Hamilton had mentioned his name and his involvement during the interview. The reporter might have chosen to leave that out of the article. But it was probably the last straw. What else had filled his mind? And then that situation with Steward and my painful face. Was Steward setting the two doctors against each other?  Actually I should be getting go to the coffee room, to hear what happened exactly. But I prefer to stay in my bed right now. Dr. Tachikawa had kissed me. True, on my forehead, but it still was a kiss. I get a warm feeling when I remember it. Kodjiro.
