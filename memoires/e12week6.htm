I had a long talk with Jessica. She’s worried about the side effects of the uploads. I myself was under the impression that there are a lot less than a few weeks ago. I hardly get headaches and the nausea is completely gone. But that wasn’t what Jessica meant. She suspects the uploads might be addictive and she’s worried about possible long term effects. For that reason she’s suggested to give them a rest.

I jumped up in anger when she told me this. I exploded. “How dare you! You think you can use your authority as a psychologist against me this way?” Jessica looked at me expressionless.  “I know you hate it that I fancy Kodjiro and not you, but now you’ve really gone too far.” Jessica walked toward the basin to get a glass of water and put it on the table in front of me. I almost threw it back at her. Thankfully I could just about control myself and fell back into the chair, feeling dejected. My violent reaction had only confirmed her point. I took a few gulps and tried to relax. “Dear Rosanne,” Jessica began, “you’re absolutely right concerning my feelings for you and Tachikawa, but in this case it has nothing to do with it. I’m really concerned about you and I wouldn’t want things to get out of hand. Please believe me.” I really wanted to stay angry and leave that stuffy little room, but I knew she was right. It takes no more than two days to get me bad tempered when I haven’t had an upload. And my experiences with the amusement park memory were so overpowering that the world around me was becoming totally irrelevant. When I talked to Jessica about it I underplayed it. After all, she has the power to convince the team to remove my implant if she thinks it’s necessary. If that were to happen I could never relive memories again. I’ll have to preserve my friendship with Jessica.
