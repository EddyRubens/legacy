I desperately needed an upload. After brushing my teeth I couldn’t get away from the mirror. I kept on staring at my reflection and didn’t like it at all. I hardly recognized myself. Was that really me? Those features, those eyes. Seemed they belong to somebody else. They anxiously stared at me.

I must have a new upload. If necessary, one of my own. I don’t mind a memory I have gone through before either. As long as I can feel that stream of information for just another time. Those two seconds in which the memory enters are highly intensified. All details and emotions involved create a fantastic experience. The feeling of being somebody else and experiencing the world in a different way is absolutely addictive. It was obvious to me earlier. Although such strong withdrawal symptoms are new to me. Or probably caused by neuroleptics? I haven’t taken them for a couple of days. 

The urge getting a new upload had become so strong that I had to admit it. If I had asked for it, they would surely have refused it to me. So now I had to do it secretly. First thing, I searched Steward’s room. M-Stores were surely there, but I couldn’t find them. Then I came up with the idea that Adrian also has M-Stores to test his instrument.

Adrian was working hard in his small musty office. He didn’t shave for at least one week. Did he leave this space at all? I opened a window to get some fresh air. “Weather is way too beautiful for staying in whole day,” I told Adrian. “Yes, yeah, I know,” he muttered frustrated. Things didn’t work out. He turned the switches and looked a bit confused. “What was it, you wanted to know?” he asked. “I was wondering if you could help me,” I made it up, right there. “Maybe you need a test subject?” Adrian looked at me doubtfully. “Do you really think we should do that? After the event in the coffee-room I have doubts about your health.” “Nothing wrong with my health,” I lied. “I only find it hard, now that Kodjiro is not here anymore.” Adrian nodded. “In that case it will be even better. I would like to test something, with just a simple upload.” I was jumping of joy and couldn’t help showing my enthusiasm. “Fine,” I answered acting as normal as possible. “Let’s do it right away.”

It sure was a trivial memory. Something like sitting in the sun, a memory I have experienced before. One of my first uploads and despite the soberness of this memory it impressed me exactly when I had it for the first time. Again I felt great. Adrian asked me to stay for just a second and after about fifteen minutes he was as happy as I was. Between the snows of his monitor you could clearly recognize the bright light of the sun.
