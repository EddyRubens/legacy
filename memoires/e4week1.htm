I’m much better now, thankfully. It’s quite alright really, the way things are now. I’m a little hazy, I have no pain and all the time in the world. I’m reading a bit, playing games and of course laying those jig-saws. The black hole, in which I threatened to disappear, is all gone. I’m cheerful, friendly and merry. My positive attitude is in sync with my physical recovery. The better I get, the more cheerful I become. And as my recovery progresses, I am given more freedom, which helps to make me feel better. The day before yesterday I was allowed to go back to my ‘own’ room so I’m finally rid of that dreadful see through mirror. Just as during the test week I am now to report at set times to the appropriate team members for tests, physiotherapy and such. The rest of the time I’m ‘off’. It’s almost like being back at school, going from classroom to classroom with the occasional free period. I’m pretty pleased. I’ve gone off most of my medication. I will keep using anti-epileptics, out of precaution. According to Dr. Hamilton it’s very unlikely that the implant will ever cause another attack though. Every new day symbolizes another jump forward. If things continue in this tempo, I’ll be back to normal soon. The doubts I was having for a while are all gone. I’m still me. The implant has not affected my personality and also didn’t cause any brain injuries. I have to say I was afraid of that when the epileptic attacks started happening.

Still, it’s a strange idea, having an implant inside my head. Dr. Hamilton says that the surrounding cells are connecting well to the implant, which they have now accepted as a part of the neurosystem. A nice idea, being able to augment the body like that. I don’t feel it at all anymore. It’s there of course. But like my kidneys or pancreas; I don’t feel it. I’m glad about that, ‘coss I’d hate to think it could start itching or something like that.
