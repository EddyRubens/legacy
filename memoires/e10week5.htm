The new test subjects were all very much stressed. Understandable, ‘coss their test results will be discussed this afternoon and they will be informed whether they are allowed to get an implant or have to leave.

I never had to answer so many questions. They were all worried. Even Alice, who seems to be okay after all. “What if this goes wrong, what if that goes wrong?” I couldn’t answer all the questions, but I think I could reassure them a bit. My soothing words had also a positive effect on myself. I started to believe in my own comforts more and more. “Yes, it is a safe procedure. Besides some inconveniences there is nothing scary to expect.” They were also uncertain for the test results. “What was your answer to the question of the use of alcohol?” I recognized this uncertainty and I could guarantee that it couldn’t be that hard because I also passed that test. I am going to take part of the evaluation team and I promised I would have a good word with all five of them.

It was funny to see the evaluation from the other side. The test results were converted to beautiful graphics, through which you could see which brain parts were developed the best. The EEG-results and the MRI-scan confirmed or denied that. I have asked for a bit more information about the importance of this, but I was called back with a view on the clock. “Within the time we had for you evaluation some months ago, we now have to decide what’s going to happen to five test subjects!” That kept me quiet for the rest of the meeting and therefore I didn’t say a word when they decided that it would be better for Thomas if he went home tomorrow. The reason isn’t clear to me, but Thomas is not suitable for an implant. The other test subjects will go to the hypnotizer, one by one, to find out who might probably get a MESI. After that they will all be admitted to the left wing. There I’ll give my responsibility into the hands of others for a couple of days. I intend to visit them after the operations.

Next Monday a new group of test subjects will arrive. I became very quiet when Dr. Hamilton noticed that the Californian woman would be among them. I am allowed to give her a preferential treatment, he specifically told me so.
