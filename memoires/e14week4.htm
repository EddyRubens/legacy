Tomorrow I will return to Portland. Time flies extremely fast and I’m afraid this is such a holiday from which you’ll have to recover when you are home again. A real working holiday. Due to all those presentations I have seen a lot of Holland. Tonight there will be another presentation held in Eindhoven and then I’ve finished it.

photo's taken at this presentation

Maybe it is too early imagining how it felt to be here but the idea that I couldn’t find my feet is forcing upon me. What did I expect from the two weeks in Holland? Maybe I had hoped secretly that my return home would have a special place. One of the reasons why I am in Holland is the fact that I had to find again some of my roots. The thing I found again is the anxiety which got me out Holland at the time. I’m glad I have my family and it’s good to acknowledge family ties. But at the same time I’m glad that I return to Braintec tomorrow. Again I feel that good-tempered breath into my neck.

The only moment when I really felt my roots was when I looked through the old photo-albums. When I cleared them up I nosed about the attic. There is a lot of rubbish out there. I wonder why my mother hasn’t thrown it away yet. People can relate memories to matter and then find it hard to get rid of it. Behind the suitcases with summer clothes and the boxes with Christmas-decorations I found a large carton. It was properly sealed and nowhere on it was written what was inside. It made me curious. Carefully I prized off the small strings and scotch tape and opened the box. There were three fully loaded trash bags in it. I opened the first one and my mouth fell open with surprise. My old soft cuddly toys! Brammetje and Snowball! Femke and Stampertje! I immediately opened the other bags. All my friends were still there and they stared at me with great expectation. They had been lying on this attic for years! Neatly wrapped, but still had become a bit mouldy. I suddenly started to cry. One by one I cuddled the soft animals. They comforted me and I did comfort them too because they had been locked up for such a long time in those plastic bags. Oh, and Goldie! I almost forgot.

I have washed half of the cuddly toys. Couldn’t get more into the washing-machine. The other half I will wash in the Research Centre. Obviously I will take all of them with me. They are better friends than, for example, Jeanine. Brammetje will never ever complain about the danger of an implant in my head. And Sjakie really likes it when I talk about Kodjiro all the time. When my mother saw me playing with my cuddly toys she got mad. “Aren’t you far too old for that?” she asked cynical. “I’m never too old for love and affection, dear mother!” I answered.
