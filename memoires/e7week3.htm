Dr. Tachikawa asked me this afternoon if I had really cooked my own dinner as he was told. I confirmed that. He gave me a pat on the back and said that he already knew I’m a good woman. “Despite of that we will go to the Kaiseki Ryori again, this evening,” he whispered in my ear. “Do you think I’m still welcome there?” I asked him astonished, but he was already heading for the door. He walked through the corridor without turning back.

The rest of the day he ignored me. I had misbehaved myself so much the previous time that I couldn’t believe he meant it. Why would he invite me a second time for dinner? Maybe he dislikes eating alone. He has to, ‘coss he did turn up knocking at my door at eight o’clock. Fortunately I was ready to go. We didn’t speak while we drove to the Ryori. Usually I prefer this silence, but tonight I felt kind of awkward. Does he suspect something from me? I tried to find a good question to start a conversation, but I couldn’t find one. This evening I wouldn’t drink too much, I quietly resolved to do so. Otherwise Dr. Tachikawa might get a bad image of me.

We were kindly greeted when we entered the Ryori and were escorted to the same table as last week. So they weren’t forgotten us. After a couple of glasses filled with a very special wine we started to talk. Especially I did. Dr. Tachikawa wanted to know everything about me and the love affairs I was into. Those two or three glasses were enough to blur my natural borders so I told him shamelessly about every secret and naughty detail he wanted to know. He started to giggle childlike when I told him about a petting on the dog-sledge while the snow buzzed twenty centimeter underneath me. His eyes were full of wickedness. He touched my hand and said: “Otemba. That’s what you are, Otemba!” Whatever that means! I quess it’s some kind of nickname. He repeated it when we said good night to each other.

Satisfied with the good company, the lovely food and last but certainly not least the wine, I dreamt a bit further. It was a nice evening. I’ve got the absolute feeling that I’ve got it all in my hands and that I can do anything I wish to do with it. Gorgeous.
