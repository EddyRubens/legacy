I went along with Jessica to San Francisco for the weekend. It was just fantastic. Saturday night there was a festival in a complex of auditoriums. There were about twenty bands and going from hall to hall you could always find something good. I thought one band that mixed opera singing with trendy house music were tremendous.

Best concert in years. A constant thumping rhythm layered with incredibly melodious vocals. The atmosphere was great. Before me was a guy with a chicken on his back. It was one of those rucksack things. Its little head bobbed up and down with the rhythm. ‘Well, if a chicken can dance, so can I’ I thought to myself. During all the swinging I suddenly realized what I wanted. I’m going to sing in a band too. First I’ll take some singing lessons of course. But then I’ll be just as good as this. I’ll tour the world with my band and perform in every major city. I won’t become so famous that I’ll be recognized on the streets, because that would be awful. But I will be financially independent and be able to buy whatever I like. It doesn’t have to be much. It’s no good having lot’s of things when you’re always traveling around the world. But a chicken like the one before me, for instance, would be handy.

Being away for the weekend has done me a lot of good. I have indulged myself completely. Being back at Braintec is ever more boring though. Even though the research is fascinating and the work diverse, I get stuck in a rut real easy. The crazy San Francisco nightlife beats everything. At Braintec almost everyone is deadly boring. All they can talk about is technical details. And the other test subjects only talk about their past or the far future. There doesn’t seem to be anything happening in between for them. This type of research will result in that sort of thing of course, but it’s still boring.

If I tour with a band that won’t be my lot. As soon as the performances become routine I will hit the studio and when recording becomes tedious I’ll start touring around the world again. The people I’ll meet won’t be so boring. Can’t think of a better life, really.
