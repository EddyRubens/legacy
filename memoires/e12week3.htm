The uploads and extractions are almost becoming routine. I still get a kick out of them, but they don’t surprise me as much. The memories are all different of course. Sometimes I’m so impressed by a particular memory that I find it hard to think about anything else for the rest of the day. I had one of those intense uploads about ten days ago. I miss those when I’m not getting them.

It’s become the same tedious routine for the team, apparently. Everybody was waiting with great disinterest until Steward had selected an M-store. He was scrolling through the files, looking for a memory I hadn’t relived yet. There were a few and he named them; a boat trip, another birthday and somebody’s dog. “Can I have the amusement park memory again?” I asked, surprising myself. I didn’t think the memories Steward mentioned would be as exciting as the amusement park one. Steward turned to me and said: “No, you can’t. We don’t upload the same memory twice.” “Why not?“ I shot back. “You can watch a movie more than once. You can read a book again.” “Yes,” Dr. Hamilton agreed, “but you’re forgetting something very fundamental. We’re not doing this research to entertain our test subjects. This is a serious, solid research program where the usefulness of the results are more important than the pleasure the test subjects get out of it.“ He sounded slightly irritated.

Strangely enough I felt irritated as well. It was an impulsive suggestion, but now I wanted nothing rather than to saunter through that dream world. I started to whine like a little kid. “Aren’t you curious what effect it might have? When you see a movie for the second time, you experience other things too. Surely that must be interesting?” Dr. Tachikawa became restless. But he kept quiet. “Sure it won’t do any harm.” Steward said and I felt grateful to him for the first time. “Even if we can’t use the results, we have nothing to lose.” Dr. Hamilton appeared to be buried in his thoughts. Say yes, say yes, say yes, I wished to myself. Only thinking about this memory had already turned me into a child! Dr. Hamilton looked at Dr. Tachikawa, who was staring into the distance, expressionless. Then Dr. Hamilton gave in: “Do what you want. I’ll read the reports later.”  To everyone’s amazement he just walked away. Dr. Hamilton was always there when I got an upload and I much appreciated his presence. He’s a good guy and the fact that he’s always so serious makes the procedure really trustworthy. Dr. Tachikawa shrugged. “We can do it without him.” he mumbled. He was right of course. The procedure had become so routine that one person could deal with it without any problems.
