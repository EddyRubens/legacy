OK, I have to admit I was in a bit of a shocked state. The thing Dr. Hamilton wanted to show me was a diamond studded tennis bracelet. He said he bought it for me because I, "...was such a good sport for doing all of this." I know for a fact, that I am not the only 'good sport' here. And, I also know for a fact, that diamond studded tennis bracelets were not handed out to all of the other 'good sports!'

I figured that it was time I drew the line without being too harsh. I didn't want to embarrass the poor old guy. "Dr. Hamilton I am both flattered and shocked that you think of me that way. But, you must remember that I have a boyfriend that I am very devoted to." "I...I Uh, I never intended..." he interrupted. I interrupted him right back and said, "You are a very nice man. Just not the one for me. But, I do know for a fact that Sheila has somewhat of a crush on you. She mentioned it once to me. Maybe you should try to pursue that route." "Really?" he said. "I had no idea! Sheila is very hard working and loyal to our company. She always told me no matter what happens, she'll stand by my side." "See," I continued. "That how women sent messages, through discrete comments!" Dr. Hamilton smiled and apologized for his actions. He said he hoped that this wouldn't change the work relationship we had or the friendship we made. I assured him that I was flattered and happy to remain just friends. Then I left his office.

On the way back to my room I saw Sheila in the hall. She was reading over a file. I figured, since I baited Dr. Hamilton, I should prepare her to be hooked. "Hi Sheila!" I said as I approached her. She looked at me with a half smiled and squinted as she said, "You sure seem to have had your spirits lifted. Is there anything you would like to talk about?" "Why yes there is, Sheila." I said smiling. Sheila smiled and said "We should go into my office." I shook my head 'No' and said, "I will tell you here, then I must get going." "Okay..."she said. "I just came back from Dr. Hamilton's office..." I said in a whisper. "He just told me that he thinks you are cute and he has a bit of a crush on you!"

Sheila's face turn red and she told me to stop fooling around. I swore her to secrecy. I told her, as my therapist, she was bound by law not to share anything I tell her. She smiled at me. This was the first time I think, I can remember her being truly happy. I think I am doing a good thing. I will head back to my room now. Such a busy day!
