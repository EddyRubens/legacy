So it had been a very busy day with three presentations after each other. I was watching the clock continuously and no time was left for me to complete and finish it. Although I succeeded. ‘Coss I reached many people. In the morning in museum the Vleeshal in Haarlem, in the afternoon and in the evening back in Rotterdam.

photo's taken at these presentations

Remarkably many people love to talk about science-fiction films or books. “Have you seen the latest movie? In it you can really do this or that. Do you think it is possible in real life soon?” Besides that they have frequently asked me questions whether it is possible to register different information instead of memories, like for example a Chinese dictionary. Useful when you are going to travel. I happen to talk about it with Kodjiro and Steward just a week and a half ago. It is not part of the Braintec’s research and that’s why we don’t do it. But theoretically speaking we do not exclude the possibility. Obviously the results could be negative, because all information will be saved in the short time memory. When, for instance, you copy all information from such a Chinese dictionary to the brains you will not find any connection between individual words and you will miss the grammatical sentences to apply to the linguistics. It will act as if somebody has read all words very fast. After such an upload you are not able to read any Chinese, not even speak nor understand. Maybe through regular uploading of complete language courses there are possibilities of something like a speed training method.

Speculations on futuristic possibilities are favored during my presentations.  Enthusiastic visitors ventilate their visions on the future and their opinions. So much enthusiasm from a boy of Groningen when I talked to him this morning in Haarlem I have not yet met before with my future test subjects. His name is Michael and he has been test subject before in another research. He couldn’t wait any longer to register. I hope he persists. Today again about twelve people showed serious interest in becoming a test subject. First we still have to see who is really coming to Portland. But if I’m honest to myself I truly hope Michael is among them. ‘Coss he seemed a kind boy to me.
