There is a lot of organizing to be done to get the presentations on the road. I agreed with Doctor Hamilton that I’d do a minimum of three. I made a promise and I want to keep it. Actually it’s a good thing for me to be busy. This nagging about my health is beginning to bore me. If I’m busy organizing presentations it’s much easier for me to deal with my family and friends’ good intentions. They want so much to help me. Now they can without getting on my nerves.

Patrick, my brother in law, said he wanted to help me with promotion materials. When he saw the pens and brochures I brought, he offered to help me by putting a Dutch version together. He’s got a small print shop and enough time to do it immediately. I translated the text while he scanned the pictures and prepared a digital design. Then we printed it. I had a great time doing this. I was taught silkscreen printing at art academy, but this is much more fun. The printing process was done digitally, even up to the folding. Fantastic! The end result is a knockout! I’m ever so grateful to Patrick for putting in all this time and material without even charging me. “That’s what family is for” he said.

And what are friends for? Jeanine called late yesterday evening to tell me she had enjoyed seeing me again but that she felt very uncomfortable in my presence. She says I’ve really changed. I emphasized that I’ve mainly come to the Netherlands to give these presentations. Why did I say that? Why do I shut myself off from the few people who have my best interests at heart? I feel like I’m ungrateful and I’m afraid I might be abusing their good intentions. That’s exactly what I’m doing with Jeanine. When she talked about our appointment for tomorrow and told me that there’s a sort of fair at the university where she works (all stuff to do with science) I asked her if that would be a good place where I could have my first presentation. She had some objections but I waved those away. After that she said she’d arrange it for me.
