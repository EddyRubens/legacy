It took me more than five hours to organize the facilities although I managed to cook inside the Research Center. I had enough of the delivery food and I wasn’t in the mood to eat outside again.

I found some cooking equipment in the relaxation room. The sight of this dusty material fancied me to go cooking. Although I had searched through the whole building, I couldn’t find any pots or pans. There had been one pan, once. Unfortunately they’d thrown it away when it was scorched. These doctors are certainly very smart but it’s obvious that they don’t know how to cook a meal. I bought some pans and all that’s necessary to prepare something tasty. It turned out to be a Danish apple soup and a vegetarian rötti. A strange combination, I know, but those were the two things I was dying to taste. My colleagues gave me a strange look when they saw me cooking.

“Are you building that up from scratch?” “Why don’t you come with us to eat downtown, that’s much easier!” “Why are you doing that? Is the microwave out of order?” They were also concerned about the safety, because there was no cooker hood. I placed the equipment in front of an open window and explained that the smoking alarm would have gone off thousand times already if it was really so sensitive. My colleagues smoke more than ten singing tea kettles at a time. In spite of all the amazement and resists there were lots of volunteers to taste it. Eventually I had cooked too little. It was nice to cook and perhaps even nicer to eat something I cooked myself. I’m sure I will do that more often.
