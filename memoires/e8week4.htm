My lovely old sweater was over the top! I entered the laboratory with the sweater in my arms. I was almost seated in the special chair when Dr. Hamilton asked me what I brought with me. “My sweater, “ I explained. “This is my favorite sweater, wasn’t I supposed to take it with me?” Dr. Tachikawa confirmed his request but he didn’t look as friendly as he used to do. “Show it to me,” he demanded. I held the sweater uncertainty. Wasn’t it good enough?

 “What an old junk!” Dr. Hamilton shouted. “From where did you get that? From a hobo?” Dr. Tachikawa and Dr. Steward came closer to take a better look. “And it stinks!”

Indeed, a vagabond fits it perfectly. The brownish greys with dark smudges give a poorly effect. The few holes I had repaired so carefully did also. I looked dismayed at my sweater and felt a bit ashamed. If you aren’t attached to this sweater as much as I am, it might shock indeed. But it is still my favorite sweater! Then it almost seemed as if Dr. Hamilton would loose his self-control. “That is totally unexepted!” he shouted. “What the hell were you thinking?”  What was wrong with him; to shout at me like that! Dr. Tachikawa understood that I had no wrong intentions. He carefully explained how my special sweater could be horrible for others. For this extraction session it was meant to transfer the colors and structure of a cloth, eventually supplemented with a small sense of convenience. Test subjects which would have my memories of this sweater implanted, would probably get very confused if this tatter feels good to them. For an extraction session, my sweater is totally unfit. That I already understood.

But what now? Jessica interfered carefully. “Do you remember the sweater that you bought on Roosevelt Avenue lately?” she almost whispered. “I mean the pink one. Wouldn’t that be perfect?” I remembered this sweater very well. Almost two weeks ago I suddenly had the impulse that I needed some new clothes and this pink sweater was a real bargain. It was very smooth and it fitted perfectly. Though I feel sorry for this acquisition. It just isn’t the right cloth for me. With all the patterns and that bright pink I look like someone else. I went to my room to get this alternative sweater. The team members were very pleased with it; this sweater really fitted them. They gave me the same explanation about the procedure as yesterday and they underlined that I should not have too much associations. I shouldn’t think about the acquisition and especially not about my favorite sweater. I only had to concentrate on the colors, the viscosity and how the wool touches my skin. I think I managed to do just that. This session went quite smooth, although we had an awkward start. My memory of the pink sweater shall be uploaded tomorrow, ‘coss Dr. Hamilton had another appointment this afternoon. I have no headache this time, but I still feel a bit irritated by the rejection of my favorite sweater. I had to eat some nasty sushi as well, hadn’t I?
