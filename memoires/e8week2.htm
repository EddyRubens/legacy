The extraction turned out to be better than I expected. I was afraid just for nothing. But it was very tiring. After they connected all the wires to the extractor, I had to do some relaxation exercises. That was difficult for me because I really had the collywobbles. They had to give me some pill for that.

I was asked to fully concentrate on a huge clock that they’d placed in front of me. The kind of clock you will see at a train station. Dr. Steward asked me with a hypnotizing voice to concentrate at all the details. The mechanical ticking distracted me from the shape and the details and this clock was also giving me too much associations. The end of time, New Year’s Eve, waiting for my boyfriend to pick me up to the prom. Dr. Hamilton wasn’t satisfied by that. He talked something through with Dr. Tachikawa who was indeed happy with the diagrams he was reading from the screen. “Keep them basic, keep them basic” Dr. Hamilton repeated. It took at least three and a half hour.

The first hour passed by very slowly; I have literally followed the pointers. After then I entered some kind of flush in which time didn’t matter. At that moment the clock became an object without a meaning and without associations. I cannot tell at which moment the extraction took place. Mark, the test subject who’d got my memory uploaded the same afternoon, said that the clock pointed five to two. That will be it then. I am so tired of today that I want to go to sleep early. And I have an headache. I guess that will be over after a good night sleep.
