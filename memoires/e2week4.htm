It appears I had a mild epileptic attack. Temporal epilepsy. I smelled petrol, a thick brownish smell with a green glow. I became all closed up and started sweating like mad. I shook my head to the left and back in a continual movement that I could not control. I made chewing noises and drooled. Very freaky, this, although I was a bit sedated while it happened and felt absolutely no fear. That came later, when I came to. Have I become epileptic? Did something go wrong in my head after all?

Dr. Hamilton told me not to be afraid. An implant seldom is accepted into the body without certain temporal neurological responses and this form of temporal epilepsy regularly occurs. The lobe does not experience the implant as part of the body and is struggling with it, as it were. I may experience another attack but I should not worry. It’s a mild, harmless form of epilepsy that leaves no traces. I won’t suffer any injuries. Except that annoying headache. The thumping just won’t stop and the nurse can’t give me any more morphine. It slows down the recovery process and has an addictive effect. As if I didn’t know.
