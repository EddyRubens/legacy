When I woke up this morning I immediately noticed that the amusement park memory was gone. I remembered of course but I didn’t feel the sensation anymore. Pity. It was a fantastic feeling that I’d love to experience everyday. I got lucky really, when I think about it. I was talking with so much enthusiasm about the sound of that roller coaster yesterday that the entire team agreed that we should try to record it.

Initially I didn’t fully understand what they were on about and technically I still don’t follow but what it comes down to is that Adrian can extract a memory in the form of a sound. He’s been working day and night with his equipment and is now able to turn data into sound. It’s a marvelous thing. Something from a science fiction story. But it really works. Adrian ran a file of the dogsledge-trip I had experienced before. I was completely shocked. “How did he…, how can he…?” His detailed explanation went in one ear and out the other. And I was still perplexed. First I thought they might be leading me on. But they all remained deadly serious and Adrian got a compliment. “What we want to do today,” Dr. Tachikawa started explaining, “is upload the amusement park memory once more. You will focus again on the cart and we will extract the necessary data for Adrian’s project. Is that alright?” Yeah, sure that’s alright. I wanted nothing more than to be at that amusement park again. This time the re-experience seemed like a repeat of yesterday’s. More concentrating on the roller coaster wasn’t possible. It seemed like I had reached the limit of this memory.

Adrian needed a lot of time to process the recorded data. So at the end of the afternoon Dr. Tachikawa came to get me and together we walked to Adrian’s computer room. Our hands touched a couple of times while we walked through the halls. I didn’t dare grab his hand though. Dr. Tachikawa was happy, I could feel that. We took large steps as if in a hurry. I wouldn’t have minded if we’d walked at a slower pace or if Adrian’s workspace had been further away because I hugely enjoyed being with Dr. Tachikawa. He gently guided me into the room. More smiling faces.

“Alright, here it comes!” Adrian said, and he started the file. Again, I was perplexed. As the sound of the thundering roller coaster filled the room all the details came back to me. This I hadn’t expected. I must have turned pale all of a sudden, because I was offered a chair to sit on. Unbelievable.
