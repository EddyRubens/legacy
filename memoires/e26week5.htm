When we were inside, he motioned me to sit down at the table opposite him. “What exactly do you know about Hans and Heather?” he asked. Oh no, I thought. That’s not how we are playing the game. “First you tell me everything you know about Sonja and Jessica,” I gave tit for tat. Kodjiro sighed for a moment and answered he knew just as much as I did about Sonja. That she had left Hugo, which he knew indeed for a couple of years already through his work. And about the fears she’s having whenever she thinks they want to kill her. I can imagine that, since I had this experience just a few moments ago in that alley…

”And for Jessica,” Kodjiro continued. “You will see her tomorrow.” That sounded reasonable and I told him all I knew about the escape of the new test subjects. About this news report on the local radio and TV in Portland and how they were described as professional criminals. I told Kodjiro that I didn’t believe they had bad intentions. Actually I do not know those test subjects, although I have spoken to Hans during a Braintec-presentation in Holland. It appears to me that they are victim of fears and persecution mania as well. “That’s possible, indeed,” Kodjiro answered. “Whenever the amygdale is over-stimulated, paranoia seems to be normal.” He explained more about the medical details, but I didn’t understand him very well. It made sense. “I wish you all the best,” he continued his argument.  “I must say that I’m not interested anymore in all the fuzz in Portland. They make a mess out of it and there’s no progress at all.”

Actually I wanted to ask Kodjiro about his role-playing part in it. But I did not dare to ask. Instead of that I asked him about Steward and his rancid practices. He jumped as if he got stung by a wasp. “And now what?” he yelled. “What are you talking about?” Kodjiro scared me. That’s not the man I used to know. I rushed to the door so that I could sneak outside if he would approach me too closely. “You have become a real spitfire yourself!” I shouted in a tempting way. “Did you also install an implant? You have exactly the same temper I had some time ago! And I used to think Japanese control their emotions so well and that they think of such behavior as barbaric!” Kodjiro broke down. He fell apart on the floor like a pudding and started gasping very softly. Slowly I came forward. “Sorry,” he sniffled. “I don’t know what happened to me...”

I cannot stand men crying. Of course they have the right to show emotions but a man crying I find disgusting. Especially if it’s Kodjiro. I muttered that everything was fine and grabbed my coat to go out for a walk. After one hour I returned. Kodjiro was playing a video-game. Lots of shooting and violence. We behaved as if nothing happened. Like we were married for years.
