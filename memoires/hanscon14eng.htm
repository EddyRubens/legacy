After a tasty breakfast we go to the temple. I have sealed the laptop to my body again to be able to send data. Cloths on top of it and nobody knows. The journey goes smooth. We learn to understand Japan more and more. At the temple side we search for a bench where we can exchange information with the Brainteccers electronically without being noticed, but in sight in case something goes wrong.

Soon two Japanese and an American arrive. The three of them introduce themselves as professor. The names must be false, I think. They find the spot completely unsuitable. We say we want to stay there. They show some equipment that needs to be connected to the electric mains, they say. After some twists we agree with the terrace of a cafe nearby.

Our group gets installed at a small table. They place their equipment and plug it in the main. I glue the pods to my body and then we connect my laptop to their equipment. Everything seems to go fine. Suddenly four Japanese jump above and they place huge screens between us and the other visitors. What seemed to be an open spot suddenly became a closed room. We try to escape quickly. Heather managed to run through the screens but I’m not getting far. Two Japanese hold me tight and besides that I am still connected to the equipment with all those wires. It doesn’t make sense to scream for help. There is a marriage going on inside the temple where they hit huge Japanese drums. Outside I hear screaming “Adrian, get them”. An American man enters and wildly hits all surrounding him so the Japanese let me go in fear. Adrian takes my arm wildly. I push against the wall. The Japanese equipment falls down with a lot of noise. The electricity comes free and I see a lot of smoke. Fortunately the wires break and I can run out with Adrian. Everyone escapes in a hurry. Adrian runs after the Brainteccers. Heather pushes me behind a stone. That stone is used by Japanese to bring offers to find happiness. So it must bring us luck as well.
