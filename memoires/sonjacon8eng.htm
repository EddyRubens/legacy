At night, around three o’clock, I went to Tachikawa’s condominium.  The number and the name matched, but the building was electronically secured. And the apartment was at the highest but one floor. An old man in a blue-grey uniform was mobbing the floor in the lobby. I tapped at the thick glass of the entrance doors and beckoned him. He tiredly went to me dragging his feet. He didn’t speak English, and I had to use my hands and feet to tell him I made an appointment with someone at number 374 (the apartment next to Tachikawa’s), but my flight was delayed, and now no one was answering the door – and so on. Ha! Little security: the man opened the door and gave me a plastic key, probably some kind of skeleton key. I took the elevator to go upstairs, it took a long time, and I felt my heart in my throat. What am I to do? Upstairs I sneaked through the corridor and listened at the apartment next to Tachikawa’s. I couldn’t hear a thing. I softly but urgently knocked at the door. Still nothing. Resolutely I moved the card through the lock and the door clicked open hardly making any sound.

It was dark. I almost stumbled over some bags in the hallway. I walked trough the apartment to the window site – there was nobody home, I was lucky. I had to regain my breath to calm down – my head was glowing of adrenaline. Softly I opened the door to the sun roof. A cold wind blew inside and I got strangled in the long lace curtain which blew inside. On my toes I headed the wall separating the two terraces. The wall was approximately two meters high, but I used a chair to watch over it, to Tachikawa’s apartment. Immediately I pulled back my head: I watched directly into his illuminated sleeping room! Then I realized they could impossibly see me while it was still dark outside, and I carefully reached my head above the wall.

I saw Dr. Tachikawa talking to a grey, tall shape. He made suggestions and looked angry. Then the other man turned round to pick up an attaché case and I immediately recognized him: it was the same man I saw in the English cottage from where I escaped!  Who spoke so lovingly about his (???) 'Project for a New Global Order'... At the back of the room was a beautifully painted Japanese folding screen, and behind that I could see a bed, a Japanese futon on the ground. Someone was sleeping on it. It seemed to be woman. I climbed from the chair and moved it towards the other side of the sun roof, to get a better view on the bed. It was a woman indeed. A beautiful young woman sleeping with a happy smile on her face. And I knew this woman. It was ... Rosanne.
