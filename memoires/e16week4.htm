Sheila looked very seriously at me and she motioned me to come and sit down.  A completely different sort of test-psychologist than Jessica, I thought, while I was observing the corners of her mouth hanging down. I felt as if somebody had called me on the spot. And maybe it was true for she started talking about the big mess I had made in the coffee-room. “And last week police came to see you because you have beaten up the place at somebody’s home!” I was shocked. Sonja had told me this earlier but I assumed that it was all a joke. “Anyway, Rosanne,” Sheila continued, “I can only observe for now that you have a lot of angry attacks lately. We’ll have to stop that right now.” She was right but if she had the slightest notion to hear me out, concerning any information about the cause of all this, she is wrong. No word about Kodjiro.

She did not even ask about it. Maybe she wasn’t such a bad psychologist after all. She did wanted to know if I had problems in finding control over myself. I had no secrets about that. Long ago, when I attended primary school, I did have those moods indeed. A boy used to sit in front of me, irritating me with his self-made small doll made out of a broken pen and a cork, again and again. He danced with it on my small table. “Tulu-tulu-tululu,” he teased. I ignored him at the time. But now it was too much. How childish thing to do. I stood up and pushed the bottom part of the chair into his face. This chair had just one single metal leg which was divided into three parts. The metal touched his mouth and a small part of his tooth broke and came off. During that time they called me a witch. Not really to tease me. I think they respected me in a way whenever I went through those angry moods. Anyway, they left me alone. Much later, in high school, I went through it all over again. The girl next to me was irritating me again with her childish manners and I hit her which caused a bleeding nose. I think I cried even more than she did.

Sheila was busy writing when I told her this. “It is not a good thing when those angry attacks return.” she said. “I have a feeling that during that time you taught yourself to keep yourself under control. Due to whatever reason you seem to got rid of those restraints. I do not see any connection between the implant or with the uploads. I do think that you are going through much stress lately. That’s why I will prescribe tranquillizers for you.” I left her room with a bottle of pills. I still do not know whether I’ll take them.
