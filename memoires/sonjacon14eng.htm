We took Paul’s car and drove to the location he had chosen – a quiet suburb. In a locker in the entrance hall of the building Paul, who was now somewhat tense, found the parachute and a case with the instruments he needed. He took Rosanne and Adrian to the top, while I waited down there. It took a long time before he came back down again. "They’re very afraid but that’s a good thing, it will make the evidence convincing.," he said, as he, with the ease that you’d expect from a doctor, filled a syringe with a murky looking fluid from a small bottle. For just a moment our eyes met. He smiled. "When all this is over, I’ll invite you to my Arizona ranch." My heart speed increased, and I do believe I was blushing. "But let’s round off this job first.." he said, determined. Very different from Hugo, I thought to myself.

It was a strange sight; two people hanging by a white parachute, smiling, hand in hand, slowly floating downwards from a steel blue sky, almost without a sound, only the rubbing of silk and the resistance of the wind, and some gulls flying somewhere near. Paul injected them the moment they came down and checked the extractors. Before they fell into a deep sleep, he helped them get into the car, while I collected the parachutes and squeezed them in the trunk. In no time we were on our way, shooting past the virgin lawns of the suburb. No one spoke.

I was in the front, next to Paul, feeling amazingly relaxed. Paul started talking softly to me. He gave me instructions on how to take the M-Stores to Kodjiro and the Canadian to prove that I had killed Rosanne as well as Adrian. He was clearly worried, but I put him at ease. What could go wrong now? Adrian and Rosanne were in a deep sleep on the backseat. We turned to the motorway as twilight set in. I felt wonderful. I looked at Paul. He was an attractive man, with beautiful, pitch-black eyes and flowing grey hair. Suddenly I longed to go horseback riding with him on the Arizona prairies. And then; evenings by the fireplace... My imagination was running wild. Paul seemed to be guessing my thoughts and grabbed my hand without looking at me. Quietly droning, the car took us over the asphalt into the night.
