Robbert’s time with Braintec is almost over. His implant has been removed and when he’s fully recovered from the operation he will go back home. But he doesn’t have much to go back to. His wife has filed for divorce. He does want to leave though. “It’s been long enough, my time is up.” He said. “Time to start a new life.” 

Maybe it’s time for me to start something new too. But what? I’d better not think too much about such things. It only makes me feel depressed. It bothers me that I don’t know what I want. Even though the air has cleared, I feel less at home here than ever. I think about the Netherlands a lot. My friends, family; I feel like I’m really starting to miss them.

This afternoon I was talking about it with Sonja. It’s like I’m grabbing every chance to talk about my home country. I think she understands my struggle because she asked me if I wouldn’t prefer to go home as well. Just like Robbert. I couldn’t honestly answer that question. How important is it to have a home? I’ve got a place to myself here as well. And I like my little room here. It’s a nice place where I can be myself. What more do you need a home for? Roots perhaps. I haven’t seen my family and the surroundings where I grew up for such a long time, maybe I need my roots affirmed. Just to remind myself where I’m coming from.
