I don’t know what to think of all these changes. It only gives me a hard time. My arms and legs are covered by small wounds. I don’t have the slightest idea how they’ve reached my body. Anyway, it can’t be small animals. I’ve checked for that. I must have scratched the skin with my nails all by myself. And as a matter of fact, it pleases me to scratch away the encrusted blood. I will be satisfied if I don’t feel crusts anymore.

I’m not the only one suffering from stress. I don’t exaggerate if I say that we all are suffering from tensions. Sonja fiddles about with her hair and Steve is playing drums on the table with his fingers. How irritating. I’m almost getting a nervous break-down.

Nobody knows what we’re up to and what is going to happen right now. I overheard that test subjects are intending to leave from here. As an experienced assistant I do not recommend anybody going home too soon. I try to pay little attention to all tensions by telling jokes and thinking positively. Sometimes it helps, sometimes it doesn’t work. Whether Braintec will survive I cannot guarantee for sure. I feel the pressure myself too. Will Braintec survive without Dr. Tachikawa? And how about the financial situation? Adelle could tell us more about it. She had lunch with Dr. Hamilton and talked about the problem dealing with money. There is equipment sufficient for about twenty days, after that, most policies on subsidies stop lending money. ‘Coss then the money we receive will be just a drop in the ocean. Dr. Hamilton fears the worst scenario. 

Adelle told me she felt sorry for him. The director is one of her best friends, calling him by his first name right from the beginning. He knows her husband Donn very well. Her husband? I didn’t know she had a husband! I didn’t show my astonishment because we all wanted to listen to what Adelle was going to tell us. Obviously she had an appointment with Dr. Hamilton. She suggested asking Frank for help. Frank is an officer in the army and has the privilege to know important persons. For years the Minister of Defense invested in all sorts of developments which can be helpful sooner or later. It was Adelle’s idea to contact Frank trying to solve our problem. She thinks he is willing to support Braintec. Meanwhile I’m sure Dr. Hamilton gave him a call.

The idea Braintec will discontinue within twenty days is a thing that leaves its mark on me. Where will I go when it happens? I cannot return to Greenland and I do not fancy Holland.

The only person, apparently, not suffering from stress and being sure of himself, is Adrian. Since he got use of this instrument, testing precisely all sorts of information, he cannot stop. He made a connection to his developed system and is very pleased about the results. His original ambition to visualize dreams is his hot item again. Maybe he can realize it by making use of this instrument! I desperately wish he can continue working on it without interruptions. So much talent. It would be a dead shame if he has to stop using it.
