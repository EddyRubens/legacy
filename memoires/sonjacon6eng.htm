Hugo called me again from Paris. He said that he was worried, concerning our last conversation. He asked if I spoke with the psychologist on a regular basis (how did he know that there is a psychologist at Braintec?!!!). I told him that I didn't encounter any difficulties with the treatments. But he insisted that he had noticed that I wasn't completely "myself".

Indeed, I am not "myself" lately, but that isn't so strange. In my attempt to find out the truth I made an appointment with Tachikawa. His look was surprised, but he agreed to meet me in the Japanese garden that afternoon. He arrived fifteen minutes late, though punctual as he is, and he looked absent-minded. He asked impatiently why I had to see him "so urgently". I told him about my dreams, and that I was worried about the "strange thoughts" that came into my mind recently. He asked me what kind of thoughts, and I told him that I knew about him and Rosanne. He smiled in a relaxed manner, and said: "Well, that Rosanne... I think she fell in love with me, and that is of course a common phenomenon, but she will get over it..." Then I told him that I had the idea in my head that he might know my husband. "Sonja," he said while cautiously looking at his hands, "Sonja my dear... You are making things up in your mind. I will make an appointment with the psychologist. This isn't a good development. I will put a temporary hold on your treatment." After that he took me to the pharmacy and gave me two kinds of pills. "You will take these three times a day and this one you must take now. Go and have a good and long sleep. I think that you're just a little stressed out." He gave me a glass of water and waited until I swallowed the pill. Then he brought me to my room, and tucked me in, like a child, and he waited until I fell into a deep sleep.
