It doesn’t work out anymore. Now that I’ve been here too long I cannot behave as a tourist anymore. Acting as if I were Japanese is impossible. Their habits are incredible and it doesn’t strike me. I cannot be myself here any longer.

Now that I have stayed here a bit longer, I realize how complicated people can be. What I experienced first as friendly but standoffish, became much more complicated now. I really don’t understand. When to face people while at certain moments it’s very impolite, when and in particular how deep I have to bow forwards, which gestures I have to refuse out of respect and which manners are absolutely not done. It’s hard on me. It seems impossible for me to adjust.

No wonder I cannot understand Kodjiro. He may have experienced half of his career abroad; Japanese customs are not new to him. And now that he is ’back home’ again, he behaves like a Japanese more than ever. His strange reactions, his silence for hours and all his other incredible behavior obviously has a meaning. There’s something I will never ever understand. Why they just cannot say what they think or what they want? Why they have to act in such a mysterious way? 

Maybe I misuse his hospitality. For a couple of weeks I have been staying in his apartment and I behave like I’m living here. According to Japanese ideas there is much room here, but according to space it looks more like a student’s place. Now and then we stand in the way. We constantly argue and sometimes tension has gotten so badly that for a few times I had been considering flying back to Portland right away. A couple of times I suggested to talk about this, but Kodjiro don’t want me to leave and he doesn’t want to speak about it either. Who is he to decide about me? It won’t work anymore. Wished we could simply talk about it!

It has become my habit loafing about the streets in town. Just killing time. Usually I walk about completely lost. It doesn’t matter to me anymore how much electronics are sold in Akihabara. And all those exceptional modern and female shoes in town, never for sale in my size, can’t grab me either. Is this being homesick?

Walking through town is just a poor excuse. Actually I was hoping Sonja would drag me again into this alley. And then spending much time and fun together. For that reason I often walk about the district where I had given my blood and where this strange meeting was held. I have given the city-map to Sonja. Maybe I’ll meet her here soon.
