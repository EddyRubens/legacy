Tonight I woke up sweating all over. I had a miscarriage. Three times. After the third one I gave up. After that many difficult years went by before I accepted that I would never try to have children anymore. I had to cry. So much sorrow and pain but I came over it. I felt tired and went back to sleep again.

I gave Adelle a big hug. She was surprised and gave me a hug too. When I told her I had enjoyed her memory she wanted to know everything about it. She really enjoyed every single bit of it. I wondered if I should tell her about the miscarriages. She must have seen my doubt. She kept on asking why I did not tell her everything, until I told her. She turned out pale and gave a sigh. I took her hand and squeezed it. “You are a beautiful woman,” I told her. “You give us so many good things. Thank you!” A tear was running over her cheek and short afterwards another showed up. When her eyes were soaking wet, she immediately dried her eyes with a pink handkerchief. A deep sigh, a smile and all her grief had disappeared. “Come let us go and buy some chocolate!” she said and a few minutes later we were heading to the best sweet shop in Portland. Adelle is a regular customer in this shop and knows all the salesmen by name. We bought one pound of stuffed chocolates and drove back. On my way back home I suddenly discovered that I had found out something very intimate about Adelle. Something she’d probably never would have shared with me. I asked her about it but she skipped the whole thing. “It must have been good for a reason,” she said. “I’m rather surprised that the information did show up through the upload.” Yes, I can imagine.
