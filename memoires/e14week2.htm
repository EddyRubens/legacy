Today nothing had been scheduled in my agenda. Tomorrow I will give presentations again; three a day. Right now I rather would have done one of these locations to divide the rush somehow. Unfortunately no possibility for that. Sometimes you cannot make up your mind. Actually it was great to have the day off. That brought me a chance to visit Amsterdam for the day. I still got some friends living over there and thought of paying them a visit. No time for that after all. I did some shopping, bought myself new shoes, etc. And I also visited the Stedelijk Museum. Good to be back in Amsterdam. I really like this city.

I was joining the queue for a strawberry milkshake at Burger King. Milkshakes and bagels have become an essential part of my daily diet. Hard to find bagels here but luckily milkshakes are. When it was my turn my portable phone started ringing. The TROS speaking, somebody from Radio Online. If I was able to come to their program this evening. Through others they had heard about Braintec and they thought it a great idea to discuss the matter on the radio. I would have a chance to explain about Braintec and request people to become new test subjects. Perfect indeed, a free offer for publicity! I used to listen to Radio Online with pleasure; it is an enjoyable program. All arrangements seemed to be last-minute but that is quite normal. I had to hurry a bit to reach the studio in time. Actually I wanted to buy a souvenir for Kodjiro. Lack in duty.

It was a very pleasant experience. I was nervous seeing this huge blue microphone just in front of my nose. It did not matter. When Francisco van Jole (one of the journalists) interviewed my first memory, I enthusiastic started talking about my childhood. Explained that I was lying in my bed and felt so peaceful. Of course he meant which memory I got first as upload! At Braintec we usually talk about memories in general continuously, that’s why I started talking about my own first memory automatically. Maybe nobody noticed it and they assume that it was my first upload. Fine with me. A memory which I would like to experience again, anyway.
