“Are you coming to Mr. Soesasji’s garden this afternoon?” Dr. Tachikawa asked me after the meeting. “Of course! I’d love to!” Again I reacted much too enthusiastic. Both of us had to organize a few things so we made an appointment at 4 p.m. Time goes slow when you’re looking forward to something. We drove to the garden in his car and had some tea with Mr. Soesjasji. At first hand Dr. Tachikawa and Mr. Soesjasji spoke Japanese which I could not appreciate. As a European you cannot understand one word of it. I felt left out and didn’t know how to react. I could feel it was a very serious conversation though. What were they talking about? About Hugo, or about the improved M-Stores? The moment I really felt uncomfortable they’d probably realize they were not behaving correctly. They switched over to English but were only discussing small talk. Now and then I joined the conversation.

Mr. Soesasji gave us a basket full of Japanese sushi. Kodjiro and I sat down on a raked field of white small stones and were anxious to see what we had been offered. Naturally we got an overdose of sushi! Glad we also discovered small rice balls. They were all mine. These balls contained rice wrapped into seaweed, then stuffed with a very sweet paste of beans in the middle. I did like them. I took them all.

“This is absolutely my favorite spot in this garden.” Kodjiro muttered overlooking the field with white raked gravel of at least 50 square meters. The small stones formed organic circles like rainbows. “When I sit here for a while, I experience silence and emptiness. Serenity is meditation caused by Zen.” Kodjiro explained. That man and I were so different from each other. Usually I try hard to avoid this sort of gravel whenever I see it. It makes me nervous. I’m always a bit worried to admit the feeling running through it, kicking those small stones into any direction.

“Tell me now Kodjiro, was it your valentine’s present?” I thought I’d never dared asking. Kodjiro wiped off a small grain of rice from his upper lip and said: “No. I do like you. You are an intriguing woman but I’m not in love with you. And if I was, I would not have sent you a bouquet, I think.” I was content. Strangely enough I was pleased hearing that nothing was serious between me and Kodjiro. He was not in love with me and suddenly I was not obsessed by the idea anymore. I accepted it for what it was. Maybe this Zen-stuff had good influence on me.

I discovered another box between the sushi and the small balls of rice. Kodjiro opened it. Three new M-Stores. He got a special sort of server out of his pocket. “I want to show you something. Okay?” he asked. Wow, right here on this spot in the Japanese garden! I always welcome another upload, especially on such a beautiful location and with such good company! Kodjiro put aside some of my hair to strip the bare spot on my skull. There he installed the server. I could only see that he took an M-Store, the left one, and then all of a sudden I was in a typical American drive-in restaurant. I was sitting in front of myself! I was watching myself, staring at a pasta-menu. Yes, with gorgonzola cheese, I am so fond of. But it was not me, I looked at myself. I also had pasta decorated with small bits of bacon. We talked to each other and ate our pasta. I felt attracted to the woman opposite me. I felt attracted to myself! Very touching conversation and tension was felt. I would like to pull her over the pasta towards me and kiss her! I did not. We started giggling and I was talking about a…… mixer! Oh! This is,... I am Jessica!
