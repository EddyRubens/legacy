How could I be so stupid to think it would all be like a 007-movie? Yesterday night, at the terrace next to Tachikawa’s, when I saw Rosanne sleeping, I definitely felt like the hero of the story. I had probably overlooked ‘some details’ because of the adrenaline. Details, which caused me a lot of trouble. 

The moment, when I looked behind the wall, and saw Rosanne sleeping, I felt a hand with a piece of stinking textile over my mouth and an arm that pulled me down. I couldn’t see who it was in the dark, but when I felt the ground I slipped away, into unconsciousness. After indefinite period of time I woke up, because someone was holding an ammoniac capsule under my nose. I wildly slammed about me, but someone held me tight and I couldn’t release myself.

I seemed to be inside Tachikawa’s apartment. It was still dark outside, but I saw it was getting lighter at the horizon and the birds had already started singing. Opposite to me sat again that Canadian, and Tachikawa filled some bowls with tea. I was furious, but mostly at myself. "Well, Mrs. Boot Oil... what should we do with you?” he said. He wasn’t as friendly as he was in the cottage – when everything seemed to go his way. “What have you done with Rosanne?” I asked shortly. “Don’t you worry about Rosanne madam, she had had a wonderful night.” The Canadian quickly looked inside the adjoining sleeping room. So Rosanne was still lying there?

The Canadian opened the suitcase which I had already seen when I stood at the sun roof. There where uploads in it, at least it seemed so, but they had a different form, and beneath a little plastic cap at the end there was a long needle. The man took one, read the label, and removed the cap. I braced myself. The man behind me holding me did so as well. "Mrs. Boot Oil," the Canadian said, "you might be honored, because you will be the first human being to taste our latest experiment..." "No!" I shouted, and I resisted heavily, but the man who was holding me kept me in a firm grip.

The needle came closer to my face. “You know too much,” the Canadian said, “and that is not so clever.” I thought, and I thought so hard that my mind was hurting me. "Wait!" I shouted. The needle stopped while its point already reached my nose.  "Wait!" I shouted again while I tortured my brains to come up with a plan. I laughed sourly. “If I already know as much as you think, why should I still reject your plan?”  Tachikawa and the Canadian looked surprised at each other. Then they looked at me again. “Explain yourself!” Tachikawa snarled. I slowly and apparently calmly told them that I started to admire their real work for Braintec more and more through time, as if I knew everything about The Plan. And that I came to Tokyo to find out how I could become part of that.
