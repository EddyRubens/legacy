It’s raining cats and dogs. One endless flood coming down from heaven as if the dam’s burst. Twigs and leaves being taken along in little streams. The little light that comes through the dark clouds is diffused by the heavy curtains of rain. Besides the clattering of water there is nothing to be heard. No dogs outside, let alone the people to walk them.

I couldn’t stay in any longer. On bear feet I stood out in the pouring rain as if taking a shower: the water was not cold and made my nightgown stick to my body. Around my feet a puddle formed that engulfed my toes. The rainwater blending in with my tears. I wasn’t sad and I haven’t felt any pain for the last few days. Still the tears kept coming. Without making a sound I let everything flow from me as the rain washed me clean. I suppose I stood in the rain like that for at least ten minutes. I wouldn’t have minded to stay out there longer. I felt at one with the elements. I hadn’t felt so calm in a long time. But it wasn’t allowed. It wasn’t normal. Two assistants pulled me back in. They brought me to my room and recommended me to take a warm shower and then hit the sack. I have showered enough but I am tired. Crying has worn me out.
