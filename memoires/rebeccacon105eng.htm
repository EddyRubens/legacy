He was holding a heavy metal flashlight in his right hand. I figured that must have been what he hit me with. I was suprised, but lucky, that he didn't knock me unconscience with his swing. Though I was dazed from the strike on the head, I was impressed by my ability to wipe him out with one blow. I stood up, still blurred, and made sure the Band-Aid box was still in my pocket. I quickly concluded that I was scared, injured and needed the assistance of a man. (Obviously the first who came to my mind was Josh, but he was not an option at this particular moment.)

I ran to Thomas' room as fast as I could and banged on the door. He opened it wearing nothing but his black jockey boxers. I was too afraid to wait in the hall until he got dressed, so I pushed myself by him and ran in. I closed and locked the door behind me, while he was reaching for the light switch. "What's going on?...Rebecca......what's wrong?" he asked me. He put his hands gently above my shoulder, one just past my neck, and the other close to my chin, so the he could look me in the eyes. When he pulled his hand from my neck, to sweep the hair out of my eyes, his hand was covered in blood. His eyes grew in size and he demanded to know what happened and who did this to me. Tears were streaming down my cheeks and I started stuttering when I tried to speak. (Fear had a way of taking over me like that.) I was so upset that nothing I said seemed to make any sense. I was talking too quickly and jumbling my words together. I calmed down a bit then, finally, was able to tell him everything that happened at the Staybridge and what happened just minutes ago.

"What if I was asleep in there, Thomas?" I said. "Who knows what he could have...or would have done!"
Thomas sat me in the desk chair and said. "I want you to stay here...I'm about to go down there and kick some ass." He told me to lock the door behind him when he left, and not to open it for anyone. He quickly dressed in dark blue jeans and a white thermal shirt. He slid on his sneakers and went running out of the room. I locked the door and went into his bathroom the wipe some of the blood from the back of my head. My wet towel tactic wasn't working too well, but the cool water on the back of my throbbing head felt wonderful!
I went back out into his room and sat on the bed. I stared into subconscience, not really thinking of anything in particular, when a knock on the door slapped me back into reality. My eyes widened and my stomach turned inside out.

I got up and ran to the door. I listened for a minute but whoever was there made no sound. I did a quick survey of Thomas' room in search of something I could use as a weapon. I saw some dumbbells at the foot of the bed and hurried to retrieve one. I trudged the heavy weight with me back to the door and said in a demanding tone, "Who is it?" There was no answer, just a laugh. I knew it was Andrews. "What do you want? Just go away!" I screamed out to him. I was hoping my voice would wake Thomas' next door room neighbors, but then considered his loud music. They either had to be deaf or were non-exhistant, two qualities that gave me no advantage. The hall was quiet. I turned off the light and bent down to see if I could see a shadow under the door frame. I wanted to burst into tears when I realized that there was not a shadow of a man. There was the shadow of two men.
