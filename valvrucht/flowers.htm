Hieronder een interview met Gerard van Flowers In The Dustbin, afgenomen in december 2005. FITD waren een deel van de anarcho-punkscene in de jaren ’80, al waren hun muziek en teksten allesbehalve cliché zoals die van zovele andere groepen wel waren. Ze waren nooit een ‘grote naam’ zoals Conflict, Crass, Dead Kennedys, maar zoals ze zelf zeggen : wij waren meer bezig met direkte aktie, wat niet van alle groepen in die tijd gezegd kan worden.

Deze site wordt in januarie grondig bijgewerkt, en dan zullen illustraties aan dit interview worden toegevoegd, waaronder een interview dat ik in 1985 van hen afnam voor mijn toenmalige punk-fanzine ‘Lastig’.

 

Hi,

 

Here’s the answers – I’ve left out some questions as they were too open ended and would require a book to answer !

 

All  the best

 

Gerard

 

 

-I interviewed you once in 1985/6 for my fanzine Lastig, and indeed the questions were so simple: Anarchy? Vivisection? Eating meat? Sympathy with left-wing urban guerillas like RAF and CCC? You said afterwards: it was as if bands were filling in these questions by writing a song about every well-known issue… what anarcho-bands were an exception to the rule you would say…?

 

Most of the best ones I suppose – Poison Girls, Rubella Ballet, The Mob, The Apostles, Zounds, Blyth Power etc Anyone who didn’t use the phrase ‘the system’, perhaps. Also some lesser known bands like The Committee, who were only lesser known because they didn’t play up to all the clichés. Of course, the more clichés you used, the bigger you became within that scene.

 

I was always uncomfortable with lyrics that were sure they were ‘right’ about something – I always instinctively felt that was dangerous and sometimes clearly insincere.

 

-What was the “Vethixo” disco? “Vethixo” was part of a new language you were creating, could you expand a bit more ; why, what for, etc. Some words like ‘love’, ‘god’, ‘bliss’ or many other words, what did they sound like in your language?

 

I didn’t get very far with the language. To be honest I can’t remember what the idea was, but I guess it was equal mixes of youthful arrogance and pretension!

 

-What was the Wapping anarchy-centre like, the bands, the people, the frustrations of drug-abuse maybe?

 

It was horrible – it was the first time I, or my friends, had come across fellow punks that we didn’t actually like – there always seemed to be the solidarity of the outsider up till that place. There were a couple of interesting bands I saw there – Twelve Cubic Feet spring to mind – but generally I didn’t like it there at all. I don’t understand what you mean by the frustrations of drug abuse in this context, but for the record, I wasn’t taking drugs at all and don’t remember seeing any at Wapping. Not that I was exactly a regular. The only fond memory I have of the place is singing ‘You Made Me Love You’ (an old crooner song by Judy Garland) a capella and the sound man putting loads of echo on my voice – quick thinking on his part! I remember Andy Martin from the Apostles enjoying that moment but I don’t many people got it.

 

There was another anarchy-centre (Centro Iberico?), what was that like?

 

That was a bit better, but not much. Nobody I knew really went to those places unless we were playing (‘we’ incidentally were the Anabollic Steroids – my first band – not FITD). The Centro Iberico was run by old Spanish anarchists, but they were never at the gigs or anything.

 

Which was the one financed by the Crass/Poison Girls single ‘Bloody revolutions/Persons Unknown?’

 

Wapping.

 

-I know a lot but not everything, but could you tell the readers about the ‘All The Madmen’ records and ‘96 Tapes’ labels? Who ran it? What bands were on it. Am I right to think a demo of Pandora’s Box was also on 96 tapes – I’d love to see the ‘96’ catalogue? (And what was the name of Pandora’s Box before they changed their name, under their former name, weren’t they on a well-sold compilation album (Bullshit Detector 2?)?)

 

At the times we had contact with them, All The Madmen was run by Alistair Livingstone from the Puppy Collective / Black sheep housing co-op. Who was, and is, a genius now writing a blog called Green Galloway. 96 Tapes was run by Rob Challice

Who went on to work in the music bizness. I don’t remember Pandora’s Box.

 

-What other bands in the first half of the eighties were musically different from the rest, I’m thinking of The Suspect’s singer (Dave Asgrove???), Null&Void, Blyth Power, Pandora’s Box, Chumabawankas (sic?) of course, name and describe a few others please?

 

Loads – my favourite band was Dexy’s Midnight Runners – a great British soul band. I loved all sorts of music, as long as it had a heart and / or a brain – in other words, a soul. At the time, I was also listening to the 60s singer Melanie Safka a lot too. And Adam And The Ants were spectacular in the short period between donning the red Indian look and deciding to go for children as a target market.

 

Southern Death Cult were great for a while too, as were Bow Wow Wow. I really like Chumbawamba by the way, though I wasn’t keen back then.

 

-Who were Great Society,

 

Grace Slicks first band before Jefferson Airplane

 

Monochrome Set,

 

Stylish post-punk band featuring Andy Warren from Adam & The Ants.

 

Sex Gang Children,

 

They were around at the same time as Southern Death Cult but were more quirky and less ‘rock’.

 

Rauschenberg,

 

Robert Rauschenberg was an American Pop Artist

 

Ornette Coleman?

 

An American saxophonist – free jazz etc

 

-You also mention Modern English: I think their first album (‘Mesh and Lake’???) was superb but it got watered down later. What other records between ’77 and ’84  by bands like Bauhaus and Southern Dead Cult were and are still in your collection?

 

None by bands like that (since I moved over to Cds). A quick glance at my CD rack shows what I’ve bothered buying again:

Dirk / Kings – Adam & The Ants

Can’t Stand The Rezillos

Crass – Best Before 1984

Jilted John – True Love stories

ATV – The Image Has Cracked

Gang Of Four – Entertainment

The Slits – Cut

Subway Sect compilation

…that’s all from the punk side of things. Unless you count Dread Meets Punk Rockers Uptown, which is a compilation of all the reggae they used to play at the Roxy, and Sex, which is a comp of the records on the jukebox in the Kings Rd Shop.

 

-Did you know that the composer of Nat King Cole’s ‘Nature boy’ was pre-hippy Edhen Abhez? He’s got a great CD out now, released by RE/Search I think?

 

No I had no idea – thanks for this info! I got to Nature Boy via Nat King Cole rather than anything hippy. Though obviously I must have picked up on the mystic lipstick where the song was sealed with a loving kiss. Or something.

 

-What went wrong at the gig you did with Flux and KUKL (Björk’s band on Crass recs)? Flux were quite dogmatic I think whereas KUKL were not?

 

We (FITD) originally organized it as a Stop The City benefit – we were the only band involved at this point - and asked Flux to play. Then they muscled in, chucking bands we’d asked to play off the bill and replacing them with their own impositions, one of whom was KUKL – for a band of supposed anarchists, they (Flux) sure enjoyed wielding power- the power of their popularity as right-on anarchist role-models. You can imagine how we felt about that.

 

-And tell me everything about the tour with Fuzzbox? I remember they had a hit? Please tell everything that went good, wrong, neutral, and especially hilaric?

 

This question is too big axel – it would require a book to answer it! Suffice to say it was an enormous mismatch but we had the time of our lives! I still have my tour diary and Antje has hers – we may one day put them on the website.

 

-Mars Bars Tour (Mars being ‘attacked’ by the ALF around that time) and you think the animal liberation movement was the strongest thing punk ever produced. I do think so too, maybe apart from the squatters movement all over Europe.

 

No, not punk, but the anarcho punk movement. I think the wider punk movement influenced all sorts of things, often to do with design and aesthetics. I thought the squatting movement across Europe was established before punk? But yes, I think the strongest thing the anarcho-punk culture did was enliven the animal liberation movement.

 

Contradictions are always part of life, aren’t they?

 

I think contradictions are the crucial difference between dogma (which produces totalitarianism) and pragma (which doesn’t have to). I think contradictions are  beautiful – they create the spaces through which ideas can be explored and progressive ideas emerge.

                                               

Contradictions are an integral part of any life lived beyond dogma and rules, and therefore any attempt at freedom.

 

-Drinking and smoking joints, that was what you were doing, as so many other people did (I still remember a somewhat rare sort of black hash I bought at school the same day your 12” and Lost Cherrees LP arrived at home via mail-order. A day never to forget, really. But as I see the cheeks on your pictures I wonder if other drux were involved, speed maybe? Not that I care by the way, and I’m probably wrong? But you do admit to drink too much nowadays?

 

We didn’t really take any other drugs on a regular basis, though we all dabbled with speed and acid. I don’t drink at all these days! If you’re thinking of the lyrics on ‘Diary Of An Alcoholic’, those were written about 1992. The lyrics on South London aren’t (as people sometimes presume) autobiographical – parts of it are taken from my experiences, but then changed and messed about with to make a better song. Indeed, that’s the lyric where I realized not everything had to reflect my own experience 100%. Which is either artistically growing up or selling out or both or neither.

 

-Do you still have the booklet that came with “All the best…” demo? And what exactly got lost in the fire of Cold Harbour recs, who released your latest single ‘Lick my…’, master tape, video’s?

 

This won’t seem overly nostalgic, but 1) no and 2) I don’t know – I think it was a Rick Astley album as there was a big studio downstairs. I remember being driven mad when The Christians were recording ‘Ideal World’ down there – it was all I heard for about a month.

 

-Are you playing live again? Want to release more music on CD? Reactions of public and/or labels?

 

We will perform live again, but not in the standard gig way unless we can find some way of subverting the context. I’m not sure why we should put music on CDs now we have mp3 culture, but never say never. I haven’t even thought about labels. The public reaction has been quite astonishing in terms of downloads – thousands every month. I guess this must be the internet at work!

 

-I made the comparison with my fave artists these days: Antony and the Johnsons? I’m not saying you sound the same, but just melancholy with piano? Do you see the comparison, or is my imagination running away with me?

 

Funny – I hadn’t heard them when I got your interview but I have since and I think he’s great. I think he displays a certain honest vulnerability that I can identify with and I’d be flattered by any comparison.

 

-What would you advise or readers to listen to, read, watch (paintings, sculptures,…), please expand a little on your interests?

 

My interests these days are with art, football and love. I’d advise readers to go their own way and love whatever music/art they love no matter what their contempories think. That’s the most important thing – if you love it and everyone else hates it, still love it.

 

-Any last words (for now)?

 

TA!
