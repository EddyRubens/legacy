Our goals in Military Science:

Goal 1. “To continue the development of military professional competencies.” This goal is achieved through the Integrated Memory Storage Program. The high points of the program were the memory storage activities conducted by all undergraduate students during the two Memory Days per year (one day in the first semester and one day in the last). These activities were designed to be enjoyable while the student is available as a test subject for Braintec at the same time.

Goal 2. “To maintain a military environment conducive to academic study and professional research development.” This goal is achieved by maintaining a regimented, military environment, supporting research development  but also easing the student's re-integration into the mainstream after completion of studies.

Goal 3. “To create opportunities to cultivate a balanced lifestyle, complementary to the military ethos.” The storage of student's memories was motivated by the desire to produce a confident military force, being able to maintain the physical appearance, bearing and depth of character expected in an open society. This way, they underscore the importance of physical condition and the intricately linked general health. At a later point in time, after potential combat missions, these cadets can review the memories they have chosen whenever they want. This can probably reduce posttraumatic stress.   
 

  

 

 

 

Braintec office at the Royal Military Academy (KMA) in the Netherlands

Think of the Netherlands, and what may come to mind is a quaint countryside of historic canal houses, fields of tulips, and of course those ubiquitous windmills. Though the Netherlands today is a highly urban and technologically sophisticated nation, the "old" country still plays a valuable role as a memory. Nowadays the Netherlands are able to copy and store memories as well, since Braintec run the temporary Integrated Memory Storage Program at the Royal Military Academy in 2003.

To support is our mission. The Royal Dutch Society for Military Science aims to foster Military Science in its broadest sense, ranging from peace and support operations to military technology. Funds are provided by the Ministry of Defense. Braintec opened a temporary office in the HBS-building of the Royal Military Academy (KMA) in Breda in appreciation of this support, where we conserved the most precious memories of military personnel in training.

 

The Integrated Memory Storage Program (IMSP) was designed to improve the psychomotor skills and lay knowledge of undergraduate students at the Military Academy. It presented every student with the opportunity to develop desirable character traits while participating in enjoyable memory uploads. Since all the facilities were available at the Academy, uploads were conducted locally.

The students could participate in a number of memory uploads. Finally, two days per semester were designated as Memory Days, during which memory extractions and drill competitions took place. The duality of roles that students at the Military Academy were faced with could sometimes be confusing to the novice. It was expected that they would be full-time students and test subjects at the same time.

Officer-students are always expected to display depth of character, an appreciation for the finer things in life and the ability to socialise with comrades-in-arms and civilians alike. Opportunities for a balanced lifestyle were created by uploading each other's memories. The conserved memories were usually interesting and often contentious, leading to lively discussions among the students.

An opportunity to archive these memories arose out of the Military Academy’s participation in the Braintec Program. Since one of the primary reasons for participating at this research program was the conservation of memories, the Academy’s students were saving their most precious moments in life. This may come in hand when a graduated student is sent to war. In case of posttraumatic stress or other misfortune, the soldier’s best memories are stored at the Braintec office and ready to be uploaded again.
