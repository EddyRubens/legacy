Frequently Asked Questions
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
It is not possible to procure such an amount of insights in medicine as we do with this project without the use of human test subjects.
How does it work?
Braintec has developed two kinds of implants. The smallest implant, called MSI (Magnetic Stimulator Implant), is capable of sending memories from a server to the memory area. The MESI (Magnetic Extractor Stimulator Implant) is also capable of extracting the memories outside the brain and sending them to a server outside the body. The server is placed diagonally above the left ear. It is a round device that is technically similar to a Transcranial Stimulator. Small plates can be placed inside the server. These plates are called M-stores, they actually contain the memories. Especially the brain area around the amygdale and the hypothalamus are stimulated during the re-experience of memories. The neural network takes over these qualia almost directly and activates the whole system to process the new arrived information.
Has Braintec already gained results?
Yes, and we are proud to say those results are quite impressive. We now have more insights into the human  experience of memories than ever before! We have started working on a parallel research to find a cure for Altzheimer's disease which will be financed by the ProMed pharmaceutical company. And we expect much more sensational discoveries in the near future.
What if something goes wrong?
We can always rely on an extensive insurance to cover any incurred harm.
Is it possible to upload other information than memories?
Perhaps. We have no experience with that and it doesn’t interest us either. But we certainly don’t exclude the possibility.
What kind of memories do test subjects re-experience?
Every use of human test subjects brings some risks and so does ours. Our research is always guided by experts and the risks we’re taking are very limited. The importance of the expected results of our research is in moderate proportion to the risks and scruples to test subjects of our team.
Isn’t your research too dangerous?
During the early stages of our research we only worked with very basic memories, for example the memory of a red balloon. At this moment we are working with more complex memories like the remembrance of a location, general impressions or events. It doesn’t matter how old these remembrances are. When we start working with a newly inserted implant, we start with basic memories before we go on to the ‘important stuff’.

Is it pleasant to re-experience memories? 
Most of the test subjects find experiencing memories as surprisingly pleasant.
How much do the test subjects get paid?
It is prohibited to attract test subjects through payments or to ‘buy’ contribution. Our remunerations are as large as the law permits. For example: being a test subject for 28 days makes $ 5240 excluding travel costs.
Is it difficult to become a test subject?
No, but you have to be in good health. You can simply apply by filling in our questionnaire and sending it back to us. Only when you complete the registration as accurately as possible, we can evaluate your suitability as a test subject.
How old do you have to be to become test subject?
We are searching for people of any age to take part in this project. Anyone over 18 years can become a test subject for Braintec.
Are you allowed to break off your commitment to Braintec at any time you wish?
Yes, of course. Braintec cannot force people to take part in their experiments against their will.
