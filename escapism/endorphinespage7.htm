Unfortunately, I wasn’t able to take pictures when the biggest mass of ice calved down. It was so eminently beautiful; I didn’t dare to miss a second of it. So I was nailed to the ground with the camera in my right hand and my mouth wide open. Of course, I could have pushed the shutter release without looking through the lens, but there was no time to think about that. I was totally caught up in the spectacle. This brick of ice was so enormous that it looked as if the whole glacier was collapsing. When it hit the surface it filled the air with water drops. I could see a clear rainbow for more then ten seconds. It took me at least ten minutes before my knees stopped shaking.
