It took me one and a half day to walk towards the village of Oqaatsut. I enjoyed walking alone again. I had a lot to think over and there was even more countryside to explore. It was a perfect opportunity to get my mind cleared and to recharge my batteries.

Oqaatsut used to be one of the most important settlements of the Dutch whalers, my ancestors, during the 16th and 17th century. They boiled the whale oil both on board and ashore. For many years they bartered with the Inuit, trading skins for beads. Now 46 Greenlanders inhabit the village. I think I’ve seen them all.
