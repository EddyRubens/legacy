There are a lot of young dogs as well. Gaba has a litter. I was curious if he planned to keep every one of them. He couldn’t decide about the puppies yet, but said that two of the older dogs would die very soon. He pointed them out. They looked very healthy to me, so I asked for the reason. One of them was already two years old, he told me, and the other one was too small... 

The expression a dog’s life must have its origin in Greenland!

People in Greenland just don’t feel any tenderness for animals. Especially not for dogs. Dogs are there for pulling the sledges and they are certainly no pets. The quality of the breed can save a person's life and that’s why the weakest ones have to be killed. I can understand the need for this, but it was still shocking to come across a dead dog. She was clearly killed with stones and her legs were tied. I think she was pregnant. Nature here is violent and so are the people. But why didn’t they remove the corps?
